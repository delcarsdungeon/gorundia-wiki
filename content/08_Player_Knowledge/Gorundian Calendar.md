# Gorundian Calendar

The people of **Gorundia** recognize twelve months divided by four great seasonal festivals.  
Several cultures maintain their own traditional names for the months, but the **Common calendar** is used for trade, law, and most official records.

## Notes

- **Needfest, Growfest, Richfest, and Brewfest** are major seasonal celebrations observed across most cultures.
- The **Common month names** are used in legal documents, guild charters, and royal proclamations.
- The **Elven calendar** reflects natural cycles and flowering patterns.
- The **Nomad calendar** reflects animal totems tied to seasonal migrations.
- **Dwarves** *Among dwarves the year turns not on crops or weather, but on the rhythm of hammer, pick, and vault—forge in winter, delving in summer, and the weighing of gold in autumn.*  Formal dwarven dates are listed first with the name of the king, the year of their reign, and then the common dwarven calendar date.
	- “In winter we feed the forges. In summer we feed the mines.”
