# Forms of Address on Oerth

Below is a list of titles of nobility, religious figures, knights, and respected commoners, along with their **generic forms of address**.

These conventions apply primarily to **Oerth and the Flanaess**. Other regions of the multiverse may use different customs.

The ranks are presented **from highest to lowest**.

These addresses are considered **generic forms**. Many rulers and high officials possess **unique or ceremonial styles of address**. These are usually discovered by:

- Asking attendants or heralds  
- Observing how others address the individual  
- Making a **Knowledge (Nobility & Royalty)** check

Most noble ranks reflect **land and population under their governance**. Knightly ranks are used across the knightly orders of the Flanaess, while the final categories represent **honored commoners**.

# Rank Descriptions

## Knight / Lady

This is the title given to a **newly inducted knight** of an order.

Such knights may command a **troop or platoon** in a standing army but usually have **limited authority** beyond their immediate command.

---

## Elder

An **Elder** is a respected commoner who has achieved prosperity and reputation within their community.

Elders typically:

- Are older members of the community  
- Possess successful trades or businesses  
- Own small parcels of land  
- Command respect among local citizens

The title is largely **reputational** and does not always grant legal authority.

However, exceptions exist. Some towns along the **Wild Coast** are governed by **Councils of Elders** instead of hereditary nobility. This is also common in:

- Frontier towns  
- The cities of the **Yeomanry**  
- Barbarian and nomadic tribal societies

---

## Gentleman / Gentlewoman

This title is given to **prominent non-noble citizens**, usually merchants or guild members.

Gentlemen and Gentlewomen:

- May not own noble land  
- Often wield **significant economic power**  
- Are influential in **guild politics and trade**

Guildmasters and leading merchants are often addressed by this title.

---

## Esquire

An **Esquire** is typically the **heir to a successful business or merchant house**.

This title:

- Indicates the successor to a family enterprise  
- Is primarily a **courtesy title of respect**  
- Carries limited authority until inheritance occurs

However, a capable Esquire may develop a **strong reputation** prior to taking control of the family business.

---

## Yeoman

Yeomen are **successful independent commoners**, usually craftsmen, farmers, or tradesmen.

A Yeoman typically:

- Owns a small parcel of land  
- Maintains a respected trade  
- Trains apprentices and journeymen

In the **Yeomanry**, the title carries much greater prestige. The nation was founded by successful commoners who built its **democratic government**, and thus the title reflects both **economic success and civic standing**.
