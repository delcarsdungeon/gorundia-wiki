# Maesters of Gorundia

> [!important] Summary
> **Maesters** are respected practitioners of arcane magic in Gorundia. While not a formal order, the title functions as a social rank roughly equivalent to knighthood, recognizing wizards whose abilities are valuable enough to place them within noble courts.

> *“If a knight protects a lord with steel, a maester protects him with possibilities.”*  
> — attributed to an early court chronicle of House Ravenster

>[!quote]*“Most court wizards live in silk, drink good wine, and spend their days arguing about spell geometry.  
>But every so often one of them follows the army into the mud, sets half a battlefield on fire, and saves a thousand lives.  
>Problem is… you usually don’t know which kind you’ve got until the arrows start flying.”*  
>— **Sorek Redmarch**

---

## Overview

In Gorundian society, a wizard who demonstrates consistent competence and reliability is often addressed as a **Maester**.

The title is not granted by any central authority. Instead it emerges through **reputation and patronage**, usually after a mage enters the service of a noble house.

A maester typically serves as:

- arcane advisor  
- magical courier  
- ward keeper  
- magical consultant  

The title signals that the wizard is considered **trustworthy enough to operate within noble courts**, where magical mistakes can have catastrophic consequences.

---

## The Role of Court Wizards

Most maesters spend their careers attached to noble households.

The arrangement is mutually beneficial:

- nobles gain magical expertise and prestige  
- wizards gain stable patronage, protection, and resources  

Daily responsibilities may include:

- maintaining magical wards  
- casting or receiving long-distance messages  
- advising on magical matters  
- identifying magical artifacts  

Some maesters also accompany their patrons on campaign.

---

## Reputation Among Soldiers

Court maesters occupy an unusual place in military culture.

Experienced soldiers respect powerful war mages, but they often mock the more comfortable court practitioners.

A common barracks nickname for such figures is **“silk wizards,”** referring to the comfortable lifestyle enjoyed by many court mages compared to the hardships of campaigning.

Nevertheless, when battle turns desperate, even the most cynical soldiers are quick to look for the nearest wizard.

---

## Paths to Becoming a Maester

There is no single route to becoming a maester.

Common paths include:

- apprenticeship under an established wizard  
- study at the [[Allaric Collegium Arcanum]]  
- recruitment by a noble patron  
- recommendation by another respected mage  

Regardless of origin, a wizard generally earns the title **only after demonstrating competence and reliability in the service of a patron.**

---

## Social Standing

Within Gorundian courts, a maester often occupies a status roughly equivalent to a minor knight.

They may:

- dine at noble tables  
- advise their patrons privately  
- maintain personal laboratories or libraries  

However, their authority ultimately derives from **their patron’s favor rather than hereditary rank**.

---

## Known Examples

Several prominent maesters have played important roles in recent events:

- **Count Veymont, Maester [[Vorias Serrin]]** — sometimes nicknamed _“the Counting Wizard.”_ Known for his analytical approach to warfare and politics, Serrin is widely respected for treating magical conflicts as problems of coordination and probability rather than spectacle.
- [[Norwin Stonyeye]] — a practitioner involved in several major incidents in the capital  
- [[Hylvar]] — [[House Ravenster]]'s lead House Mage and battlemage present during the defense of the capital
