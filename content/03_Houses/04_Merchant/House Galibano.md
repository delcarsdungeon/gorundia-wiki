## Overview

House Galibano is one of the most powerful merchant families operating within **County Wyrmlea**, though officially they hold no noble title.

Their influence comes not from land but from **stone, contracts, and leverage**.

Originally immigrant masons, the Galibanos built their fortune organizing quarry logistics and construction across Wyrmlea. Over three generations they transformed from builders into the **central intermediary between the dwarven quarries and the human world of law and politics**.

Today, if a road, bridge, wall, quarry, or fortress is built in Wyrmlea, someone in the Galibano network is being paid.

---

## Origins

The house was founded by **Marco Galibano**, a master mason who arrived in Gorundia around **1160 OR** with a small company of stoneworkers.

At the time Wyrmlea’s quarries were expanding rapidly, but the region lacked a coordinated system for transporting and selling the stone.

Marco recognized the opportunity.

Within two decades he had secured contracts overseeing quarry infrastructure, transport roads, and fortified stone depots.

These depots became the foundation of the family’s wealth.

Over the following generations the Galibanos absorbed smaller guilds, purchased failing operations through debt, and built a **construction syndicate that effectively controls the movement of Wyrmlean stone**.

## Leadership

### Head of House

**[[Marco Galibano]] (the Younger)**  
Grandson of the founder and current master of House Galibano.

Marco remains the undisputed head of the family and has ruled the house’s affairs for many years, overseeing its transformation from a powerful construction consortium into the dominant intermediary between the dwarven quarries of Wyrmlea and the wider markets of Gorundia.

His eldest son **[[Salvatore Galibano]]** had long been groomed as his successor and heir apparent.

Salvatore was killed during the **dragon catastrophe in the capital**, the same chain of events that followed the infamous **duel between Sir Beringer Amadis and Duke Wilhelm Vogel**.

The Crown has since declared that **Countess [[Zara Vallytrex]] also died in that disaster**, though the speed of that declaration has raised quiet questions.

Salvatore’s death shattered the house’s carefully prepared succession.

Marco therefore retains full authority over the family.

## The Dwarven Alliance

The true foundation of Galibano power is their relationship with the **dwarves of Wyrmlea**.

The dwarves mine the stone and shape it with unmatched skill, but they have little interest in navigating the labyrinth of human law, taxes, and politics.

House Galibano fills that role.

They manage:

- trade contracts  
- quarry charters  
- construction rights  
- export logistics  
- negotiations with human authorities

This arrangement allows the dwarves to focus on **craft**, while the Galibanos handle the messy world of **paper, law, and noble politics**.

## Craftsmen Before Merchants

Unlike many merchant dynasties, the Galibanos maintain a strict tradition:

Every named member of the family begins their life in the **quarries**.

Their progression follows a familiar path:

1. Quarry labor  
2. Stone cutting  
3. Masonry and shaping  
4. Contract and trade management

Many Galibanos become competent sculptors and masons in their own right.

They do not equal dwarven masters, but the dwarves respect that they have **worked the stone with their own hands**.

As one dwarf foreman once said:

> “A man who has never cut stone cannot be trusted to sell it.”

## The Bard’s Gate Venture

Marco the Younger’s youngest son **Nikko Galibano** was given responsibility for expanding the family’s influence into **Bard’s Gate**.

The plan was to establish control over construction contracts, warehouses, and river infrastructure.

Instead, Nikko collided with the city’s entrenched power networks.

Resistance formed around several local figures:

- **Thalrik**, whose connections to the **Dregs** controlled much of the city’s labor pool  
- **Guaco**, whose criminal syndicate was deeply entrenched and loyal  
- **Norwin** and **Rykeena**, who backed the effort to resist Nikko’s expansion

Together they cut off labor sources, disrupted shipments, and exposed enough of his operations to make his position untenable.

Rather than escalate the conflict, Marco recalled Nikko and negotiated peace.

The resulting arrangement became an **unspoken territorial understanding**:

- The Galibanos do not attempt to dominate Bard’s Gate’s construction rackets.  
- Bard’s Gate interests do not interfere with Galibano operations in Wyrmlea.

In Bard’s Gate the story is remembered simply:

Nikko Galibano came to claim the city.  
The city sent him home.

## A House Between Two Thrones

For decades the Galibanos operated under a stable arrangement with **Countess [[Zara Vallytrex]]**.

Zara allowed the family to run the quarry logistics and contracts that the dwarves preferred not to manage.

In return the wealth of Wyrmlea flowed smoothly through her county.

More importantly, Zara served as a **buffer between Wyrmlea and the ambitions of Duke [[Wilhelm Vogel]]**.

That balance has now collapsed.

With both **Salvatore** and **Zara** gone, two layers of leadership may change at once:

- The ruler of **Wyrmlea**
- The authority of **House Vogel**

For a house built on stability and long-standing contracts, this uncertainty is deeply dangerous.

## Stonewake Hall

**Seat of House Galibano**

Stonewake Hall stands on a rise overlooking the primary quarry roads outside **Cindar’s Wake**.

Unlike most noble estates, it was **built from the ground up by the family themselves**.

The structure is half fortress, half guildhall.

Massive blocks of dark Wyrmlean basalt form its walls, each face polished with the quiet pride of master masons. Decorative carvings show scenes of quarry work, stone hauling, and dwarven-style reliefs depicting tools rather than warriors.

Visitors often notice three unusual features.

**The Quarry Gate**  
The front gate is a massive stone arch wide enough for six quarry wagons to pass abreast. It is deliberately oversized, symbolizing that the house serves **trade before nobility**.

**The Contract Chamber**  
A circular hall lined with stone benches where contracts are negotiated. No chairs. Everyone sits on the same level. The dwarves insisted on this tradition during the earliest negotiations.

**The Founder’s Stone**  
A rough, unfinished block of granite in the central courtyard. Tradition says Marco the Founder personally cut it from the first quarry he secured. Every Galibano heir must carve a mark into the stone upon entering adulthood.

The courtyard floor around it is covered in years of chisel marks.

## Galibano Contract Law

House Galibano operates under a set of internal rules known as **The Stone Articles**.

These rules govern family operations, trade agreements, and internal discipline.

They are not written in any public charter.

They exist as **memorized tradition passed between senior members of the house**.

Common principles include:

**Stone Before Paper**  
A contract sealed with labor or stonework is considered stronger than a written agreement.

**Blood Does Not Cancel Debt**  
Family members are protected — but obligations to the house must still be repaid.

**Craft Before Rank**  
Every Galibano must begin in the quarries before handling trade or contracts.

**The Quiet Ledger**  
Certain debts are recorded in a secondary set of accounts known only to the inner family.

These are not always financial.

Sometimes they record favors.

Sometimes leverage.

Sometimes names.

## What Marco Galibano Fears

Marco’s system works because it sits **between powers**.

The dwarves mine the stone.  
The human nobles control the land.

House Galibano connects the two.

Marco’s greatest fear is that a new ruler—count or duke—might decide the Galibanos are **no longer necessary**.

A noble who imposes direct control over the quarries could attempt to replace Galibano contracts with ducal officers and tax collectors.

Marco knows that would not work.

Quarries stop producing when skilled labor disappears.  
Roads fail when supply chains break.  
Stone shipments vanish when the people who move them refuse to move them.

The Galibanos control Wyrmlea not through force, but through **knowledge, labor, and contracts**.

Still, Marco knows that all it takes is one ambitious ruler to believe those things can be replaced.

And if that happens, the quiet merchant house of Cindar’s Wake may remind the realm of something important:

A family born in the quarries  
knows very well how to bury problems in stone.
