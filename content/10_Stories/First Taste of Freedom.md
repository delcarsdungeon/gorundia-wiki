# The First Taste of Freedom

**The First Taste of Freedom**

The clash of steel and the cries of men echoed across the plains of the Khorovisk-Gorundian border. The skirmish was chaotic, a storm of flashing swords, snorting warhorses, and banners snapping in the wind. Among the cacophony, Elizaveta of House Golitsyn clutched the reins of her white mare, her silver hair streaming behind her like a banner of surrender. Her pale blue eyes, bright and unyielding, searched the battlefield for her chance.

It came with the thunder of hooves.

Beringer, squire of House Amadis, charged through the fray like an arrow loosed from a bow. His warhorse, a massive destrier, plowed through the enemy lines, scattering soldiers like autumn leaves. His emerald green eyes burned with determination, and his lean, strong frame moved with practiced precision. He spotted her—an enemy noblewoman clad in gleaming armor, her silver hair a beacon.

He didn’t hesitate.

With a fierce cry, Beringer drove his mount toward her, his lance discarded in favor of speed. Soldiers shouted warnings, but it was too late. He reached out, grabbing her by the waist and pulling her from her saddle in one swift motion. Elizaveta gasped, her breath stolen not by fear but by the sheer force of the moment.

"Hold on!" Beringer shouted, spurring his horse back toward Gorundian lines.

Elizaveta clung to him, the smell of sweat and leather mingling with the copper tang of blood in the air. She risked a glance over her shoulder to see the Dominion soldiers shouting and giving chase. Yet with every stride of the warhorse, the cries grew fainter, and her heartbeat steadied.

Freedom was within reach.

---

By dusk, the small company of Gorundian knights had left the battlefield far behind. The storm rolled in suddenly, a dark curtain blotting out the stars and pelting them with freezing rain. Beringer, leading the group, spotted a faint light in the distance—a small brewery nestled against the edge of a forest.

The weary party took shelter, the warmth of the hearth washing over them as they shed their drenched cloaks and armor. The air inside smelled of malt and wet earth. A single brewer, an old man with a kind face, welcomed them without question and brought forth mugs of ale.

Seated by the fire, Elizaveta and Beringer finally had a moment to breathe.

Her silver hair glistened in the firelight, and her pale skin seemed almost otherworldly. Beringer, unaccustomed to the sight of such beauty, hesitated before speaking. "You fought bravely," he said, his voice low but sincere. "Even for a Dominion noble."

Elizaveta met his gaze, her blue eyes steady. "You’ve granted me what I could not take for myself—freedom. For that, you have my thanks."

Beringer, unsure of what to say, reached for a mug of ale and handed it to her. "Then drink to it. To freedom."

She hesitated only a moment before accepting. The mug was heavy in her hands, the ale dark and frothy. She brought it to her lips, the bitterness mingling with a strange, unfamiliar sweetness. For the first time in her life, she tasted something freely given—no strings, no demands, no fear.

Beringer watched her, a small smile tugging at his lips. “You’re safe now,” he said, his tone soft.

Elizaveta lowered the mug, the faintest hint of a smile ghosting across her face. “It tastes... different,” she murmured.

“What does?”

“Freedom.”

The fire crackled, the storm raging outside, but inside the brewery, the tension eased. For the first time, Elizaveta felt the weight of her old life lifting, the chains of her father's tyranny falling away. And for the first time, Beringer saw not an enemy, but someone just as trapped by circumstance as he had once been by his own doubts.

The two shared a long, quiet moment by the fire, their gazes meeting in understanding. Though they came from different worlds, the storm outside seemed to wash away the lines that had divided them.

That night, the taste of ale would forever bind them—not as enemies, but as two souls finding their place in an uncertain world.
