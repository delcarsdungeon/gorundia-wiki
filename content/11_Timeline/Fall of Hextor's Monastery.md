# The Fall of Hextor’s Monastery

In the weeks following [[The Bloody-Handed Deception]], the party tracked a network of Hextorite cultists to a fortified monastery hidden within a volcanic caldera. The complex served as both temple and military outpost for the Iron Tyrant’s followers.

At the monastery’s heart ruled the **Legate-Patriarch of the Iron Hierarchy**, a senior war-priest and paladin of Hextor who commanded the garrison and oversaw the cult’s operations in the region.

The party stormed the monastery, fighting through its defenders and ultimately slaying the Legate-Patriarch in the sanctuary itself. The destruction of the monastery disrupted the cult’s command structure and scattered its surviving soldiers across the countryside.

---

## Escape Through the Stone Roads

During the assault, the party discovered and rescued a captured **dwarven Tomb-Sealer**, one of the secretive custodians of dwarven burial rites and under-tunnel networks.

In gratitude, the Tomb-Sealer led the party through a series of ancient subterranean passages, allowing them to escape the monastery before the remaining Hextorite forces could regroup.

These hidden roads eventually brought them to the sealed dwarven hold of **[[Dern Kaldur]]**.

---

## Arrival at Dern Kaldur

Dern Kaldur had remained largely isolated from the surface world since the **Clay Uprising**, sealing its gates and limiting outside contact for decades.

The party’s arrival caused considerable interest among the dwarves. Their role in destroying the Hextorite monastery and rescuing the Tomb-Sealer earned them an audience within the hold.

While in Dern Kaldur, the party met **[[Betram Moffet]]**, who would join their company shortly thereafter. The hold is also the homeland of **[[Karnath Warclang]]**, though he would not enter the campaign until much later.

The dwarves of Dern Kaldur expressed cautious interest in reopening **formal relations with Gorundia**, suggesting that the long isolation following the uprising might finally be ending.

---

## The Naming of Dwarf-Friends

In recognition of their deeds, the party was presented with finely crafted **dwarven shortswords** and formally named **Dwarf-Friends of Dern Kaldur**, a rare honor among the holdfolk.

The moment was nearly undermined soon after when several members of the party sold their gifted blades upon returning to civilization.

When **[[Guaco]]** realized the cultural insult this might represent, he hastily scrambled to reacquire the swords before word of the slight could reach the dwarves.

---

## Whispered Aftermath

Later speculation suggested the monastery may have been part of a larger intelligence struggle.

One theory holds that a rival spymaster had been monitoring the Hextorite cultists operating out of the caldera. By destroying the monastery, the party may have inadvertently disrupted that covert operation.

If true, the subsequent attempt to frame **[[Orin Kharne]]** for the murder of **[[Elyndra Sylvanranth]]** may have been an act of retaliation — using Orin as a convenient scapegoat to strike indirectly at **[[Theron Sylvanranth]]**.

The truth of this connection remains uncertain.
