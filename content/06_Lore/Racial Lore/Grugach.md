# Grugach Cultural Taboos

The [[Grugach]] elves maintain strict cultural and spiritual traditions tied to the natural world. Certain actions are considered grave taboos within their society, as they disrupt the balance between the people, the land, and the spirits that dwell within it.

---

## Disturbing Sacred Sites

Certain places in the wilderness are considered spiritually significant to the Grugach, including:

- Ancient groves
- Sacred pools
- Hidden glens
- Spirit-marked stones or trees

Disturbing, exploiting, or desecrating such sites is a **serious taboo**, as it disrupts the bond between the elves and the spirits of the land.

---

## Wasting Natural Resources

The Grugach believe strongly in maintaining the **balance of nature**.

Taboos include:

- Killing animals without need
- Harvesting more resources than required
- Careless destruction of forests or waterways

Strict cultural guidelines govern hunting, gathering, and the use of natural materials to ensure **sustainability and harmony with the land**.

---

## Revealing Hidden Communities

The Grugach are deeply secretive and wary of outsiders.

Revealing:

- The location of hidden settlements
- Sacred paths or sanctuaries
- Defensive or magical protections

to non-elves is considered a **grave betrayal**, as it could bring exploitation, invasion, or desecration by those who do not respect the forest.

---

## Breaking Bonds of Kinship

Grugach society is tightly bound by **family and clan loyalty**.

Severe taboos include:

- Betraying one's kin
- Harming another elf of the tribe
- Breaking sworn oaths within the clan

Such acts are believed to weaken the spiritual unity that sustains the tribe.

---

## Using Unnatural Magic

The Grugach revere the natural cycle of life and death.

Magic considered taboo includes:

- Necromancy
- Magic that corrupts or blights the land
- Rituals that bind or enslave spirits of nature

Such practices are seen as violations of the sacred balance maintained by the forest.

---

## Abandoning the Forest

Grugach identity is inseparable from the wilderness.

Choosing to abandon the forest to live permanently among cities or foreign cultures may be viewed as a **cultural betrayal**, as it severs one's connection to ancestral traditions and the spirits of the land.

Some tribes consider such individuals **lost to the forest**, no longer truly Grugach.
