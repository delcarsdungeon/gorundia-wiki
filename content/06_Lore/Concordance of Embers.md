# The Concordance of Embers
*(Commonly called Embermeet)*

Once a year, at dusk, the capital marks the **Concordance of Embers** — a sanctioned observance acknowledging bonds, intentions, and loyalties not yet sworn.

It is **not a marriage rite**.  
It is **not binding**.

It is, however, **very visible**.

---

## What’s Different Tonight

- Braziers and low lanterns line major streets  
- Knights are conspicuously off-duty  
- Music appears without warning  
- People are watching each other more than usual  

A common phrase is heard throughout the city:

> “Walking the embers tonight?”

Meaning: *Are you declaring something—or hoping someone else does?*

---

## What People Do (And Notice)

### The Ember Walk

Two people walk together between lit braziers without speaking.

No vows.  
No priest.  
Just acknowledgement.

Widely understood as **intent, not commitment**.

---

### The Shared Flame

A candle or brand lit from a public fire and carried away.

This means:

> “This bond exists, but remains private.”

Favored by soldiers, nobles, and anyone who does not want an audience.

---

### The Refusal

Declining an offered walk or flame is socially meaningful.

It is **not rude**.

It is **informative**.

People remember.

---

## Social Reality
*(Unspoken but Absolute)*

- Gifts are considered **gauche**  
- Speeches are **fatal**  
- Overconfidence will be **punished**  

This night exposes:

- Who misreads the room  
- Who knows exactly what they’re doing  
- Who is about to be talked about tomorrow

---

## The Halfling Complication

The halfling quarters treat Embermeet as a **week-long affair**.

They:

- Actively intervene  
- Encourage conversation  
- Engineer “chance” meetings  
- Applaud honesty and mock nonsense  

Nobles find this deeply unsettling.

It works anyway.

---

## Why It Matters

The **Triad** tolerates Embermeet because:

- **Pelor** blesses warmth and mercy  
- **Heironeous** respects openly chosen bonds  
- **Pholtus** favors clarity over pretense  

No oaths are sworn.

But many are revealed.

---

## Final Common Saying

> “Embermeet doesn’t make promises.  
> It just stops people from lying.”

And in the capital, that’s dangerous enough.

# The Concordance of Embers Ball
## Order of Presentation

The Concordance is **not a wedding rite**.

It is the public recognition of bonds — sworn, chosen, or newly declared.

The ceremony unfolds in **strict order**.

---

The great hall is lit by two towering **braziers** at the center of the floor.  
Twin staircases descend from opposite balconies.

Men line one stair.  
Women line the other.

When their names are announced, they descend separately, meet between the braziers, and proceed together onto the dance floor.

No vows are spoken.

**The walk is the declaration.**

---

## Procession of the Embers

### ➊ Newly Wed Couples
*The smallest and most honored group.*

Those married since the last Concordance are presented first.

They descend separately, meet between the braziers, and cross together as **husband and wife**.

This is **recognition — not union**.  
The union has already occurred.

Tonight affirms it before the realm.

---

### ➋ Betrothed Couples *(Date Announced)*

Those formally pledged to marry and who have set a date.

They descend separately.  
They meet between the flames.

They pause — briefly — so the hall may witness.

This is **intent with timetable**.

---

### ➌ Declared Courtships
*The largest and most watched group.*

Pairs who have chosen to publicly acknowledge courtship walk the embers together.

No date is required.  
No contract implied.

But once walked, **it is understood**.

---

## Movement of the Floor

After each pair crosses, they flow outward into the dance.

1. **Newlyweds** begin the first formal dance  
2. **Betrothed couples** join next  
3. **Declared courtships** follow  
4. The remainder of the hall joins once the final pair has crossed

By the end, the floor is full — **red gowns and polished steel** in motion beneath emberlight.

---

> [!warning] Important Social Notes
> - **Refusal is permitted — but witnessed.**
> - **Hesitation is remembered.**
> - **Walking twice in the same year is… ambitious.**
> - **Walking with someone new next year is not illegal — but everyone will notice.**

## Opening of the Concordance

This year’s Concordance is being formally opened by the **elven delegation of the Verdant Bench**.

In honor of ancient custom, they will light the **first brazier** and restore the tradition of **Walking the Embers** — a silent procession between flame-lines to signify declared intent.

---

## Sanction of the Triad

The **Triad of Law and Light** stands in sanction, not control.

> [!info] Pelor
> The clergy of Pelor **tend and light the braziers**, ensuring the ceremonial fires burn clean and steady throughout the night.

> [!info] Pholtus
> A Pholtan clerk **records declared pairings for witness only**.

> [!info] Heironeous
> The Chaplain of Heironeous **opens the night with a brief invocation** on honor and declared intent.

No vows are exchanged.  
No priest binds hands.

But **names spoken publicly at Embermeet are remembered.**

---

## The Halfling Stewards

Behind the ceremony, the halflings of **Yondalla’s Hearth** serve as volunteer stewards of the evening.

They:

- Organize processions  
- Smooth introductions  
- Guard against social disaster  
- Ensure that food, wine, and good sense circulate freely  

If the elves give the rite its **roots**, and the Triad gives it **legitimacy**, the halflings make certain it **actually happens**.
