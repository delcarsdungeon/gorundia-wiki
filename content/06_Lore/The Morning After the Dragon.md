## The Morning After

By the next morning, word of what happened in the arena has spread through the capital like wildfire.

Dragons have a way of doing that.

Sir **Beringer Amadis**, Lord Marshal of the realm, discovers a new and entirely unexpected consequence of heroism.

His valet discovers it first.

Shortly after sunrise, a servant from the palace staff arrives carrying a wooden tray stacked with sealed letters.

Ten minutes later another arrives with a basket of flowers.

Twenty minutes later a footman from a noble household appears with a velvet case containing a silk ribbon.

Then another.

Then another.

By mid-morning the hallway outside Beringer’s chambers looks like the receiving dock of a florist.

Bouquets lean against the wall.  
Perfumed letters stack on a table.  
Silk ribbons in a dozen noble colors spill from a basket.

One servant delivers three embroidered gloves.

None of them match.

Another presents a folded veil in pale blue silk.

A lady’s favor.

A third arrives with a small silver locket containing a painted miniature portrait and a note that reads only:

_“For courage such as yours.”_

The palace staff, who have clearly decided this is the most entertaining thing to happen all week, deliver everything with impeccable professionalism.

Beringer’s valet does what any good valet does in a crisis.

He begins writing replies.

One by one.

Perfectly polite.

Perfectly formal.

Perfectly noncommittal.

By noon he has produced a stack of thank-you letters large enough to qualify as a minor archive.

Meanwhile the gifts keep arriving.

Silk ribbons meant to be tied to a lance.  
Handkerchiefs embroidered with tiny crests.  
Prayer medals _“for the protection of so brave a knight.”_  
An armful of roses sent by a baron’s daughter who has never spoken to him.

At one point a young page appears carrying a small crate.

Inside are twelve pairs of ladies’ gloves.

All different.

All clearly chosen with great care.

The page shrugs helplessly.

“My lord… more are coming.”

Somewhere in the stack is also a small sealed envelope.

Anonymous.

From a gentleman.

No explanation is offered.

By early afternoon the valet quietly produces a second basket.

The first one has filled with ribbons.

From somewhere down the corridor a pair of maids are whispering and giggling as they pass.

News travels fast in court.

Apparently standing in front of a dragon and trying to talk it down has consequences.

None of them involve peace and quiet.

---
## A Small Retrieval Attempt

Late morning brings a brief disturbance in the hallway outside [[Beringer]]’s chambers.

[[Kat Kendal]] appears at the far end of the corridor.

She is walking very deliberately in the direction of absolutely nowhere in particular.

Anyone who knows her even slightly would recognize the look on her face: the tight jaw of someone **reconsidering a decision made the night before**.

The white ribbon had seemed like a good idea at the time.

A simple thing.

A quiet gesture.

Nothing dramatic.

Certainly not one of the embroidered silk monstrosities that half the court appears to have sent.

Just a ribbon.

And a note.

Unfortunately the note contains the phrase:

> “the bravest thing I have ever seen.”

Which, upon sober reflection in the morning light, is **mortifying**.

Kat slows as she approaches the barricade of flowers, letters, ribbons, gloves, and devotional tokens stacked outside the Lord Marshal’s door.

Two palace guards stand nearby with the resigned expressions of men who have decided this is **none of their business**.

She scans the pile.

Somewhere in that mountain of silk and perfume is **her ribbon**.

If she can just—

A palace maid passes carrying another basket of flowers.

Two pages arrive with a crate of ribbons.

Someone further down the hall laughs.

Kat takes one careful step closer to the pile.

Then another.

Her hand hovers over a basket of ribbons.

White.

Blue.

Red.

Gods.

There are dozens.

She spots one.

White silk.

Her heart jumps.

She reaches—

“Lady Kendal.”

Kat freezes.

Behind her stands **Alard Fenric**, holding a ledger and quill.

He studies the hallway with the calm focus of a man attempting to bring **military order to romantic chaos**.

“Were you looking for something?”

Kat straightens instantly.

“Just passing through.”

Alard glances at the pile.

Then back to her.

His expression does not change.

“I see.”

He makes a small note in the ledger.

“Your ribbon has already been cataloged.”

Kat’s ears turn red.

Alard turns the page and continues writing.

“The Lord Marshal’s reply will be prepared this evening.”

Kat stares at him for a moment.

Then turns sharply and walks away with the rigid dignity of someone pretending nothing has happened.

Behind her, Alard calmly writes another line in the ledger.

From the pile of ribbons, a single **white silk ribbon** peeks out from the basket.

---
## Alard Fenric Solves a Problem

By mid-afternoon **Alard Fenric** sets down his pen, shakes out his hand, rubs his eyes, and stretches.

Then he walks over to where the petty cash is kept.

He counts it.

There is enough.

Enough to hire **four secretaries for a week.**

He sets two guards at the door and locks it to prevent anyone entering during his absence.

Then he heads for the office of **The Black Stag Lancers**.

He is looking for a man close to retirement.

Someone who can read and write.

Someone who can stand a post.

Someone vigilant, discreet, trustworthy, polite, and capable of discerning threats, dangers, and useful information hidden inside silk ribbons and perfumed letters.

He must also be able to draft an appropriate reply.

After interviewing several men, Alard settles on **Man-at-Arms Harkor Skane**, the company quartermaster.

Alard explains the temporary secretary post.

He offers **double pay** for a week’s probationary period.

Skane accepts.

---

## A Second Recommendation

Alard’s next stop is **House Amadis**.

He asks for a recommendation for a woman capable of performing the same work.

Amadis listens.

Then he recommends **Shieldmaiden Koriel Targost** for a week-long trial.

Targost once served as bodyguard to the young **Sophia**, and attended lessons with the girl during her service.

If she survives a week of noble correspondence, the position may become permanent.

---

## The Hallway

With their aid secured, **Alard, Skane, and Targost** return to Beringer’s quarters.

They make good progress through the palace corridors.

Until they turn the final corner.

Then they stop.

The hallway ahead of them is **completely blocked**.

Flowers.

Letters.

Boxes.

Ribbons.

Gloves.

Veils.

Tokens.

Small gifts.

Notes tied with silk.

The guards have refused to allow the piles to be brought inside the Lord Marshal’s chambers.

So they have simply stacked them in the hall.

Alard looks at the other two.

Then smiles faintly.

“You may begin by sorting through which of these we start with.”

He gestures to the mountain of correspondence.

“I will go and continue where I left off.”

“When you have made your selections, bring them and join me.”

He turns and walks away.

“We have a long night ahead of us.”

Skane and Targost watch him disappear down the corridor.

Skane exhales slowly.

“No wonder he’s paying such a high wage for a week.”

Targost nods, looking at the piles.

“We’ll be lucky to see a bed… never mind the sun.”

And with that, they begin.

## Noblewomen Sending Letters or Favors

**Dame Kat Kendal** – House Kendal  
Daughter of Baron Kendal. Normally composed and practical, Kat impulsively sent a **small white ribbon tied around a folded note** late the night after the arena incident.

By morning she is **absolutely second-guessing the decision**.

The note itself is simple and unpolished compared to the others:

> “My lord —  
> I only wished to say that what you did yesterday was the bravest thing I have ever seen.  
> — Kat Kendal”

**Lady Ysoria Fenmere** – House Fenmere  
A court regular with a sharp wit. Sends a **single red ribbon** and a note that reads:  
_“Should you ever require someone capable of honest conversation at court.”_

**Lady Clarisse Halvern** – House Halvern  
Shy, deeply religious. Sends a **silver prayer medal of Heironeous** “for the protection of so brave a knight.”

**Lady Alinne Voss** – House Voss  
Known flirt. Sends a **pair of embroidered gloves** and a letter that is… enthusiastic.

**Lady Elira Sunhollow** – House Sunhollow  
Merchant-noble family. Sends a **basket of roses and citrus fruit** with a carefully polite letter praising Beringer’s “restraint and wisdom.”

**Lady Tessa Mallov** – House Mallov  
Daughter of Count Mallov. Sends a **lance ribbon in the house colors** and a very formal note clearly written by a tutor.

**Lady Marienne DeSalle** – House DeSalle  
Cousin to Baron Remy DeSalle. Sends a **perfumed handkerchief embroidered with her crest** and absolutely intends the court to notice.

**Lady Virelle Cron** – House Cron of Thunderhold  
A distant cousin of Lynwenden Cron. Sends a **small silver locket** containing a miniature portrait and the message:  
_“For courage such as yours.”_

**Lady Celindra Allaric** – House Allaric  
A young cousin of Duke Eldric Allaric. Sends **three mismatched gloves** with a note apologizing that the proper pair could not be found.

**Lady Roswyn Tharlen** – House Tharlen  
Ambitious minor noble. Sends a **veil of pale blue silk**, a classic courtly favor.

## A Most Inappropriate Correspondence

One delivery arrives in a **small velvet case**, sealed with crimson wax and tied with a ribbon that is far too elegant to be accidental.

Inside is a **silver locket**.

When opened, it reveals a painted miniature portrait of **Dame Saraphina**.

Dame Saraphina is widely regarded as one of the most beautiful women in the capital.

She is also widely regarded as one of the most **dangerously scandalous**.

The portrait shows her reclining against velvet cushions, hair loose over one shoulder, wearing a gown that appears to be **engaged in an active disagreement with basic tailoring**.

It is not technically indecent.

But it is absolutely **not the sort of portrait typically sent to a married Lord Marshal of the realm**.

Folded behind the miniature is a letter written in confident, flowing script.

# **Letter enclosed with the jeweled locket of Dame Saraphina Larkspur**  
(sealed in violet wax with a rather enthusiastic impression of her signet)

>[!note] My dearest **Lord Marshal**,
>You must forgive the boldness of a lady who writes when perhaps she ought only _dream_. But after the spectacle in the arena—and the sight of you riding out beneath the cheers of half the kingdom—I find myself quite unable to do anything so sensible as remain silent.
>
>It would be terribly improper of me to say that you cut a heroic figure.
>
>So I shall say instead that you looked **dangerously magnificent**.
>
>The capital has not spoken of anything else since. Every salon, every gallery, every foolish supper conversation eventually circles back to you. The ladies pretend to discuss politics, but in truth they are discussing **you**—your horse, your cloak, your eyes, your stubborn refusal to look impressed by the attention.
>
>Which of course makes it worse.
>
>I confess I have taken a small liberty. The locket accompanying this letter contains a miniature portrait of myself. I am told the painter captured me _rather well_—though I fear the pose may be considered… adventurous by stricter households.
>
>You must promise not to show it to the priests.
>
>Or at least not to the stern ones.
>
>Think of it as a small token of admiration from a lady who has never entered a tournament, but who recognizes a champion when she sees one.
>
>I know, of course, that you are a married man. The court has reminded me of this fact no fewer than three times since I first mentioned writing to you. One aunt nearly fainted.
>
>Still, admiration is not a crime. Not yet, at least.
>
>And if it were, I suspect half the ladies of the capital would soon find themselves sharing a cell.
>
>Should you ever wish to call upon me, my family estate stands beneath the **Larkspur hills**, and my doors are always open to heroes—particularly those who ride black horses and carry the weight of the realm on their shoulders with such irritating composure.
>
>Until then, you must keep the locket.
>
>If only so you remember that somewhere in this kingdom is a lady who believes the stories about you…
>
>…and enjoys them far more than she should.
>
>With admiration that is surely **entirely respectable**,
>
>**Dame Saraphina Larkspur**

---

> [!important] **Postscript (in a different hand, smaller):**
> 
> If the portrait inside shocks you, Lord Marshal, please remember—
> the painter insisted on _historical accuracy_.
> 
> I merely agreed to sit still.
> 

The letter has clearly been **read several times**.

Not by Beringer.

By the people responsible for processing his correspondence.

---

### Alard Fenric’s Ledger Note

_“Dame Saraphina.”_

A pause.

_“Lord Marshal is married.”_

Another pause.

_“Response must be polite, brief, and incapable of being misinterpreted by anyone with even a modest imagination.”_

A final line is added.

_“Destroy the envelope.”_

---

### Harkor Skane’s Professional Review

Skane opens the locket again.

He studies the portrait carefully.

“For the record,” he says quietly, “this is **extremely inappropriate**.”

He reads the letter again.

“And very well written.”

Koriel Targost clears her throat.

Skane sighs and closes the locket.

“Purely professional assessment.”

---

### Koriel Targost’s Recommendation

Targost folds the letter with deliberate precision.

“If the Lord Marshal opens this,” she says calmly, “half the court will assume he’s accepted an invitation.”

She slides the letter across the table toward Alard.

“And the other half will assume the Lady Marshal intends to kill someone.”

Skane considers this.

“Both outcomes seem plausible.”

Targost gives him a look.

Skane stops talking.

---

### Final Disposition

Alard writes the response himself.

It reads:

> _Dame Saraphina,_
> 
> _The Lord Marshal thanks you for your kind words and generous token._
> 
> _Your encouragement in the defense of the realm is sincerely appreciated._
> 
> _— By the hand of Alard Fenric, in service to the Lord Marshal._

The reply is sealed.

The locket is placed with the rest of the gifts.

Though not before Skane opens it **one last time**.

“Just confirming the evidence,” he mutters.

Targost does not look up.

“Close it.”

Skane closes it.

Immediately.
