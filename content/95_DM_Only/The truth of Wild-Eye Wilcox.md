# GM Truth: Wild-Eye Wilcox

Wilcox is not what the Syndicate thinks he is.

He isn’t just a gutter mage with a drug problem.

He is the **survivor of a failed magical dissection**.

And the part of him that survived **is no longer entirely inside his own body.**

---

# The Real Story

About twenty years ago Wilcox was a **promising gnome mage working under a private research patron**.

Possibly:

• a Dominion researcher  
• a secret Allaric academy project  
• an independent necromancer

The research topic was **divinatory cognition** — essentially:

> _How do wizards perceive magic and future probabilities?_

They attempted to **separate and externalize the portion of a mage’s mind that performs magical intuition.**

It went badly.

Very badly.

Wilcox’s mind **fractured into two pieces**.

---

# The Doll

The porcelain gnome child is not haunted.

It is **the other half of Wilcox’s mind**.

More specifically:

The doll contains the **cognitive structure responsible for magical insight**.

The part of Wilcox that:

• sees spell patterns  
• predicts magical outcomes  
• solves arcane problems  
• understands magical theory

When the experiment failed, that piece of him **anchored itself into the nearest vessel available**.

A porcelain doll.

---

# The Result

Wilcox became two beings.

### Wilcox (the body)

Still capable of casting magic.

But:

• impulsive  
• chemically dependent  
• degraded judgment  
• erratic behavior

His **IQ dropped**, but his **Magery remained intact**.

---

### The Doll

Silent.

Aware.

Intelligent.

Patient.

The doll is the **real wizard**.

It cannot move or speak normally.

But it **guides Wilcox**.

He mutters questions to it because **he is literally asking the smarter half of himself for help.**

---

# Why the Doll Moves

The doll cannot walk.

But it **can manipulate probability**.

Tiny magical pushes.

Fate nudges.

Entropy shifts.

The same divinatory cognition that lets mages anticipate spells can also:

• influence chance  
• bias outcomes  
• alter small events

So when no one is looking:

A table leg shifts.

Someone bumps a shelf.

A vibration moves it.

A rat knocks it.

A vibration moves it.

Small things.

Enough that eventually the doll ends up somewhere else.

---

# The Yellow Eye

Wilcox’s golden eye is the **burned-out remnant of the experiment**.

It functions as a **magical receptor**.

He doesn’t consciously use it well anymore, but it gives him:

• flashes of magical perception  
• instinctive countermeasures  
• subconscious pattern recognition

When he's high on Phoenix Drops it **glows faintly**.

---

# Why Divination Fails Around Him

The doll is constantly performing **predictive interference**.

Any attempt to magically locate or analyze Wilcox is disrupted by the same effect.

Diviners experience:

• contradictory readings  
• scrambled timelines  
• missing information  
• magical noise

Essentially the doll is **constantly solving the problem before the spell finishes casting**.

It is **actively defeating the divination**.

Even if Wilcox himself doesn’t realize it.

---

# Why Theron Couldn't Nail Him

Theron sensed the problem immediately.

But the situation is maddening.

There is **no crime to point at**.

No clear magic residue.

Just wrongness.

Theron probably realized something like:

> “This man is not alone in his own head.”

But without proof, there’s nothing to prosecute.

---

# Why Wilcox Is In Bard's Gate

The original researchers believed Wilcox died in the accident.

He escaped.

Barely lucid.

He wandered.

Eventually the **Lee Business Syndicate found him**.

They kept him because:

• his wards work  
• his concealments work  
• magical detection fails around him

They think they’re employing a crazy gnome wizard.

In reality they’re employing a **living divination anomaly**.

---

# The Scariest Part

The doll **is getting better at influencing events**.

Very slowly.

Very subtly.

Right now it can only move itself occasionally.

But eventually it may learn to:

• manipulate dice rolls  
• cause accidents  
• guide spells  
• alter probabilities

Which means one day the doll may realize something terrifying:

> Wilcox is the **less useful half**.

---

# The Hook (if you ever want it)

One day the doll disappears.

Wilcox is **panicking and incoherent**.

And suddenly the Syndicate’s magical protections **stop working**.

Because the real wizard just left the building.
