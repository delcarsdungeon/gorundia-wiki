## After the Winter Court

_The table was heavy with food and heavier with silence, as Bertram shared a meal with [[Theron Sylvanranth]], [[Cornelia Bordella]], and Baroness [[Braga Kharne]]. The business with the Winter Court was behind them, but its shadow lingered as they reminisced about the farmland battle at the edge of the forest, [[Lothlindor Fields]]

**Braga**: They’re all right, but they’d be doomed if they relied on me to have them go up against something bigger than fucking goblins.  
_She growled, glancing aside, her pride wrestling with shame at how her emotions had flared during the fight and her suspicion of [[Beringer]]._

**Sylvanranth**: (sigh) But how in the nine hells would I manage to get these folks trudging through a forest, head to toe in armor, with horses even? The terrain would make us sitting ducks.  
_His fingers tapped the table as if arranging pieces on an invisible map, already planning, already scheming, excitement flickering in his ancient eyes._

**Cornelia**: …Although an alliance would prove fruitful in the short term, I don’t think it would benefit both parties in the long run. More elves in the fight could send the optics that this is a racial matter, not something for the whole kingdom. And I doubt Lady Nienor would part with her men. It would leave her vulnerable, and she is a lesser lord with limited force, after all.  
_Her tone was clipped, analytical, her hand at her chin as she measured optics against necessity._

_The conversation faltered, the table settling into a tense quiet until Bertram’s voice broke it._

**Bertram**: So—Clive’s becoming a father in a couple of weeks. His kid will probably be the first native of Fort Stormarrow. He’s terrified, but happy beyond belief. Jarod heard his father passed of old age back at the farm; he’s on leave to visit the grave. Asked Lyosha to give him some words at the next mass. His sister says his father’s last words were that he was proud of the man Jarod became.

Anna, one of our archers, told me her daughter’s taken to the bow after watching her train. Poe’s brother Morgan… he’s in a bad way, drinking too much since his wife left, picked a fight in the Gryphon’s Roost. Poe doesn’t know how to help him.

One of the Dragon Guard confided in me—she’s grateful to you, Sylvanranth, for giving her a second chance as a soldier. Said it’s better than the road she was on before. And Rodie, one of the new cavalrymen… during the farmlands battle, it was the first time he saw the Baroness up close. Said she was taller than he imagined… and rather pretty, too. Told him he had a death wish, but reassured him he’ll find his soulmate someday—maybe even prettier than our headstrong Baroness.

**Braga**: Taller? Taller?! Is this kid a godsdamn gnome to think this much of my height?!  
_She slammed her fist into the table, cheeks reddening as she masked her embarrassment with fury._

**Sylvanranth**: Glad to see not all the Dragon Guard resent me, but I fail to see how any of this is relevant, Bertram.  
_His gaze was icy, cutting across the table._

**Cornelia**: Bertram. Who is this Anna?  
_Her eyes narrowed, hand hovering near the cutlery as if she might pluck one of his eyes with it._

_Bertram cleared his throat, suddenly sweating under three sets of stares._

**Bertram**: What I’m trying to say is—it’s easy to lose yourself, whether you’re staring too far inward or outward. These people—our people—confide in me. Their troubles, their joys. Each one with their burdens and their reliefs. And that’s just a handful among the barony.

It reminds me who we’re doing this for. Sometimes I think you put too much weight on your own shoulders, even considering your stations. And—for the record—Anna is just an archer who loves her kids and her husband very much.
