### Vignette: “After the Exit”

_The Griffon's Roost, hours after escaping the chaos of Dungeonland…_

---

The tavern was unseasonably rowdy, smoky with cheer and unhinged laughter—half from relief, half from too many cups raised too quickly. Cornelia leaned back against Bertram’s shoulder, a half-empty wine goblet swinging from one finger like a pendulum of bad decisions.

“They had teacups that bit you,” she said, voice musical with mirth and Merlot. “And a rabbit. With a monocle. Bertram, darling, he judged me. He looked at me like I was the weird one.”

Bertram gave a gentle grunt, like a man weathering a storm of nonsense with grace.

Braga, slumped but regal in a tavern chair too small for her armor-plated pride, was flushed with wine and a curious kind of joy. Dungeonland had left her shaken, yet somehow freer. She waved a cup.

“And the cards! Gods! Don’t forget the cards! They tried to conscript me. Me! I’m a duchess, not a deck!”

Cornelia’s eyes glittered as she leaned toward Bertram again—closer, conspiratorial, though her voice trembled just enough to give her away.

“He’s going to try to drag me out of here, Bertram. My brother. He’ll come with his voice like thunder and orders like I’m still some simpering girl he can boss about.”

Her hand fluttered to his, chaste, light as a vow unspoken.

“And I… I rather like it here.”

A pause. The candlelight caught the curve of her smile—soft, uncertain, and fragile as spun glass.

Then—

“SWORD!”

Everyone jumped.

Blue blinked, wide-eyed, barely upright herself.

“Do you… do you need me to stab someone?”

Braga, standing now and swaying like a defiant statue in a gale, scowled magnificently.

“No, you twit. Give me my sword.”

Blue stumbled to her pack and reverently pulled the blade free, handing it over with a little hiccup of awe and confusion.

Braga held it upright, hand over heart.

“I swear—by this blade. By my title as Baroness of Westhold. By my duty as Warden of the West…”

She turned to Cornelia, voice cutting through the tavern din with clarity born of wine and war.

“That you are under my protection.”

She paused, then grinned crookedly.

“And even more impressively, the protection of my husband. Who is, let’s face it, a mountain.”

Cornelia laughed—soft and warm and shocked by gratitude. She knelt then, swaying, near tears.

“Thank you,” she whispered.

For a moment the room quieted.

Even the dice stopped rolling.

Cornelia rose unsteadily, brushing her skirt with trembling fingers, turning toward the bar with a drunken little spin of joy.

Behind her, a deep voice rumbled from near the doorway.

“Braga.”

Baron **[[Orin Kharne]]** had been standing there long enough to hear the oath.

He looked at his wife.

Then at Cornelia.

Then back at Braga.

A slow nod.

“My wife has spoken.”

He folded his arms.

“That’s settled.”

A pause.

“And if her brother comes to drag her away…”

Another pause, longer this time.

“…he can talk to me about it.”

The room erupted.

Someone cheered. Someone started pounding on the table.

Braga squinted across the room at him, still holding the sword upright.

“Well,” she muttered.

“That’ll do.”
