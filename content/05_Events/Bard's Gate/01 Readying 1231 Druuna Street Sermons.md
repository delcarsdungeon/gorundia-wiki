> [!important] STREET SERMON OF DRUUNA SUNSEEKER  
> _Recorded by Lantern Watch scribes near the Lower Shrine of the Dawn._

![[Druuna Street Preacher.png|320]]

Druuna stands on an overturned crate.

Her robes are as clean as she can get them. They’re still patched.  
There’s dried blood at the hem that didn’t fully wash out.

She holds up a small copper-and-tin sunburst.  
It gleams because she polished it. Again.

She squints at the crowd.

“Alright. Listen a minute. I ain’t good with fancy words.”

A few chuckles.

“I didn’t grow up reading books. I don’t talk like priests.”

She taps the sunburst against her chest.

“But I know this.”

“Pelor is strong.”

She flexes one arm. Wiry muscle, scarred knuckles.

“Strong don’t mean cruel.”

She points at someone in the crowd — a half-orc with a busted lip.

“You think strength means hittin’ first. Hittin’ hardest. Hittin’ back.”

She shakes her head.

“Strength is takin’ a hit… and standin’ up.”

She pauses.

“And not turnin’ into the thing that hit you.”

That one lands quieter.

“Pelor’s light ain’t just for nobles.”

She gestures vaguely toward the richer wards.

“Ain’t just for elves with soft hands.”

She slaps her own chest.

“It’s for us.”

She points around.

“For the ones who work till their backs break.”

“For the ones who get told they’re monsters.”

“For the ones who ain’t wanted unless there’s a cart to pull or a wall to break.”

Her voice grows rougher.

“He sees you.”

Simple. Direct.

“When you carry stone.”

“When you bury your dead.”

“When you don’t swing that fist even though you could.”

She lowers the sunburst slightly.

“Mercy ain’t weakness.”

She looks straight at the biggest bruiser in the group.

“You think it’s weak not to crush somebody smaller?”

She shakes her head.

“That’s power.”

She pounds her chest once.

“Real power is choosin’ not to.”

A pause.

“I get beat.”

Matter-of-fact.

“They tell me don’t come back.”

She shrugs.

“I come back.”

Some nods. They’ve seen it.

“Not ‘cause I like pain.”

A snort.

“I don’t.”

“But light don’t stop shinin’ just because somebody throws mud at it.”

She lifts the sunburst again. It’s simple. It’s dented.

“Pelor’s strong enough for you.”

“And if He’s strong enough for you—”

She looks around slowly.

“—then you’re strong enough not to be what they say you are.”

Silence.

Then, softer:

“If you’re tired… sit by the shrine.”

“If you’re angry… talk to me before you break something.”

“If you’re hungry… we’ll share what we’ve got.”

“And if somebody hits you…”

She exhales slowly.

“Stand up.”

Beat.

“Let ’em see you stand.”
