# The Keeper’s Report

**Location:** Temple of the Dawn — Capital  
**Time:** Late evening, after sunset  
**Participants:**  
- **[[Lyosha Ohotnik]]**, Dawnfather of Pelor  
- **Canoness [[Elidra Vance]]**, Senior Cleric of the Synod

---
## Setting

Late evening.

Oil lamps burn low along the walls.  
The stone of the temple still holds the warmth of the day.

Lyosha stands near a side altar, hands folded behind his back.

He does not turn when he hears footsteps approaching.

---
## The Conversation

“**Dawnfather Ohotnik.**”

Lyosha inclines his head slightly.

“**Canoness.**”

A brief silence passes between them.

“He filed his report.”

Lyosha does not ask which *he*.

He already knows.

“And?”

Canoness Elidra folds her hands into her sleeves.

“He found no deviation.”

Silence settles in the chamber.

Lyosha exhales slowly.

“That is good.”

Elidra studies him carefully.

“He was sent because of the **Ohotnik name**.”

“I am aware.”

Another pause.

“He carries the title **Keeper of the Black Threshold**.”

Lyosha turns at that.

That one lands.

“I know the title,” he says quietly.

“Do you know **why** he bears it?”

Lyosha says nothing.

Elidra continues.

“He served under **Dawn Captain Armand Helior**. You remember the case.”

Lyosha’s jaw tightens slightly.

“Yes.”

“He reported him.”

Lyosha does not move.

Elidra’s voice remains even.

“Helior used a shadow relic to fight vampires. It saved lives. It justified itself.”

“And then?” Lyosha asks.

“And then he stopped waiting for inquiry.”

That alone explains much.

Lyosha closes his eyes briefly.

“He executed a priest on suspicion,” Elidra adds.

A long silence follows.

“The blade was destroyed. Helior was stripped of rank. Solgard testified against him.”

Lyosha’s voice lowers.

“He turned on his own captain.”

“He turned on a man he respected.”

Another silence stretches between them.

Elidra watches him carefully.

“He was sent to Bard’s Gate to determine whether you had crossed the same line.”

Lyosha nods once.

“Reasonable.”

“He did not find that you had.”

Lyosha says nothing.

Elidra’s tone softens — only slightly.

“He respects you.”

That earns a faint, humorless breath from Lyosha.

“He should not.”

“He does.”

She lets that linger.

“He told the Synod that **lineage is context, not guilt**.”

Lyosha turns fully toward her.

“And Druuna?”

Elidra’s lips twitch faintly.

“He recommended **oversight**. Not censure.”

Lyosha nods once.

“That is also reasonable.”

Elidra studies him for another moment.

“He was not sent to condemn you.”

“No,” Lyosha says quietly.

“He was sent to ensure I would condemn myself, if needed.”

Elidra inclines her head.

“That is the office of the **Keeper**.”

Lyosha turns toward the eastern windows.

Beyond them, the sky is still dark. Dawn is hours away.

“He chose correctly,” Lyosha murmurs.

Elidra pauses at the doorway before leaving.

“As did you.”

---
## Closing Note

Lyosha Ohotnik remained standing in the quiet temple long after the Canoness departed.

He did not smile.

But the weight in his shoulders eased—

if only slightly.
