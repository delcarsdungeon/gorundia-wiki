Vogel lies open in the sand.
The clerics had tried.
They knelt. They prayed. They unbuckled the breastplate with shaking hands.
For one thin, desperate breath, there had been hope.
Then the steel came away.
And with it, the truth.
The plate had been the only thing holding him together.
When it lifted free, what remained beneath did not resemble something meant to rise again.
Crushed inward.
Ribs collapsed like splintered beams.
Blood pooling but not flowing.
A man who had refused death long after flesh had agreed to it.
The clerics stepped back.
One whispered a final prayer.
Another simply shook his head.
There is no spell for that.
The breastplate lies in the sand beside him, caved inward like a fallen helm.
Dawn touches the rim of the arena.
You lift a hand to wipe sweat from your brow.
Your arm rises—
Slowly.
Too slowly.
As though the air has thickened into syrup.
The murmur of the crowd stretches thin.
A voice calling for order elongates, distorts—
And then—
Stops.
A knight is frozen mid-kneel.
A squire’s dropped cloth hangs inches above the ground.
Dust motes suspend in the air like trapped stars.
You try to swallow.
The motion feels deliberate. Mechanical.
Your breath leaves your mouth and hangs there, white and unmoving.
Cold settles along your spine.
Not wind.
Settlement.
The arena does not slow.
It congeals.
A raven stands on the arena wall.
You do not hear it land.
It simply is.
Another.
Then another.
Then a multiplication beyond counting.
Black shapes rim the arena, line the rails, dot the banners, fill the sky in patient geometry.
They do not caw.
They do not flap.
They wait.
Time thickens.
Aylassa hangs mid-leap, skirts frozen in flare.
Thranduil’s bowstring hums in arrested tension.
Spells cling to wizard tongues like unborn thoughts.
The ravens turn.
All of them.
Toward Wilhelm Vogel’s corpse.
One hops onto his chest.
Tilts its head.
And pecks.
Not flesh.
Thread.
His skin does not tear.
It loosens.
Like cloth long stitched and now unpicked.
The first seam splits.
Darkness seeps from beneath his skin.
Not blood.
Not shadow.
Something that remembers both.
The flock compresses.
Feathers unravel inward, drawing tight, sinking into him — through him — as though his body were merely scaffolding for something that has been assembling for centuries.
His blood rises.
Not in droplets.
In lines.
Black sigils tracing themselves across the sand.
His ribs arch upward with a wet, articulate patience.
His spine lengthens.
Vertebrae separating like beads pulled on a wire too long.
His mouth opens.
No scream.
No breath.
Inside his throat something pushes outward.
Iron extrudes from bone.
A crown begins in the ruin of his jaw.
His face stretches.
Thins.
For a heartbeat his eyes still exist.
Aware.
Then they sink backward into a depth that has no bottom.
His chest splits.
Not violently.
Cleanly.
Like a door opening into a cathedral of night.
From within him, darkness pours upward.
It carries structure.
Bones form inside it.
Not his.
Hers.
Shoulders narrow.
Arms unfold from pooled absence.
Chains emerge from inside his ribcage, sliding free as though they were always housed there.
Hair spills downward like torn silk soaked in the black between stars.
His flesh peels in ribbons.
Each strip becomes feathers before touching the sand.
The last of his heartbeat stutters—
—and becomes hers.
She steps forward.
Out of him.
Not separate.
Not summoned.
Grown.
The empty armor collapses behind her with a hollow metallic sigh.
Frost spreads from her bare feet.
Across the sand.
Across steel.
Across breath.
Dawn recoils.
Her edges flicker — strained, but fuller than before.
Anchored.
Fed.
She regards the husk without interest.
Then she inhales.
And every mind in the arena feels the intake like pressure behind the eyes.
When she speaks—
It is not sound.
It is intrusion.
Three voices braided into one.
One ancient as burial mounds.
One sharp with judgment.
One vast and patient.
And beneath them—
A whisper that once signed decrees in gold ink.
“I required Vogel blood.”
The words press against the inside of your skull like cold hands.
“You delivered the girl.”
A pause.
Something like displeasure.
But older.
Disappointed inevitability.
“Marielle was… acceptable.”
The air tightens.
Invisible weight descends.
“My son’s blood will suffice.”
The chains at her wrists do not move.
Yet something shifts.
Tightens.
As if distant bindings have slackened.
“I would have preferred yours.”
The arena does not breathe.
Somewhere far above, the ravens rearrange themselves into spirals that hurt the eye to follow.
“Zarathryss Vaelorath…”
And the name is not accusation.
It is ownership.
The name reverberates through bone.
Zara freezes.
Bronze flickers beneath her skin.
Green corrosion streaks through it like rot beneath polished metal.
“Bronze of the Storm-Tide.
Guardian once of ships and crown.
You who drank where no dragon should drink.”
Shadow coils tighten around something unseen.
Not her body.
Her name.
“You fell long before you knelt.”
The chains at the Queen’s wrists tremble.
Frost deepens in the stone.
“You are bound.
By oath in marrow.
By death sealed in my sight.
By the crown of bronze you surrendered.”
The Queen leans forward slightly.
The arena feels smaller.
“Prove your fall.”
The command settles into Zara like molten iron poured into bone.
And in that suspended, terrible stillness—
Her eyes lock on Thalrik.
The composure is gone.
No silk. No calculation. No amused superiority.
Just a woman.
And she is afraid.
Not for herself.
For what is about to happen.
The dread in her face is naked — the look of someone who sees the avalanche already falling and knows she cannot stop it.
The command burns behind her eyes.
Her jaw tightens.
Her throat works once.
Twice.
She is fighting something immense and invisible.
Then she forces her mouth to move.
The sound does not belong in this frozen world.
It cracks the stillness like thin ice splitting under weight.
“Run.”
Ragged.
Audible.
Small — but impossibly loud in the absence of everything else.
The word ripples through the syrup-thick air.
She never breaks eye contact.
Across the arena, Thranduil releases.
The bowstring snaps forward.
The arrow leaves the string.
In a normal world it would be blinding.
Here—
It moves like a thought struggling through grief.
The shaft turns slowly in the suspended air.
Behind it, other elven archers loose in perfect reflex.
A staggered line of arrows follows the first, each impossibly fast by mortal measure—
And agonizingly slow in this distorted moment.
Aylassa completes her backward leap, blades sliding into her hands inch by inch.
Wizards strain, mouths opening around protection spells that crawl toward existence.
The first arrow nears the Raven Queen.
Frost at her feet deepens.
Feathers begin to peel from her shoulders.
Not blown.
Unmade.
Her silhouette destabilizes — edges unraveling into strips of black.
The arrow closes the final inches—
And she is no longer there.
Her form collapses inward into a vortex of ravens.
Chains snapping into shadow.
Crown fragmenting into black shards that dissolve mid-air.
The arrows pass through empty space where she stood.
One slices through a cloud of feathers.
Another buries itself in frost-rimed sand.
The trailing arrows follow suit, hissing through a storm of wings.
The ravens erupt upward in a spiraling column, a cyclone of black tearing the frozen air.
Time shudders.
The Queen’s voice echoes once more, distant now:
“Prove your fall.”
Time snaps.
Sound detonates.
Arrows hammer into sand and stone.
Aylassa lands, blades flashing.
Wards bloom across the bowl in fractured halos of light.
Zara tears outward — bronze and verdigris splitting silk as wings rupture into the air.
The arena shrinks around her.
Elven archers loose again immediately.
Arrows streak for eye ridges, wing joints, the seam beneath the jaw.
They strike—
—and shatter.
Wood explodes into splinters.
Heads glance off scale with angry sparks.
Her hide is monstrous.
Corroded bronze, yes.
But thick.
Ancient.
Lightning begins to gather behind her teeth.
Sapphire Flame is already moving.
Hands weaving geometry in the air, lines of force snapping into place around the royal tier.
Orin sees it.
He knows that spell.
Utter Dome.
A prison of force that will hold against almost anything.
He does not hesitate.
At the same instant the Krozen shoves the Prince backward toward the King with a single armored forearm, Orin locks eyes with him across the chaos.
No words pass between them.
They do not need them.
Two warriors who understand exactly what the next seconds mean.
They nod once.
Agreement.
Then they move.
Orin drives Braga bodily toward the forming sphere, one massive hand at her back.
She starts to protest—
He shoves harder.
She stumbles through the shimmering boundary just as the dome seals with a ringing pulse of force.
The King and Prince vanish behind the barrier with her.
Outside it, the Krozen is already stepping forward.
And Orin is right beside him.
They jump down together from the royal tier into the arena sand.
The landing hits like dropped anvils.
Sand erupts around their boots.
They straighten.
Ahead of them, the dragon turns.
Lightning burns white behind her teeth.
The Krozen grips his two-handed axe and begins to advance.
Orin rolls his shoulders once, rain steaming from his armor in the building charge of power.
They move forward together.
Then the Krozen breaks into a charge.
Orin half a stride behind him.
The axe rises.
The dragon inhales.
The breath erupts.
Not a lance.
A wall.
Verdigris lightning and corrosive storm flood the space between them.
The Krozen vanishes inside it.
Orin throws himself sideways behind the archon’s bulk an instant before the blast expands.
Even so the storm slams into him like a siege engine.
Lightning tears across his armor.
Corrosive rain hammers the sand around him.
He hits the ground hard, rolling through scorched grit as the blast rips past overhead.
For a heartbeat the Krozen is still visible inside the storm.
A silhouette advancing through the blaze.
Armor blackening.
Feathers burning away.
Fur igniting along his shoulders.
He does not slow.
He leans into it.
The axe continues its arc—
—and stops short.
The distance was too great.
The breath too wide.
He never reaches her.
The storm consumes him mid-stride.
A howl tears from the maelstrom.
Not fear.
Not pain.
Fury.
Frustration.
A sound ripped from something celestial that was denied the strike.
The howl echoes off the stone walls of the arena—
Then cuts off.
The breath dissipates.
There is no body.
No smoking corpse.
Only a glassed, blackened furrow carved through the sand where he charged.
The axe head lies half-melted, embedded yards short of her foreclaw.
Orin pushes himself up from the sand, armor smoking, lightning still crawling across his pauldrons.
Behind him the Utter Dome glows softly, the King and Prince watching through its perfect sphere of force.
Zara lifts her head.
A thin line of blood runs from the corner of one eye where an elven arrow struck true.
She did not even notice.
Her attention had been on the one who ran toward her.
Now he is gone.
The arena trembles.
The command still burns.
“Prove your fall.”
And there are so many softer targets.
