## Breakfast with the Duke

One of the duke’s _druzhinnik_ [droo ZHEE nikk] is already present when **Sir Beringer Amadis** enters.

The man stands near the wall, half in shadow—broad as a gatepost, wrapped in battered mail and dark leather hardened by long use. His armor is old but meticulously kept, layered and practical rather than decorative. A heavy helm hides most of his face, its narrow eye slits giving him the look of a man who watches without blinking.

A scarred shield rests close at hand. A quiver of javelins rides his back. A short, brutal sword hangs at his hip, positioned for work rather than ceremony.

He does not move as Beringer approaches.

He does not _need_ to.

The duke plants his boots and lets the room settle around him.

**Duke Orenic** studies **Sir [[Beringer]] Amadis** for a long moment, then exhales through his nose.

**Orenic:** “Before we begin—small apology.”

He spreads his hands, palms up. The gesture is formal. The warmth is real.

**Orenic:** “When I came myself to retrieve my daughter from that… haunted nonsense you people collect yourselves into, I did not give you proper attention.”

He shakes his head, beard shifting with a rueful grin.

**Orenic:** “You were doing everything correctly. Knight to knight. And I…”

A dismissive wave.

**Orenic:** “I became distracted.”

He squints at Beringer, then gives a conspiratorial wink.

**Orenic:** “You had head full of iron bells. Happens. Does not count against you.”

He snorts.

**Orenic:** “Your companion. Bertram.”

The duke’s mouth twists into something between admiration and disbelief.

**Orenic:** “Man has _massive_ balls, coming to capital at all. Knives everywhere. Smiles sharper than daggers. And he walks in anyway, like he is late for church.”

He inclines his head to Beringer—just enough to count.

**Orenic:** “I should have said this to you then. Knights deserve better.”

---

Breakfast is served.

And with it, the duke’s niece.

She steps forward carrying a heavy platter with practiced care. She curtsies—earnest, precise—and nearly dents the floor. Broad shoulders. Thick arms. Hips that look like they could survive a siege engine. She tilts her head coyly; it mostly looks like she’s lining up a tackle.

The duke beams.

**Orenic:** “My niece. **Milena**.”

He gestures proudly, speaking about her as if she were not standing two paces away.

**Orenic:** “She takes after my sister. Her mother.”

A fond snort.

**Orenic:** “Strong woman. Broke man’s nose on wedding night because he deserved it.”

Milena smiles serenely and adjusts the platter.

**Orenic:** “My own daughter?”

He waves vaguely.

**Orenic:** “Little bird. Slips through cracks.”

He thumps the table lightly. It creaks.

**Orenic:** “This one?”

He gestures at Milena.

**Orenic:** “Infrastructure.”

Milena bats her eyelashes. Somewhere, a table trembles.

**Orenic:** “Strong back. Good legs. Can lift sack of grain and husband at same time. If winter bad? She wrestles it. If bandits come? She throws bandits.”

He leans closer to Beringer, voice dropping—not secretive, just practical.

**Orenic:** “I want Bertram in my family.”

A pause. No humor now.

**Orenic:** “Name. Loyalty. Grandchildren who break furniture.”

Then the grin creeps back in.

**Orenic:** “I have not yet told him this.”

A beat.

**Orenic:** “I am telling _you_.”

He shakes his head slowly.

**Orenic:** “Skinny woman. Bordella woman.”

The certainty is absolute.

**Orenic:** “All bones and sharp elbows. What is she—fencing foil with opinions?”

A beat.

**Orenic:** “A man marries Bordella, he marries letters. Letters and sighing and poetry.”

He gestures at Milena, who smiles sweetly and cracks her knuckles by accident.

**Orenic:** “A man marries Orenic, he marries winter, land, and a house that does not break.”

He sits back, satisfied.

**Orenic:** “Marriage is infrastructure.”

One last beat, then a shrug—genuine, almost kind.

**Orenic:** “He can still visit skinny one. I am not monster.”  
  
_The duke gestures once, sharply._

_Orenic does not continue._

### The Duke’s Table

The food is not presented so much as _deployed_.

There is no attempt at pageantry. No silver domes. No delicate portions. This is the sort of meal meant to be eaten quickly, with purpose, and without apology.

- **Black rye bread**, still warm, torn by hand into thick slabs.
- **Rendered fat and salted butter**, streaked with garlic and coarse herbs.
- **Hard, aged cheese**, sharp enough to bite back.
- **Smoked sausage and cured meats**, sliced thick—garlic-heavy, peppered, and unapologetic.
- **Pickled vegetables**—cucumbers, cabbage, and beets—bright and sour against the fat.

At the center of the table sits a single heavy bowl of **hearty stew**, ladled rather than plated. Beef and root vegetables swim in a thick, greasy broth, barley and cabbage filling it out. Steam rises steadily.

To drink, there is **dark small beer** and a bottle of **clear spirits**, poured sparingly and only after plates are filled.

It is campaign food. The kind meant to keep you standing, keep you warm, and keep you alive.

The duke eats first.

The conversation can wait.

_  
  
  
He eats like a man on campaign—quick, practiced, unapologetic—clearing space in his world before filling it again._

_Whatever comes next can wait until the food is gone._

---

The duke turns fully back to **Sir Beringer Amadis**, humor giving way to weight.

**Orenic:** “Now. To you.”

He squints at Beringer like he’s judging a blade still in its scabbard.

**Orenic:** “I will say this honest. I have not seen you fight. Not with _my_ eyes.”

A pause. A grin creeps in.

**Orenic:** “But things, I hear.”

**Orenic:** “I hear you unhorsed Duke Wilhelm Vogel in tournament. Proper lists. Judges, banners, crowds — pretty lie we tell ourselves about fairness.”

He nods once.

**Orenic:** “Story travel fast.”

He leans closer.

**Orenic:** “Then I hear again. Second time. No songs. No audience. But people speak different. Vogel speak different.”

A short, satisfied laugh.

**Orenic:** “Where I am from, man who loses once makes excuses. Man who loses twice makes enemies.”

His teeth show.

**Orenic:** “You have done this to a duke.”

He straightens and snaps his fingers.

**Orenic:** “So. I do not need to see your sword. I see what _follows_ it.”

He gestures without looking.

**Orenic:** “Here **Radomir Stonehand**.”

The druzhinnik inclines his head to Beringer. Not a bow. A measurement.

**Orenic:** “He is not here to admire you. He is here so that when clever people decide to test these rumors, they regret timing.”

Orenic grips Beringer’s shoulder, firm and approving.

**Orenic:** “You carry weight now. People will try to climb you. Others will try to push you off cliff to see if you fall.”

A wolfish grin.

**Orenic:** “I am curious which comes first.”

Orenic’s grip tightens briefly, then releases.

_**Here's where he lets Beringer talk at length, inquires his plans, makes his promises.**_

**Orenic:** “You have squire. Kendal girl.”

He nods once, respectful.

**Orenic:** “Her father is good baron. Cadaras Kendal. I like him. Keeps his word. Keeps his land.”

A pause.

**Orenic:** “Girl is sharp. Brave. Will make someone very proud.”

Then he shrugs, as if this is simply how the world works.

**Orenic:** “But she is noble woman.”

The statement is final, not cruel.

**Orenic:** “Which means she must marry soon. And when she does, she stops standing behind you.”

He taps two fingers against his own shoulder.

**Orenic:** “Where I am from we say—man who walks forward with only one shadow behind him is already halfway dead.”

A wolfish grin.

**Orenic:** “You need more eyes. Not fewer.”

He waves a hand, already turning away.

**Orenic:** “Go. Make stories worse.”

Radomir steps in beside Beringer without ceremony.

_Not a guard._

_A consequence._

The first thing he says—quiet, gravel-dry—is only six words:

**Radomir:** “Stand where I can see you.”

---

Milena follows **Sir Beringer Amadis** to the door.

Her steps are light. Careful. Almost delicate.

At the threshold, she pauses, hands folded properly, posture impeccable.

When she speaks, her voice is warm, clear, and unexpectedly soft—almost musical.

**Milena:** “Sir Beringer?”

She smiles, genuine and untroubled.

**Milena:** “My uncle speaks… enthusiastically.”

A polite inclination of the head.

**Milena:** “I wished to ask—when should I expect Sir Bertram to call upon me?”

No pressure.

No irony.

Just a courteous question, asked as though discussing the weather.

Behind her, a table creaks.
