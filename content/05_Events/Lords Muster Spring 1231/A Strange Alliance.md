## Summary

A quiet but pivotal exchange between [[Sophia]] and [[Milena]], occurring shortly after their private discussion regarding marriage, agency, and political positioning.

The conversation reveals a shared intellectual ground between the two—unexpected, sharp, and immediately consequential.

At its center is a piece of **gnomish literature**, exchanged not as entertainment, but as *recognition*.

This moment marks the beginning of a subtle but significant alliance—one built not on affection or politics, but on **shared cognition and method**.

---

## Full Account

### The Hall

Sophia steps into the side hall and stops there, hands braced on the cool stone for a moment. The noise of the chamber dulls behind her—voices arguing land, dowries, obligations. People deciding things.

She breathes. Once. Again.

Footsteps, unhurried.

Milena doesn’t crowd her. She stops a polite distance away, hands folded, posture relaxed but exact.

“We are to be sisters now,” Milena says.

Not tentative. Not testing. Simply stated.

Sophia turns, heat still in her cheeks. “You know they’re trading you like a horse, right?”

Milena blinks once. Then her head tilts—just slightly. Appraising. Curious.

“Yes,” she says. “That is the theory.”

Sophia frowns. “The *theory*?”

Milena’s mouth curves—not a smile. Recognition.

“They believe I am being placed,” she says. “Aligned. Exchanged.”

A pause.

“I am not.”

Sophia folds her arms. “Milena—”

“I chose him,” Milena says, gently interrupting. “He chose me.”

That lands harder than Sophia expects.

“They pushed,” Milena continues. “We met. We spoke. We laughed. We stopped pretending otherwise.”

Another pause. Short. Precise.

“So. We allow elders their fiction.”

Sophia studies her now, reassessing. “You’re… fine with this.”

“I am *pleased*,” Milena says. “And also realistic.”

She shifts her weight, the movement economical.

“Arranged marriages are not cages,” she adds. “They are frameworks. Inside framework, one may still build.”

Sophia lets out a quiet, humorless breath. “You make it sound like policy.”

Milena nods once. “It is.”

Sophia shakes her head slowly. “They’ll think you’re obedient.”

“They already do,” Milena says. “This comforts them.”

Beat.

“They underestimate cost of that assumption.”

Sophia looks at her sharply now. “You’re not angry.”

“No,” Milena says. “I am *positioned*.”

That earns a short, surprised laugh from Sophia before she can stop herself.

“…Gods. You’re terrifying.”

Milena’s eyes warm, just a fraction. “Yes. I have been told.”

They stand there a moment, the noise of men arguing bleeding back in.

Sophia exhales, steadier now. “You know they’d lose their minds if they realized how much agency you actually have.”

Milena inclines her head. “Correct. So they will not realize.”

A beat. Then, softer—not kinder, but honest.

“You are not wrong to be angry for me,” she adds. “That instinct is… admirable.”

Sophia meets her gaze. There’s something like relief there now. And respect.

“…We’re going to be trouble, aren’t we?”

Milena allows herself a real smile this time. Brief. Sharp.

“In different ways,” she says. “But yes.”

From the chamber, a raised voice—someone calling for Milena.

She turns to go, then stops, glancing back.

“For what it is worth,” she says, “horses are valuable. People fight wars over them.”

Then she’s gone, footsteps measured, unbothered.

Sophia watches her disappear down the hall.

When she turns back toward the chamber, her shoulders are straighter.

---

### The Table

When they return to the table, Sophia is different.

Not lighter. Sharper.

The misery is gone. In its place: calculation.

She catches Milena’s eye and allows a faint smile. Barely there.

Milena sees it. Registers. Accepts.

They sit. The men resume arguing numbers and names, convinced this is where power lives.

Sophia leans slightly toward Milena. Low voice.

“You meant it,” she says. “About frameworks.”

Milena does not look at her. Watches hands. Watches reactions.

“Yes.”

Sophia’s fingers tap once on the table. “You read.”

Not a question.

Milena’s answer is immediate. “Constantly.”

Sophia hesitates, then risks it. “Gnomish romance.”

Now Milena turns her head.

“…Which one.”

Sophia exhales, half amused. “It’s brutal. Bad structure. Footnotes fighting footnotes. There’s a chapter that’s just a legal argument pretending to be a confession of love.”

Milena nods once. Recognition.

“Girl exploits inheritance law,” she says. “Guild arbitration. Three marriage contracts that cannot coexist.”

Sophia freezes. Then stares.

“That’s it. Exactly.”

Milena reaches into her satchel. Produces a slim, absurdly thick book. Margins scarred with notes.

“Read in original,” she says. “Translations weak.”

She turns it so Sophia can see the title, stamped in tight, overworked gnomish script:

***Zrikket-Vossel an’k Tindlefex Vraam***  
*(“The Inadvisable Courtship of Clause Seventeen, Subsection Love”)* 

Sophia squints. “That title should be a crime.”

“Almost was,” Milena says. “Author defended it before guild council. Won.” 

Sophia laughs under her breath. Real this time. “Of course.”

Milena slides the book across. Just enough. No show.

“Keep,” she says. “Ideas useful.”

Sophia takes it carefully. “This might be the least romantic book recommendation I’ve ever gotten.”

Milena shrugs. Small movement.

“Romance is strategy,” she says. “With softer lighting.” 

Sophia looks at the book. Then at Milena. Eyes bright now.

“…We’re going to be trouble.”

Milena nods once.

“Yes.”

Around them, the men keep talking—utterly unaware that the real alliance at the table has already formed.

## The Book

**Zrikket-Vossel an’k Tindlefex Vraam**  
*("The Inadvisable Courtship of Clause Seventeen, Subsection Love")*

A dense and deliberately convoluted work of Teenkdiwar gnomish literature blending legal theory, romantic narrative, and adversarial commentary.

Notable features:
- Contradictory footnotes that actively argue with the text  
- Legal structures used as emotional and narrative drivers  
- Romance framed through **contract law and competing obligations**

## GM Notes

- This is the *real* alliance moment—quiet, invisible, dangerous  
- The book is a symbolic “I see you” between two high-agency characters  
- Neither of them is fooled by the political structure they’re inside  
- Both intend to **use it instead of resisting it**

Future use:
- Gnomish texts as coded strategy manuals  
- Callbacks in dialogue (“Clause Seventeen applies here”)  
- Shared shorthand between Sophia and Milena that others cannot follow
