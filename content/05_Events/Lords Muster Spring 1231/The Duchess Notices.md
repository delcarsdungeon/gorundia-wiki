# The Duchess Notices

*Evening following the trial and sentencing of [[Ponzio Bordella]].*

The palace is finally quiet.

For three days the court has been consumed with the **Bordella crisis**, the trial, the testimony, and the public sentencing.

Now, at last, **Duchess [[Irena Orenic]]** sits alone in the Orenic apartments reviewing invitations, attendance lists, and court memoranda that had piled up during the chaos.

One name appears repeatedly.

Then stops.

Her finger taps the parchment once.

> “Vladek.”

Across the room **[[Vladek Orenic]]** looks up from a glass of wine.

> “Yes.”

She turns the page toward him.

> “Where is Lady [[Calira Meroven]].”

Vladek squints.

A long pause.

> “Meroven…”

Another pause.

Recognition dawns.

> “Ah.”

Irena slowly raises her eyes.

> “You know.”

Vladek nods once.

> “Yes.”

A beat.

> “Explain.”

He considers.

> “Lord Marshal arrive.”

> “Very angry man.”

> “Arrest Bordella brothers.”

A small gesture with the wine glass.

> “Much shouting.”

Irena’s voice becomes quiet.

> “And Lady Meroven.”

Vladek nods again.

> “Also there.”

Silence.

> “You watched the Lord Marshal arrest an Orenic-blood noblewoman.”

> “And said nothing.”

Vladek shrugs slightly.

> “Marshal look busy.”

This does not improve matters.

Irena rises slowly.

> “Where is she.”

Vladek thinks.

> “Probably cell.”

Irena freezes.

Very slowly she turns.

> “You think.”

Vladek spreads his hands.

> “I did not inspect dungeon personally.”

A pause.

Irena walks toward the door.

> “Summon servant.”

> “Find Lady Meroven.”

> “Immediately.”

Vladek tilts his head.

> “If she is in dungeon…?”

Irena stops in the doorway and looks back.

Her tone is perfectly polite.

> “Then the Lord Marshal will have a problem.”

A beat.

> “And so will you.”

She leaves.

The door closes.

Vladek watches it for a moment.

Then drains the wine.

> “Ah.”

A pause.

> “This will be unpleasant.”

## Letter Sent to the Lord Marshal

Later that same evening, after the household servants confirm that **Lady [[Calira Meroven]]** has indeed been confined since the day of the arrests, Duchess **[[Irena Orenic]]** sends a sealed note to **[[Beringer]]**, Lord Marshal of Gorundia.

The handwriting is neat.

The tone is impeccably polite.

And extremely dangerous.

---

**To Lord Marshal [[Beringer]],**

It has come to my attention, somewhat belatedly due to the recent disturbances surrounding the trial of **[[Ponzio Bordella]]**, that **Lady [[Calira Meroven]] of House Meroven** has been held in royal custody since the day of the Bordella arrests.

I presume this occurred during your efforts to secure the situation and prevent further disorder.

However, Lady Meroven is of **Orenic blood**, and her continued confinement appears to have been… overlooked.

I would be grateful if you would kindly clarify the circumstances of her detention, and arrange for her prompt release should no formal charges exist.

As you are no doubt aware, the Meroven family stands under the historical protection of my house.

I am certain this matter is nothing more than an unfortunate administrative oversight during an exceptionally busy week for the court.

I look forward to your reply.

With respect,

**Irena Orenic**  
Duchess of Royonad

---
## Courtly Implication

The letter is outwardly courteous.

But every noble who reads it will understand the message:

**The Duchess has noticed.**

And someone is about to be very carefully embarrassed.
