# The Serrin Conclave

> [!important] Summary
> **The Serrin Conclave** was a private gathering of senior maesters held the night after the dragon attack on the capital. Count Serrin convened the meeting to analyze the magical response to the attack and, quietly, to begin examining the political implications unfolding afterward.

> *“Most men are trying to predict what the baron will do. That is the wrong question.”*  
> — Maester [[Vorias Serrin]], Count Veymont

---

## Overview

On the night following the dragon’s appearance over the capital, **[[Count Serrin]]** invited a small circle of trusted wizards to his residence for what he described as a **“post-incident review.”**

In Gorundia, accomplished wizards are granted the honorific **Maester**, a title culturally treated with the same prestige as knighthood. Most serve noble houses in advisory roles, enjoying comfortable patronage in exchange for magical services and occasional displays of arcane authority.

Present that evening were:

- [[Vorias Serrin]]
- [[Norwin Stonyeye]]
- [[Hylvar]]
- **Maester Belcair Vost**
- **Maester Ilthran Varrow**

The discussion began as a technical examination of the failed magical response to the dragon attack.

It ended as a far more subtle conversation about **politics, power, and the future of House DeSalle.**

---

## Background

The dragon’s sudden appearance above the capital had triggered a chaotic response from dozens of spellcasters, clerics, and battlemages present in the plaza.

While the combined magical response forced the creature to withdraw, the effort had been **poorly coordinated**, revealing weaknesses in the capital’s magical defenses.

Serrin gathered several respected practitioners to analyze the incident:

- to understand **how the magical defense collapsed**
- to determine **what lessons could be learned**
- and, perhaps more importantly, to quietly evaluate **what the chaos revealed about the political players involved**

Wine was served early.

Port replaced it later.

---

## Timeline

**14 Readying, 1231 — Evening**

The maesters gathered around a long oak table in a quiet chamber of Serrin’s residence. A charcoal map of the capital plaza lay across the surface, marked with chalk circles where spells had struck and lines tracing the dragon’s descent.

The review began formally.

Hylvar described the **first coordinated volleys**.

Norwin explained the moment **crowd panic broke casting discipline**.

Maester Belcair Vost outlined the **failure to establish layered suppression fields** before the dragon regained altitude.

Maester Ilthran Varrow described the **breakdown in clerical containment attempts.**

Serrin listened quietly.

Occasionally he asked questions.

“Who saw the summoning matrix?”

“How long between manifestation and the first offensive spell?”

“Which clerics attempted containment?”

At one point Serrin rose and erased half the chalk marks on the map.

He tapped the plaza.

“This,” he said quietly, “is where the fight was decided.”

A pause.

“Not when the dragon arrived.”

Another tap.

“When coordination collapsed.”

The review continued for nearly two hours before the formal analysis slowly softened into speculation, anecdotes, and the dry humor of men who had survived something that could easily have killed them.

The candles burned low.

The port came out.

And only then did Serrin lean back in his chair.

---

## Participants

### Key Figures

- Count [[Vorias Serrin]] — host of the gathering  
- [[Norwin Stonyeye]] — participant and observer  
- [[Hylvar]] — battlemage involved in the initial magical volley  

### Other Maesters

**Maester Belcair Vost**

A meticulous arcane mathematician in service to **House Ketteryn**. Vost specializes in battlefield geometry and magical coordination. Known for his obsession with spell-range calculations and for quietly correcting other mages during combat briefings.

**Maester Ilthran Varrow**

A thin, pale, bonded and church authorized necromancer attached to **House Orenic**.  Varrow maintains an unsettling calm demeanor and tends to analyze magical engagements in terms of *survivability curves* and *casualty probabilities*. Despite the morbid reputation of his discipline, he is widely respected for his battlefield logistics knowledge.

---

## The DeSalle Discussion

As the evening began to wind down, Serrin idly swirled his glass of port.

“Now,” he said mildly, “while I have you all here…”

A brief pause.

“…there is another matter unfolding that I find intellectually interesting.”

His eyes moved briefly to Norwin.

“Remy DeSalle.”

The room quieted.

Serrin continued calmly.

“Most men are trying to predict what the baron will do.”

He took a sip of port.

“That is the wrong question.”

“The baron,” he said evenly, “is already solved.”

A small pause.

“The interesting variable is the son.”

He glanced toward Norwin.

“Ser Jareth.”

Serrin leaned back slightly.

“You see, the boy is… inconvenient to simple predictions.”

A faint smile.

“He is a real knight.”

He let the phrase hang.

“Which is unfortunate for his father.”

Young DeSalle had earned his spurs properly.

Raider hunts along the eastern marches.

Patrols under the Amadis banner.

Serrin tapped a finger against his glass.

“War educates.”

A glance around the table.

“Particularly when one serves under a competent commander.”

A faint emphasis.

“[[Alistair Amadis]].”

He raised one finger.

“His father — loud, ambitious, convinced of his own brilliance.”

A second finger.

“Amadis — quiet, methodical, keeps men alive.”

A shrug.

“A young knight exposed to both does not remain confused for long.”

His tone softened slightly.

“The result is a particular kind of man.”

“A knight who defines himself by **earned honor rather than inheritance.**”

“And once that happens…”

A thin smile appeared.

“…the relationship with the father becomes complicated.”

“Not hatred.”

“That would be simple.”

A brief pause.

“Something worse.”

“Embarrassment.”

---

## The Durathen Factor

“And then,” Serrin added mildly, “there are the **Durathens.**”

A faint trace of amusement crept into his voice.

“They keep their grievances like dwarves keep their Book of Grudges.”

“Carefully recorded.”

“Meticulously balanced.”

“And never forgotten.”

He slowly turned the glass in his fingers.

“The interesting thing about that particular entry…”

A pause.

“…is that the boy has read it.”

A brief glance toward Norwin.

“Years ago.”

“I thought it educational.”

He did not elaborate.

“A knight who has served real campaigns understands such things instinctively.”

“He may never say it aloud.”

“But privately…”

Serrin spoke quietly.

“My father caused that.”

Silence hung over the table.

“So when the news eventually reaches him…”

Serrin shrugged slightly.

“The question will not be how his father died.”

He set the glass down.

“The question will be whether his father was given the chance to refuse.”

“If the answer is yes…”

A small nod.

“…the matter ends.”

---

## Outcome

The meeting concluded without formal resolutions.

However several quiet conclusions were reached among the gathered maesters:

- the magical defense of the capital had failed due to **coordination collapse**
- the dragon attack revealed serious weaknesses in **organized magical command**
- the political situation surrounding **House DeSalle** was likely to resolve itself through generational change

As the gathering broke up, Serrin offered Norwin a final remark.

“Give the Lord Marshal…”

A brief pause.

“…and your other patrons…”

The faintest smile appeared.

“…my regards.”

---

## Current Relevance

The Serrin Conclave marked the first quiet recognition among the capital’s magical elite that **the political balance surrounding House DeSalle had already shifted**.

Serrin’s analysis suggested that the future of the barony would likely be decided **not by Remy DeSalle**, but by his son.
