# WITCHHOLME RECEIVES

_A mini-adventure vignette (social + uncanny), designed to unfold in the background before most PCs realize anything has changed._

---

## 1) Arrival at Witchholme

The front door closes behind them without a sound. Not a click. Not a creak. Just… closed, as if the house finished a sentence.

[Elspeth Ryomar] turns at once—posture snapping upright out of habit—and stops.

**Their cloaks are no longer on their shoulders.**

They hang neatly on the hooks by the door. Brushed. Smoothed. Aligned by height, as if Witchholme understands rank, posture, and presentation.

**Elspeth (quiet):** “All right. That’s worse.”

**[Yelena Vladovna Orenic] (quiet, dignified):** “Zis house has… manners. Old ones.”

**[GM PROMPT]** Ask for any immediate PC reactions: suspicion, curiosity, ward-checks, refusal to proceed, etc.

---

## 2) The House Guides Them

A door opens deeper within Witchholme. Lamplight blooms into being—not sudden, not alarming—warm and deliberate. The air smells of polished wood, old stone, and something like dry herbs.

It feels less like being led… and more like being _received_.

**[GM PROMPT]** If any PC tries to “take point,” Witchholme still frames the route: doors open ahead, hallways subtly encourage the same path. It never blocks—only redirects.

---

## 3) The Parlor Is Already Prepared

The parlor is ready for civilized conversation.

- A fire burning at the exact temperature of hospitality.
- Chairs arranged conversationally, not defensively.
- Porcelain that does _not_ match Witchholme’s usual “found object menace.”

Elspeth frowns. “It’s hosting.”

Yelena inclines her head. “Yes.”

They sit—not because they are told to, but because the chairs are already aligned to them, subtly adjusted for posture and dignity.

---

## 4) Tea Service (The Mannequin Poltergeist)

The parlor door opens again.

A mannequin enters—painted wooden face, glass eyes, dressed in a servant’s vest and pressed apron. It carries a tray with a teapot and cups. It moves with careful precision.

**Elspeth (instantly):** “—No.”

**Yelena (quiet fascination):** “…Fascinating.”

The mannequin pours tea flawlessly. No one asked it to.

**[GM PROMPT]** Let players decide whether to accept tea. If anyone refuses, the mannequin simply waits—patient, formal, mildly judgmental.

---

## 5) Rykeena Stumbles In (PC Beat)

The parlor door creaks. Footsteps shuffle in.

**[[Rykeena]]** appears in the doorway—hair doing what it wants, brain still booting up. She stops cold.

Fire. Tea. Mannequin in an apron. Two impossibly composed young women sitting like they belong here.

**Rykeena (slowly):** “Okay… so either I’m still dreaming, or the house has opinions now.”

The mannequin turns and offers her a cup.

Rykeena accepts it automatically. Muscle memory is undefeated. She sips, pauses, and mutters:

**Rykeena:** “Rude. That’s actually good.”

Only then does she look back at them.

**Yelena** inclines her head with impeccable courtesy. “Good morning. I hope we are not disturbing you.”

**Rykeena:** “No. I just live here.”

A beat.

**Rykeena (honest):** “Who the hell are you?”

**[GM PROMPT]** Let Rykeena drive the next 30–60 seconds: questions, jokes, suspicion, or calling for another PC.

---

## 6) Elizavetta Arrives (and Yelena Recognizes the Accent)

Footsteps in the hall—confident, composed. **Elizavetta** enters as if Witchholme is a drawing room she has visited before.

She speaks a line in **High Suel** (Dominion court dialect), careful and controlled.

**[GM NOTE]** Elspeth does _not_ speak High Suel. Yelena does. Elizavetta does.

Yelena’s eyes flick to Elizavetta—recognition, not accusation.

**Yelena (High Suel, brief):** “Your accent—western academies, not court primers.”

**Elizavetta (High Suel):** “Yes. My tutor was… particular.”

**Yelena (High Suel):** “A good one. You preserve the vowels.”

Yelena immediately returns to Gorundian, gracious.

**Yelena:** “My apologies. It would be rude to continue where others cannot follow.”

**[GM PROMPT]** Give Elspeth and Rykeena a beat to react to “private language,” even if they can’t parse it.

---

## 7) Sir Beringer Arrives (Very Hung Over)

Heavier footsteps. A man losing a war with his own skull.

**Sir [[Beringer]] Amadis** stumbles into the parlor, clearly and heroically hung over—hair disheveled, armor half-fastened, squinting at the firelight like it personally offended him.

**Elizavetta** lightly steadies him by the elbow—an unthinking familiarity that reads as closeness.

**Elizavetta (low):** “Sit. Before you fall.”

Beringer drops into a chair with a groan.

**Elspeth** sits up straighter at once. She does not understand the Suel, but she understands _tone_ and _history_. Her eyes track the hand on his arm. The easy compliance.

**Elspeth (cool, precise):** “Good morning, Sir Beringer.”

**Beringer (blinking):** “Elspeth? Why is there tea?”

The mannequin offers him a cup. Beringer takes it on reflex.

**[GM PROMPT]** Let Beringer’s player choose how much he notices: the room, the women, the mannequin, the tension.

---

## 8) Yelena’s Curiosity: “The Man Who Tames Magic”

Yelena sets her cup down with deliberate care.

**Yelena (dignified, to the room):** “I have been told zat one of you uses devices to make magic behave.”

She does not name Thalrik, but the bragging hangs in the air anyway.

**Yelena:** “I would very much like to meet zis man.”

**[GM PROMPT]** Who answers? Rykeena? Elspeth? Beringer? Do they call for Guy, or does he appear naturally?

---

## 9) Meet-Cute: Yelena Meets Guy Agnes

**[TRIGGER]** [[Guy Angus|Guy]] enters the parlor (sleepy, mid-thought, or carrying a device). Keep it mundane—Witchholme makes the uncanny feel normal.

Yelena does not rush. She simply watches him walk.

The balance. The economy of motion. The way his weight settles before his foot does.

**Yelena (quiet, analytical):** “You are trained.”

**Guy:** “In… engineering?”

**Yelena:** “In dance.”

She steps a little closer without realizing she has done so—too close for court. Not close enough to retreat gracefully.

Her gaze lingers one second too long.

**Yelena (more formal when flustered):** “My apologies. I did not mean to be rude. You move as if space listens to you.”

**[GM PROMPT]** Let Guy’s player decide how he responds: awkward charm, defensive humor, sudden flirt, or baffled inventor mode.

The mannequin silently refills Guy’s cup. No one asked it to.

---

## 10) Bertram Praise (Classy, Public, Unthreatening to Cornelia)

**[TRIGGER]** Sir [[Bertram Moffet|Bertram]] is present or enters shortly after (news of Cornelia is hot, and Yelena’s interest is genuine, not catty).

Yelena inclines her head properly—no coquettishness, no rivalry.

**Yelena (measured, sincere):**  
“Sir Bertram… your reputation reached Orenic before you did.”  
“It is said you chose constancy over advantage, and love over ease.”  
“In my father’s court, zat is considered a rarer courage zan war.”

She adds, gently, so it cannot be mistaken as an insult to Cornelia:

**Yelena:** “Lady Cornelia is fortunate. So is the realm, to have at least one man who remembers what nobility is for.”

**[GM PROMPT]** Let Bertram’s player react—bashful, proud, deflecting humor, or sincere thanks. This is a social “gift” that can echo later at court.

---

## 11) Closing Beat: Yelena Becomes Taken With Guy

As conversation continues, Yelena’s attention keeps returning to Guy—his hands, his devices, the dancer’s grace he forgets he has.

She remains dignified. Serious. But her curiosity has shifted from academic to personal in a way she does not yet name.

**Yelena (softly, to Guy, a line you can reuse):** “Zis is… unexpected. I would like to see it again.”

Witchholme creaks once—content, approving, like a host pleased the guests are finally interacting properly.

**[END]** The house has seated them. Served them. Introduced them. Now it waits to see what they do with the new tensions it has politely arranged.
