# Thalrik’s Opening Address Before the Court of Grundia

_Admiral Thalrik, Lord Marshal’s Counsel, steps forward beneath the gaze of the Crown._

**“My Lords and Ladies of the Court… Honored Magistrates… loyal citizens of Grundia.”**

“I am Thalrik, Admiral of the Grand Fleet — sworn to defend our waters, our people, and the sanctity of the throne.”

“I stand before you today not to indulge in spectacle…”

---

**“I stand here to enter into record an account of treason so profound that even speaking it aloud feels like summoning a shadow.”**

_He turns slowly toward Duke Wilhelm Vogel._

“Wilhelm Vogel has not merely erred in judgment.”

“He has bartered this nation.”

“Bartered it in ink, in coin… and in blood.”

“I submit before you sealed correspondence, infernal contracts bearing diabolic marks, transactional ledgers, and burial records.”

“These documents prove that Wilhelm Vogel entered into covenant with devils — not metaphorical ones whispered about in taverns…”

**“Literal entities of Hell.”**

“And what was the price of his ambition?”

_He pauses._

**“Virgin souls.”**

“Impoverished young women he courted, married, and then conveniently laid to rest.”

“I present financial transfers executed immediately following their deaths.”

“I present correspondence confirming ‘deliveries fulfilled.’”

“He did not woo wives.”

**“He procured sacrifices.”**

“And when influence alone did not move him close enough to the throne…”

“He drafted contingencies.”

“Plans to unleash demons and devils in violent coups against this very nation — against this Court — against the sovereignty he pretends to serve.”

---

_He steps forward, voice steady._

“As heinous as Wilhelm Vogel is… his antithesis would be a day at The Blood and Sand on God’s Day — where the first drink is on the house and the worst sin committed is overindulging in ale.”

“Compared to that honest debauchery…”

**“He is calculated damnation.”**

“Patriots defend their people.”

“They do not sell daughters of Grundia for infernal leverage.”

“They do not mortgage our future to Hell for a crown.”

---

**“I place these dossiers into the record.”**

“And I ask this Court — not as an Admiral, not as a rival…”

“But as a son of this nation…”

“If a man who bargains with devils, murders his brides for their souls, and prepares demons as instruments of rule is fit to sit upon our throne…”

**“Then perhaps we should replace the crown with a collar.”**

_Long pause._

“Because if Wilhelm Vogel is innocent…”

**“Then Hell itself is owed an apology.”**

---

# The Full Exchange — Blood, Steel, and Succession

Thalrik’s accusation still hangs in the chamber like smoke.

Infernal contracts.  
Virgin souls.  
Devils.  
Coup.

The dossiers sit on the table.

Wilhelm rises.

Measured.

He does not deny yet.

He does not attack.

He reframes.

---

### Step One — Undermine Position

> “Admiral Thalrik stands not as a peer of this realm.”

A glance to Beringer.

> “But as counsel to the Lord Marshal.”

The hall shifts.

> “And the Lord Marshal is Sir Beringer Amadis.”

He lets that settle.

> “If I am removed… he inherits my duchy.”

Murmurs.

> “If I am disgraced… he consolidates Duke and Lord Marshal.”

He looks to the King.

> “And the Crown becomes… advised.”

That word lands carefully.

Now it smells like overreach.

---

### Step Two — Invoke Ordeal

> “My blood has been called infernal.”

He squares his shoulders.

> “I invoke my right of Ordeal.”

The room erupts.

He cuts through it.

> “Not against a naval officer doing his duty.”

His eyes lock on Beringer.

> “But against the man whose ascension this accusation advances.”

Beat.

> “Sir Beringer Amadis.”

Steel without steel.

---

### Step Three — Drop the Blood Bomb

He raises one hand.

The room quiets.

> “There is one more matter this Court should hear before it sanctifies this field.”

He turns slowly.

> “Sir Beringer is my father’s son.”

Gasps.

He does not flinch.

> “Galmyr Vogel fathered him.”

Silence.

> “As he fathered me.”

He looks to Amadis.

No hostility.

Just fact.

> “This is known.”

That line is critical.

He is not exposing scandal.

He is exposing concealment.

> “It was withheld for stability.”

He does not elaborate.

He does not accuse.

He simply continues.

> “We share blood.”

Then:

> “He is a Vogel.”

That is the thunderclap.

---

### Step Four — Frame the Bastardy

Now the edge.

> “But he is a bastard.”

Not spat.

Not cruel.

Stated.

> “He carries Vogel blood without Vogel legitimacy.”

He turns to the benches.

> “If I fall, a Vogel still rules.”

There it is.

The genius of it.

Removing Wilhelm no longer removes Vogel influence.

It elevates a bastard Vogel.

Now the Court must decide:

Is this justice?

Or rearrangement?

---

### Step Five — Make It Divine

He looks back to Beringer.

No hatred.

Just certainty.

> “If this realm wishes to know which son of Galmyr is fit to bear Vogel steel…”

A pause.

> “Then let dawn decide.”

That’s it.

No flourish.

No shouting.

No rage.

---

# Why This Is So Strong

Because:

- Beringer knows.
    
- Amadis knows.
    
- It was kept quiet deliberately.
    
- Wilhelm is not caught lying.
    
- He looks transparent.
    
- He looks unafraid.
    
- He looks legitimate.
    

And most importantly:

He looks like the trueborn son inviting divine judgment.

---

# Clean Legal Structure (No Holes)

Clytus steps forward:

- Dawn.
    
- East field.
    
- Sanctified ground.
    
- Witnessed by priests of Heironeous and Pholtus.
    
- No sorcery unless declared.
    
- Personal combat.
    
- Death or surrender.
    

The King cannot refuse now without appearing to shield Beringer.

So he says:

> “Let the field be prepared.”

And that is that.

---

Braga is furious — this muddies her case.

The King looks ten years older.

---

# The Last Quiet Moment

As proceedings adjourn, Wilhelm and Beringer lock eyes.

Wilhelm inclines his head.

Not mockery.

Recognition.

_Brother._

Then he turns and leaves under guard.

And the entire capital goes feral before sunrise.
