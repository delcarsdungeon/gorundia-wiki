Sorek closes the door behind him and sets his helm on the corner of Beringer’s desk.

“Well,” he says, rolling one shoulder, “that went about like we expected.”

He folds his arms loosely.

“Baron [[Remy DeSalle]] is secured. House arrest, as we discussed. His rented manor in the capital — the one with the balconies and the iron gate. The sort of place a baron leases when he wants the court to believe his lands are richer than they are.”

A faint snort.

“We took twelve. Myself, [[Dana Sylvanranth]], [[Aldos Verran]], Ser [[Edwyn Durathen]] — as we discussed — and eight knights who know the difference between rank and conduct.”

He shifts his weight.

“DeSalle received us in the front hall. Immediate bluster. Denials. Claimed confusion, panic, said he was trying to save lives.”

A small shrug.

“Didn’t last long.”

Sorek gestures vaguely.

“You already know the circumstances. Lady [[Ysabel Thornebridge]]. The shove on the stair. The broken wrist.”

He nods once.

“Healing done by temple clerics. Injury resolved.”

His expression flattens slightly.

“Several witnesses saw it. Minor nobles seated nearby.”

A brief pause.

“And [[Bertram Moffet|Bertram]].”

Sorek’s mouth twitches slightly.

“When the baron began denying it, I told him plainly the witness was the same knight who jumped the rail and ran across the arena at the dragon.”

“That cooled his appetite for argument.”

He straightens a little.

“Before we entered the house, Dana had a mage sweep him for enchanted items.”

“Maester [[Orren Valecourt]]. Wizard in service to Baron [[Aldren Veymar]] of Fenwyck.  She called in a favor.”

“He located the baron’s disappearing act.”

Sorek lifts one eyebrow.

“Ring of invisibility.”

“Confiscated.”

“The baron protested it was a family heirloom.”

Sorek shrugs.

“I told him heirlooms are easier to keep when you’re not pushing ladies down staircases while wearing them.”

He shifts again.

“After that he tried leaning on his rank. Raised voice. Demanded explanations.”

Sorek glances briefly toward the door.

“Durathen was standing there when I informed him that the authority of the Lord Marshal stands above any barony when dishonor before the Crown is involved.”

A faint glint crosses his eyes.

“Ser Edwyn didn’t say a word.”

“But I’ve rarely seen a man so quiet and so angry.”

He spreads his hands slightly.

“Anyway. DeSalle is confined to the manor. Guards posted. No visitors without your leave.”

Sorek leans one hip against the desk.

“I also made sure he understood exactly who was standing against him.”

A brief pause.

“Sir Bertram Moffet.”

Sorek exhales through his nose.

“I expect the baron understands the gravity of the situation now.”

He glances at Beringer.

“Anything else you want done with him tonight?”
