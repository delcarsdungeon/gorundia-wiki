# Royal Proclamation — Elevation of Lady Cornelia Bordella

_The chamber has only just begun to settle after the matter of blood and ordeal. The King rises._

**King Rimmand II speaks.**

“Before this Court proceeds further, there remains a matter of succession within the County of Blackvale.”

_The galleries still. The Bordella delegation goes rigid._

“House Bordella has long served the Crown through grain, levy, and coin. Its stability is not a private concern. It is a matter of national welfare.”

_A measured pause._

“In light of recent events, and under the authority vested in the Crown by charter and law, the title and dignity of the County of Blackvale shall pass to Lady Cornelia Bordella.”

**“Henceforth, she is Countess Cornelia Bordella, rightful holder of Blackvale and its revenues, lands, and obligations.”**

---

_A stir moves through the hall — shock from some benches, satisfaction from others._

The King continues.

“Her conduct has been tested by adversity.”

“Her loyalty to the realm stands unbroken.”

“The Crown recognizes strength where it finds it.”

**“Let no man contest her authority without contesting mine.”**

---

_The herald steps forward._

“Countess Cornelia Bordella of Blackvale.”

_All eyes turn._
