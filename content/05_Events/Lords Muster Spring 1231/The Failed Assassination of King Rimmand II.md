# Failed Assassination of King Rimmand II

The **Failed Assassination of King Rimmand II** was a regicide conspiracy uncovered during the Lord's Muster in the capital.

Although the reigning monarch is known formally as **King Rimmand II**, the conspirators’ ritual targeted the king’s **true birth identity: Priam Ryomar**, whose bloodline connects the royal house to [[House Vogel]].

This connection allowed the conspirators to design a ritual curse that would **travel through the blood of [[Beringer]]**, whose lineage tied the Vogel and Ryomar houses together.

The assassination attempt ultimately failed when the ritual conditions collapsed before execution.

---
## The Conspiracy

Recovered documents suggest the conspirators designed the ritual around three core elements:

- **Bloodline sorcery** exploiting the shared ancestry of the Ryomar and Vogel houses.
- A **name-binding curse** keyed to the king’s true name: *Priam Ryomar*.
- A wizard positioned close to the royal dais to trigger the final binding sequence.

The spell would have struck the king without visible violence, leaving magical traces implicating [[Beringer]].

The result would have appeared to be **regicide committed by the Lord Marshal himself**.

---
## Collapse of the Plot

The conspiracy began to unravel when several elements of the plan were disrupted:

- Magical surveillance within the hall was disabled.
- Infernal summoning contingencies hidden in the hall were destroyed.
- Suspicion regarding ritual preparations spread among the assembled nobles.

By the time the conspirators attempted to initiate the final sequence, the ritual environment had become unstable.

The assassination was **never successfully triggered**, and King Rimmand II survived the Muster unharmed.

---
## Aftermath

The exposure of the conspiracy forced a direct confrontation within the court.

Accusations were laid against [[Wilhelm Vogel]], whose involvement in the plot was brought before the assembled nobility.

Vogel rejected judgment by council and instead **demanded trial by combat**, invoking his rights under noble law.

The challenge was granted.

Wilhelm Vogel was slain in the ensuing duel.

His death effectively ended the regicide conspiracy, though the political shock of the revelation continued to ripple through the kingdom.

---
## Consequences

Although the king survived, the discovery of the conspiracy destabilized the royal court:

- The fall of [[Wilhelm Vogel]] shattered the power of his faction.
- The revelation of the bloodline ritual shook confidence in the security of the crown.
- Questions of loyalty and succession divided several noble houses.

Within hours of the conspiracy’s collapse, an even more catastrophic event occurred.
