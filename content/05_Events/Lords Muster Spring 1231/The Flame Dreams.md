## The Flame’s Daydream

The green-white seam widens.

The fire swells — brighter, hotter, though still contained.

“Forest,” it murmurs.

The word rolls like thunder under leaves.

“Endless root. Endless shade.”

The flame stretches higher, casting vast shadows against the temple walls.

“Do you know what lies beneath old growth?”

The scent of smoke deepens.

Mold.

Wet bark.

Hidden decay.

“Whole centuries of rot unburned.”

The flame flickers — faster now.

Excited.

“What if—”

It flares.

Light washes the chamber in brilliant gold-white.

“What if it all burned?”

Heat ripples outward — not harming, but overwhelming.

Jinwing sees it.

Not as destruction.

As vision.

The Great Forest aflame.

Not screaming.

Glorious.

A horizon of gold.

Old trees collapsing in sparks.

Thick rot vaporized.

Parasites screaming into nothing.

The Flame’s voice rises — almost delighted.

“It would grow back.”

Green-white arcs crackle within the blaze.

“Stronger.”

“Clean.”

“No hidden blight.”

“New saplings unburdened by ancient disease.”

The rival efreeti in the arm shudders faintly — feeding off the vision.

The temple flame ignores it.

“All ash becomes soil.”

The fire pulses brighter.

“Imagine it.”

For a moment, Jinwing feels something dangerous:

Not horror.

Possibility.

Fire is not lying.

Forest _would_ grow back.

Different.

But alive.

The flame softens slightly.

“But you would not allow it.”

A hint of amusement.

“Because you love it.”

The heat presses close again.

“I do not love it.”

A pause.

“I perfect it.”

The green-white seam narrows.

“What will you give, Druid?”

“Something.”

“Or shall I remain hungry?”
