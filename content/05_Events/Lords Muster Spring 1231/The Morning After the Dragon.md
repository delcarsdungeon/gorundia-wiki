# The Morning After the Dragon

By the next morning, word of what happened in the arena has spread through the capital like wildfire.

Dragons have a way of doing that.

[[Beringer]], Lord Marshal of the realm, discovers a new and entirely unexpected consequence of heroism.

His valet discovers it first.

Shortly after sunrise, a servant from the palace staff arrives carrying a wooden tray stacked with sealed letters.

Ten minutes later another arrives with a basket of flowers.

Twenty minutes later a footman from a noble household appears with a velvet case containing a silk ribbon.

Then another.

Then another.

By mid-morning the hallway outside Beringer’s chambers looks like the receiving dock of a florist.

Bouquets lean against the wall.  
Perfumed letters stack on a table.  
Silk ribbons in a dozen noble colors spill from a basket.

One servant delivers three embroidered gloves.

None of them match.

Another presents a folded veil in pale blue silk.

A lady’s favor.

A third arrives with a small silver locket containing a painted miniature portrait and a note that reads only:

“For courage such as yours.”

The palace staff — who have clearly decided this is the most entertaining thing to happen all week — deliver everything with impeccable professionalism.

Beringer’s valet does what any good valet does in a crisis.

He begins writing replies.

One by one.

Perfectly polite.  
Perfectly formal.  
Perfectly noncommittal.

By noon he has produced a stack of thank-you letters large enough to qualify as a minor archive.

Meanwhile the gifts keep arriving.

Silk ribbons meant to be tied to a lance.  
Handkerchiefs embroidered with tiny crests.  
Prayer medals “for the protection of so brave a knight.”  
An armful of roses sent by a baron’s daughter who has never spoken to him.

At one point a young page appears carrying a small crate.

Inside are twelve pairs of ladies’ gloves.

All different.  
All clearly chosen with great care.

The page shrugs helplessly.

“My lord… more are coming.”

By early afternoon the valet quietly produces a second basket.

The first one has filled with ribbons.

From somewhere down the corridor a pair of maids whisper and giggle as they pass.

News travels fast in court.

Apparently standing in front of a dragon and trying to talk it down has consequences.

None of them involve peace and quiet.

Oh.

And one of the letters is from an anonymous… gentleman.

## Reinforcements Arrive

By mid-afternoon **[[Alard Fenric]]** sets down his pen.

He shakes out his cramped hand, rubs his eyes, and stretches before walking to where the petty cash is kept.

He counts it carefully.

There is enough.

Enough to hire **four secretaries for a week**.

He sets two guards at the door and locks it before leaving to recruit help.

His search leads him to the offices of the **Black Stag Lancers**, where he finds a man close to retirement who can read, write, and keep his mouth shut.

**Man-at-Arms Harkor Skane**, quartermaster.

Alard explains the position.

Temporary secretary.  
Discretion required.  
Double pay for a week’s trial.

Skane considers the offer.

Then accepts.

From there Alard goes to **House Amadis** seeking a second assistant — someone capable, literate, and reliable.

Amadis listens and makes a single recommendation.

**Shieldmaiden Koriel Targost.**

Once the bodyguard of young [[Sophia Amadis]] during her lessons.

Steady. Observant. Quiet.

Exactly the sort of woman who could stand watch over the Lord Marshal’s correspondence.

With aid secured, Alard, Skane, and Targost return to Beringer’s chambers.

They make good time.

Until they turn the last corner.

And stop.

The hallway is blocked solid with notes, flowers, gifts, and ribbons.

The guards have refused to allow any of it into the Lord Marshal’s rooms.

Alard studies the barricade.

Then gives a small smile.

“You may begin by sorting which of these we start with.”

He gestures toward the avalanche of noble affection.

“I will continue where I left off.”

“When you’ve made your selections, bring them and join me.”

“We have a long night ahead of us.”

Alard disappears down the hall.

Skane and Targost watch him go.

Skane exhales slowly.

“No wonder he’s paying double.”

Targost folds her arms and surveys the mountain of ribbons and perfume-sealed letters.

“We’ll be lucky to see a bed.”

And with that—

They begin.
