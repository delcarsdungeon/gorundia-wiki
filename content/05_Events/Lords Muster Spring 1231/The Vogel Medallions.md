*Later delivered by Royal Archivist Norwin Stonyeye:*

11 Readying 1231

> [!important] Ducal Administrative Medallions of House Vogel
> ### Non-Hereditary Offices of Military and Internal Authority
>
> *Extract from the Ducal Register of Appointments*
>
> Within the Duchy of Gravendar under Duke Wilhelm Vogel, numerous offices of authority exist that are **not tied to hereditary landholding**. These posts are granted directly by the Duke and may be revoked at his pleasure. They exist to administer the duchy as a centralized war machine rather than a loose confederation of noble estates.
>
> Authority for these offices is represented physically by **state-minted medallions bearing the Folded Manticore of House Vogel**. Possession of such a medallion signifies the holder acts **in the Duke’s name**, regardless of birth or title.
>
> These medallions are worn over the heart on a heavy chain and must be returned to the ducal chancery upon death, dismissal, or reassignment.
>
> ---
>
> ## The Folded Manticore Medallion
>
> All ducal administrative offices use a single standardized medallion design issued by the ducal mint.
>
> **Form and Construction**
>
> • Palm-sized disc of heavy metal, roughly the thickness of stacked coinage  
> • Worn suspended from a short bar or suspension plate pinned to the chest  
> • Hung by a thick, industrial chain  
> • High-relief image of a **seated manticore with folded wings**
>
> **Heraldic Design**
>
> The creature is depicted in a posture of controlled authority.
>
> • Wings folded tightly along its back  
> • Foreclaws grounded  
> • Tail curved along the lower rim  
> • The human face calm and watchful rather than snarling
>
> This depiction represents **dominion held in reserve rather than violence unleashed**, a symbol favored by Duke Wilhelm to express controlled and centralized power.
>
> **Metal Fields**
>
> | Office Type | Metal Field |
> |---|---|
> | Ducal War Captain | Blackened Iron |
> | Ducal Road Marshal | Bronze |
> | Ducal Castellan | Brushed Silver |
> | Ducal Revenant-Captain | Matte Black Iron |
>
> The suspension bar above the medallion bears the **title mark of the office**, stamped during issuance by the ducal mint.
>
> ---
>
> ## Tier I Offices — Ducal Military Command
>
> ### Ducal War Captain of the Levy
>
> War Captains command the **non-noble heavy infantry forces maintained directly by the ducal treasury**, including garrison companies and field detachments.
>
> While noble levies answer to their lords, War Captains command soldiers whose loyalty is owed **solely to the duke’s authority**.
>
> **Responsibilities**
>
> • Command of ducal infantry companies  
> • Coordination with Iron Covenant detachments when present  
> • Enforcement of ducal military discipline  
> • Tactical command of state-funded troops during campaign
>
> War Captains hold operational authority within Vogel’s military hierarchy but remain subject to revocation at the duke’s pleasure.
>
> ---
>
> ## Tier II Offices — Strategic Control of Movement and Infrastructure
>
> ### Ducal Road Marshal
>
> Road Marshals control the **military infrastructure of the duchy’s road system**.
>
> This office governs checkpoints, patrol posts, toll houses, and road garrisons along the king’s roads and major trade routes.
>
> Because armies move along roads, this office effectively controls **the arteries of the duchy**.
>
> **Responsibilities**
>
> • Command of road garrison detachments  
> • Control of toll authorities and inspection posts  
> • Oversight of military traffic and supply movement  
> • Authorization of troop passage and convoy protection
>
> Through this office the duke can restrict movement of both soldiers and supplies, allowing the administration to **choke or enable military operations across the duchy**.
>
> ---
>
> ## Tier III Offices — Ducal Fortress Administration
>
> ### Ducal Castellan (Appointed Fort Warden)
>
> The office of Castellan governs fortifications that are **not hereditary noble holdings**, including ducal forts, fortified supply depots, and garrison keeps.
>
> These positions exist alongside the feudal structure but remain **entirely dependent upon ducal appointment**.
>
> **Responsibilities**
>
> • Command of fortress garrisons  
> • Maintenance of defensive structures  
> • Storage and distribution of arms and provisions  
> • Regional military readiness
>
> Across the duchy many such fortifications exist, ranging from major border keeps to fortified manor houses that serve as military strongpoints.
>
> ---
>
> ## Tier IV Offices — Ducal Internal Enforcement
>
> ### Ducal Revenant-Captain
>
> The Revenant-Captains serve as the duke’s internal instruments of discipline and suppression. These officers operate outside the conventional chain of military command and answer directly to the ducal court.
>
> Their duties are rarely recorded in official correspondence.
>
> **Responsibilities**
>
> • Enforcement of ducal loyalty among officers and administrators  
> • Investigation of sedition, corruption, and disobedience  
> • Execution of politically sensitive arrests or removals  
> • Oversight of internal security operations
>
> Within administrative circles the office is often referred to quietly as **the duke’s shadow hand**.
>
> ---
>
> ## Administrative Principle of the System
>
> These offices form the backbone of Duke Wilhelm Vogel’s centralized administration.
>
> Unlike hereditary nobility, whose authority derives from land and lineage, these positions exist entirely by **appointment and revocation**.
>
> The medallions themselves therefore represent more than rank.
>
> They represent **the machinery of ducal control**.
>
> Remove the officers, and the machinery stops.
