### The Royal Breakfast Before the Muster

The summons comes early.

Not trumpet-early. Not alarm-early.

Ceremonial early.

You’re escorted through polished corridors that smell faintly of citrus oil and hearthsmoke until you’re shown into a long, well-lit chamber overlooking the inner gardens. The table is already set.

And by the gods.

It’s magnificent.

Silver chafing trays. Porcelain imported from three kingdoms. Linen white as fresh snow.

And the food.

Soft-yolk eggs in every conceivable preparation — poached, baked in herbs, nestled into toast rounds. Biscuits stacked high, steaming and split. Sausages glazed with honey and pepper. Bowls of jam in a rainbow spread across the table: blackberry, apricot, plum, strawberry, marmalade thick with rind.

And gravy.

So much gravy.

Thick, rich, gleaming gravy in silver boats positioned strategically — temptingly — near every setting.

There is not a single safe thing on the table.

No dry toast.  
No hard eggs.  
No neat fruit slices.

Everything drips. Smears. Breaks.

The halfling staff move with polite efficiency, refilling cups and plates with serene innocence.

---

Sorek pauses beside Beringer as they take their seats.

He eyes the table.

Then the halflings.

Then the table again.

He leans slightly closer, voice low.

> “It’s a game.”

He picks up a biscuit, studies it like a tactical map.

> “Culturally, they’ve got a thing about people who take themselves too seriously. So they serve a breakfast that punishes vanity.”

He gestures subtly at a duke across the table attempting to pierce an egg without catastrophe.

The yolk explodes.

A bright golden comet streaks across a velvet sleeve.

Sorek doesn’t look. He just nods once.

> “That’s a win for them.”

A sip of coffee.

> “Pretty sure they bet on it.”

Across the room, one of the Hearthguard halflings watches with the satisfied serenity of a battlefield commander whose artillery is performing beautifully.

---

As Sorek reaches for jam, his posture changes.

Just a little.

His shoulders square. His jaw tightens.

At the far end of the table sit the Duke and Duchess of Orenic.

The Duke notices Sorek first.

His expression darkens instantly — a storm gathering without warning. He murmurs something to his wife and begins guiding her subtly away from that side of the table.

But she sees him.

The Duchess of Orenic smiles.

Not broadly.

Not politely.

A slow, knowing, almost feline smile.

She lifts her fingers in a delicate little wave.

The Duke sees that too.

His glower deepens. He leans in, speaking firmly to her, and steers her further down the line.

She glances back over her shoulder.

And waves again.

Sorek exhales through his nose.

If Beringer looks at him with that what in the hell was that expression, Sorek answers without looking away from his plate.

> “The Duke’s the storm.”

He cuts a biscuit carefully. Tactical precision.

> “She’s the lynx.”

Finally, he meets Beringer’s eyes.

> “At rest? Regal housecat.”

A pause.

> “When she’s angry or hungry?”

He tears the biscuit in half.

> “Claws.”

If Beringer presses — gods help him — Sorek’s jaw flexes once.

A flicker of embarrassment, rare and uncomfortable.

He lowers his voice even further.

> “She’s a hellcat in the sack too.”

A beat.

He watches Beringer’s stunned expression with flat, unimpressed patience.

> “Or at least she was.”

Another beat.

> “It was a long time ago.”

He finally looks back toward the Duchess. She’s laughing at something her husband didn’t find amusing.

> “She enjoys ruffling him. Always did.”

He reaches for the jam — plum this time — and spreads it carefully.

> “We were both very young.”

And for a second —

Just a second —

The battlefield-hardened knight, the Old Hound of Stormarrow gets a distant look in his eyes.

Not regret.

Not quite.

Memory.

Then he blinks, and it’s gone.

He lifts his fork.

An egg ruptures.

Golden yolk spills down the front of his tunic.

Sorek stares at it.

Across the room, a halfling makes a quiet tick mark on a parchment.

Sorek sighs.

> “Damn.”

And somewhere in the kitchen, a halfling whispers reverently:

“Bounty and laughter.”
