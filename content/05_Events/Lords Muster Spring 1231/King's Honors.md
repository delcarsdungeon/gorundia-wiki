# The King’s Honors
### Bannerhall of Gorundia — Royal Court Record

At the conclusion of the assembly, **His Majesty Rimmand II, King of Gorundia**, rose before the gathered lords, knights, and envoys of the realm.

The hall fell silent.

A herald stepped forward and unrolled the royal parchment.

---

## Beringer
### Shield of the Crown

The king stepped forward.

“Sir Beringer Amadis.”

He looked across the hall.

“In an age when treachery walks easily and ambition wears a smiling face, a kingdom must rely upon those whose word cannot be bent and whose honor cannot be bought.”

He raised his voice.

“You have stood for crown and realm without hesitation, without compromise, and without fear of consequence.”

The king placed a hand upon his shoulder.

“For your honor, and for the example you set before all who serve this realm, we name you **Shield of the Crown**.”

---

## Sir Bertram Moffet
### Hound of Ironmere

“Sir Bertram Moffet.”

The king regarded him for a long moment.

“There are men who fight bravely for glory… and there are men who fight because they will not abandon those who stand beside them.”

He gestured toward the assembled court.

“Your house has long borne the black hound upon its arms — the symbol of vigilance, loyalty, and the courage that guards hearth and home.”

A faint smile touched the king’s face.

“And like that hound, you have stood your ground when danger came. Not for pride. Not for fame. But because those behind you needed a defender.”

His voice carried clearly through the chamber.

“For courage in battle, for steadfast loyalty to your companions, and for the fierce devotion that does not yield even when death stands near…”

“We proclaim you **Hound of Ironmere**.”

---

## Thalrik Vaultforge
### Warden of the King’s Law

“Thalrik Vaultforge.”

The king inclined his head respectfully.

“Steel defends a kingdom in war. But in peace, it is judgment that holds a realm together.”

He continued.

“Your wisdom in law and counsel has steadied disputes that might otherwise have broken men and cities alike.”

The king lifted his seal ring slightly.

“For your service to justice and to the rule of law within this realm, we name you **Warden of the King’s Law**.”

---

## Lyosha Ohotnik
### Lightbearer of Gorundia

“Lyosha Ohotnik.”

The king inclined his head slightly.

“Faith is easy in sunlight and comfort. It is far rarer when darkness and fear press close.”

He paused.

“Yet you have carried the light of your calling into danger without hesitation, bringing hope where there might otherwise have been despair.”

His voice carried clearly.

“For your faith and your courage, we proclaim you **Lightbearer of Gorundia**.”

---

## Fayge Moonstep
### Voice of the Unbroken Road

“Fayge Moonstep.”

The king’s tone softened slightly.

“Not all who serve the realm do so with blade or shield.”

A few nobles murmured.

“You have walked the long road beside your companions and did not falter when the path grew dark.”

He gestured toward the hall.

“For steadfast loyalty and the strength of spirit that carries others forward, we name you **Voice of the Unbroken Road**.”

---

## Norwin Stoneyeye
### Sage of the Crown

“Norwin Stoneyeye, Royal Archivist.”

The king nodded.

“Knowledge is the quiet pillar upon which kingdoms stand.”

He continued.

“Through wisdom, mastery of the arcane, and faithful stewardship of the realm’s history and secrets, you have served the Crown with rare dedication.”

The king lifted the parchment slightly.

“For your learning and your counsel, we name you **Sage of the Crown**.”

---

## Dame Rykeena
### Bulwark of the Realm

The king’s voice grew firmer.

“Rykeena.”

The hall stilled.

“When terror struck and chaos swept through the field, many thought first of their own lives.”

He looked across the nobles.

“You thought first of others.”

A moment passed.

“You placed yourself between danger and those under your protection, and did so without hesitation.”

The king raised his voice.

“For such courage and duty, we name you **Bulwark of the Realm**.”

---

## Closing of the Honors

The king turned back to the herald.

“Let these honors be entered into the rolls of the Crown.”

The herald bowed.

“So recorded.”

The hall erupted into applause.
