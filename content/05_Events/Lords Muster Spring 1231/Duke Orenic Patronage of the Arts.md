## The Duke’s Commissioned Play

_A courtly scandal, revised by force of personality._

---

**Duke Vladek Orenic** remains in an excellent mood.

He keeps one arm loosely around **Sir Bertram’s** shoulders, the way veterans do when they decide someone is permanently on their side.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“Ah. Zat reminds me.”

He brightens, eyes gleaming with the particular joy of a man about to brag about art.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“I have commissioned a play.”

Elspeth straightens immediately.

**Yelena:** “Father—”

**Duke Vladek (waving her off):**  
“Already staged.”

A beat.

**Duke Vladek:** “Twice.”

---

### Origins of the Work

The Duke speaks as if lecturing a room that should be taking notes.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“Originally, it was very… refined.”  
“Forbidden romance. Young knight. Clever noble lady. Much yearning.”

He makes a vague, dismissive gesture.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“My wife and daughters adored it.”

Yelena inclines her head. “It was elegant.”

**Duke Vladek:**  
“Yes. Elegant.”  
“And very boring.”

He taps Bertram’s chest with one thick finger.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“Zen I hear zat you humbled Ponzio Bordella.”  
“Lost an eye for it.”  
“And somehow improved his posture by removing a leg.”

The Duke’s grin spreads.

**Duke Vladek:** “So I ordered revisions.”

---

### The Revised Title

**Duke Vladek:**  
“We kept it very traditional.”

He makes a circular motion with his hand, as though this is self-explanatory.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“Masked dancers. Courtly forms. Snow.”

He does not elaborate.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“The piece is now titled _The White Thorn and the Broken Crown_.”

Witchholme creaks softly, approving.

---

### The New Opening Scene

The Duke’s eyes sparkle as he recounts it.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“Ah—ze opening.”

He spreads his hands.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“Ze rival lord is introduced first. New name, of course.”

He smiles thinly.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“He enters on a leash.”

A pause for effect.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“Led by ze dark patron.”

He does not say Vogel’s name.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“Ze patron addresses the court and gestures to him.”

The Duke lowers his voice, quoting with relish.

**Duke Vladek (quoting the play):**  
“_This is my hunting dog._  
_He bites when I point._  
_He whines when I do not._”

The rival lord struggles. The leash tightens. The audience laughs uneasily.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“Very clear.”

---

### The Climax

The Duke warms to the memory.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“Ze knight confronts them at the masquerade.”  
“Ballet, of course. All movement.”

He makes a precise chopping gesture.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“Ze hunting dog gives a long speech about order, obedience, destiny.”

He makes a choking sound of mockery.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“Zen—_down_.”

**Duke Vladek:**  
“Leg severed on stage. Illusion magic. Red silk against white snow.”

He touches just beneath his own eye.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“The knight wins—but pays for it. Blinded in the final movement.”

A beat.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“Standing ovation.”

He chuckles.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“Someone threw a boot at ze man playing the patron.”

---

### Entirely Fictional

The Duke’s smile turns sly.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“Naturally, all names are changed.”  
“All events are coincidence.”

He winks at the table.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“But people say ze story feels… familiar.”

He squeezes Bertram’s shoulder once more.

**Duke Vladek:**  
“Art should reflect truth.”  
“And truth, my friends, is far more interesting when it dances.”

Witchholme creaks softly, like a theater settling after applause.
