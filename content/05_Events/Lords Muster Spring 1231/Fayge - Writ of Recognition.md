> [!important] ✦ Royal Writ of Recognition ✦
> **Issued Under the Seal of the Crown of Gorundia**
>
> By the hand of **Prince Porthos Ryomar**,  
> Son of **King Rimmand II**,  
> Defender of the Realm and Heir to the Crown.
>
> ---
>
> Let it be known:
>
> That **High Lyric Fayge**, whose voice has stirred hall and hearth alike, is hereby recognized for excellence in the noble art of song.
>
> In Bannerhall, before sworn knights and gathered lords, her performance was judged not merely pleasing, but **commanding** — her verse shaping memory, and her memory shaping loyalty.
>
> ---
>
> ### I — Laurel of the Court
> A circlet of worked gold, fashioned in the likeness of **laurel leaves**, is granted in token of artistic triumph.
>
> This laurel may be worn at **court, feast, and formal assembly**, signifying royal recognition of her craft.
>
> ---
>
> ### II — Writ of Performance
> High Lyric Fayge is granted the right to **perform within the capital and its royal halls without further petition**.
>
> Within the bounds of performance, **satire and commentary shall not be construed as sedition**, provided they do not call for direct harm against the Crown or its sworn vassals.
>
> While traveling within **Gorundia** for purposes of performance, she shall be considered under **royal patronage**.
>
> ---
>
> ### III — Royal Honorarium
> A purse of **twenty-five gold crowns** accompanies this writ, granted freely in acknowledgment of distinguished artistry.
>
> ---
>
> Let it be recorded that **Gorundia does not fear sharp words, but honors them — so long as they cut clean.**
>
> Sealed and witnessed beneath the eyes of the **Triad**.
>
> *By my hand,*  
> **Porthos Ryomar**  
> Prince of Gorundia
