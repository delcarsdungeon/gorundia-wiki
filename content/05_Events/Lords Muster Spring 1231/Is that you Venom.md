# A Case of Mistaken Identity
## The Outer Courtyard, Morning Before the Lord’s Muster

_Outer courtyard of the palace, morning before the Lord’s Muster._

Banners ripple above the stone courtyard. Knights gather in small knots of conversation while heralds move through the crowd sorting titles and seating.

Near the chapel wall, **Dame Cwenheld** walks slowly along the path.

Her limp is subtle but steady. Her pale, damaged eyes drift across the courtyard, following voices more than faces.

Across the yard stands **Fayge**.

Tall.

Long black hair falling down her back.

Cwenheld stops.

Her expression softens.

She takes a few careful steps closer.

“…You grew it out.”

Fayge turns politely.

“I beg your pardon?”

Cwenheld smiles faintly.

“For decades you kept it cropped like a recruit,” she says warmly. “Said it was your mourning for the fallen kingdoms.”

She studies the long dark hair, seeing only the outline.

“You always said you’d grow it back when the grief stopped.”

A small approving nod.

“Well.”

“About time.”

Fayge hesitates.

“My lady, I think you may—”

“You remember Blackwood.”

Not a question.

“You stood on the shrine wall and sang while I held the gate.”

Her voice softens.

“You never stopped singing. Even when the raiders started climbing.”

Fayge blinks, caught off guard.

Cwenheld chuckles softly.

“You always did have a voice that carried.”

She tilts her head slightly.

“Do you remember Harrow Bend?”

“The shrine road.”

“You took the forward watch because you claimed elves didn’t need sleep.”

Her smile deepens faintly.

“Then the arrows came out of the trees and you were already moving.”

“You always were faster than the rest of us.”

Fayge gives a careful smile.

“I do try to stay quick on my feet.”

Cwenheld nods.

“That you do.”

She studies the silhouette again.

“You always wore your hair short in mourning.”

She gestures faintly toward the hair.

“Said you’d keep it short until the dead were avenged.”

A quiet sigh.

“Well.”

She gestures vaguely toward the palace banners and gathered knights.

“Maybe we’re getting closer.”

Across the courtyard **Dame Venom finally notices the scene**.

She’s been half-listening to a knight ramble about tariffs.

Her attention drifts.

Then she sees **Cwenheld standing in front of Fayge**.

Talking.

Venom freezes.

“…oh hell.”

She breaks away immediately and strides across the courtyard.

---

Cwenheld continues quietly.

“You remember the burned abbey outside Blackwood.”

“We thought the place was haunted.”

A soft laugh escapes her.

“Turned out to be smugglers.”

Fayge tries again.

“My lady, I’m afraid you might be mistaking me for—”

“Shield.”

Venom’s voice cuts gently through the moment.

Cwenheld turns toward the sound.

“…Venom?”

“The one and only.”

Venom steps up beside Fayge, resting a casual hand on the bard’s shoulder.

“Sorry to interrupt.”

Cwenheld smiles.

“I was just telling you about Gray Hollow.”

Venom glances sideways at Fayge.

Then back to Cwenheld.

“Yeah.”

“Cold winter.”

Fayge looks between them.

Venom gives the tiniest shake of her head.

Don’t correct it.

Cwenheld sighs softly.

“Strange thing about sight.”

She gestures lightly toward her eyes.

“Everything blurs together eventually.”

Her cloudy gaze rests on Fayge.

“But the voices stay clear.”

Venom pats Fayge’s shoulder.

“Well,” she says casually.

“Venom here needs to sign the muster rolls before the heralds start screaming.”

Fayge catches the cue immediately.

“Of course.”

She bows politely to Cwenheld.

“It was… good seeing you.”

Cwenheld nods warmly.

“And you.”

Venom gently steers Fayge away through the crowd.

Once they’re clear, Fayge exhales.

“She thought I was you.”

Venom glances back.

Cwenheld has taken a seat on a stone bench near the chapel wall.

Helmet resting in her lap.

Face turned toward the sunlight.

Venom folds her arms quietly.

“Yeah.”

Fayge watches the old knight.

“…Did those things actually happen?”

Venom nods once.

“All of them.”

She turns toward the hall, then pauses and glances back again.

“Her eyes may be failing,” Venom says quietly.

“But Cwenheld’s heart still sees the world clear as day.”

Then she gives Fayge a faint smirk.

“And unfortunately for the rest of us… it remembers everything.”

# Interuption:

---

Cwenheld pauses mid-sentence as Fayge finally speaks.

“My lady… forgive me.”

The old knight tilts her head slightly.

“Yes?”

Fayge hesitates, clearly uncomfortable.

“I think there’s been a mistake.”

A small silence falls between them.

Cwenheld waits patiently.

“I’m not Dame Venom,” Fayge says gently. “My name is Fayge.”

For a moment the courtyard noise seems distant.

Cwenheld studies the bard’s silhouette again, her pale eyes narrowing slightly as she tries to make out the shape before her.

Then she exhales softly.

“…Ah.”

The understanding settles quietly.

Her shoulders straighten, the old knight gathering herself with practiced dignity.

“Of course.”

A faint, embarrassed smile touches her scarred face.

“These eyes of mine haven’t been trustworthy for some time.”

She gestures lightly toward her face.

“You have the same height. The same hair.”

A small pause.

“And apparently I was feeling sentimental.”

A dry chuckle escapes her.

“I’m afraid you’ve just endured a handful of stories meant for someone else.”

Cwenheld inclines her head respectfully.

“Thank you for your patience, Lady Fayge.”

Her voice softens slightly.

“And for stopping me before I wandered too far down the wrong road.”

She shifts her weight, preparing to move away.

Then adds, almost fondly:

“Though Venom will hear those stories again whether she likes it or not.”

A faint smile.

“Some memories deserve repeating.”

With that, the old knight gives Fayge a respectful nod and turns carefully toward the chapel steps.
