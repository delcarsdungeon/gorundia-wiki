# The Proclamation of Lady Cornelia's Virtue

Following persistent rumors circulating in court regarding **[[Cornelia Bordella]]** and **[[Sir Bertram Moffet]]**, the Crown issued an official finding to settle the matter.

By declaration of **[[Prince Alard Fenric]]**, acting in the King's name, it was determined that **no impropriety occurred between the two**, and that the Lady Cornelia was **"pure and untouched by any scandalous act."**

The proclamation further stated that the rumors had been **"malicious court gossip intended to diminish the dignity of a loyal noble house."**

The court accepted the declaration immediately.

No further inquiry was permitted.

The matter was therefore considered **legally closed**.

---

## Reality

Privately, nearly everyone in court understands the proclamation for what it is:

a **political absolution**.

The Crown required the scandal to disappear, and therefore it did.

No one of rank will contradict the official finding.

---

## Court Reaction

Among the nobility the ruling produced several predictable responses:

- Courtiers publicly praised the Crown's wisdom.
- Several noble houses quietly withdrew their daughters from the rumor mill.
- A handful of gossips complained that the court had become **“terribly dull.”**

The matter is now considered **settled**, whether or not anyone believes it.
