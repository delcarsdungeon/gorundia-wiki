## The Ash Circle

Three hundred yards of ash surround the Fire Temple.

No birds cross it.  
No insects hum within it.

Even the wind slows at the boundary and bends away.

Jinwing feels it before her boots press into it.

Not heat.

A clearing.

The kind left after a deliberate burn — when rot has been taken, and the land waits to see what will grow.

Sabry walks beside her, steady and composed.

They step across the ash.

The temple doors stand open.

And the sentinels ignite.

Two towering figures — twelve feet tall — flare into existence on either side of the entrance.

They are not stone.

They are sculpted flame — layered fire given disciplined form in the likeness of the genasi daughters of the blaze. Their hair lifts in slow-burning currents. Their eyes glow molten and unwavering.

They bow.

“Awakened Sleeper.”

The twin voices overlap slightly — one just ahead of the other.

Their gaze shifts.

To Jinwing.

The air tightens.

Recognition.

Not threat.

Not welcome.

Recognition.

They pivot inward, forming an arch of living fire.

Jinwing passes between them.

Heat washes across her skin — intimate, measuring, like warmth tracing veins beneath bark.

The scent is resin split open by lightning.

Behind her, the ash circle falls silent again.

Ahead—

The hall breathes.

## The Hall Reborn

When Jinwing last walked these halls, ash had choked the air.

The fallen Purifiers lay where they burned.  
The efreeti had stood cruel and incandescent at the heart of the chamber.  
The temple had felt like a mausoleum that refused to cool.

Now—

The hall is warm.

Alive.

Braziers burn steady blue-gold flame.  
Stone is clean but not sterile.  
Ash rests only where it belongs — in carved basins, honored, deliberate.

It smells faintly of cedar oil.

And beneath it—

Old lightning.

Jinwing feels the difference immediately.

This is not a ruin.

It is a heart, beating again.

## The Daughters Within

Two figures step from the shadows near the central brazier.

Not the towering sentinels outside.

These are nearer to mortal height — though still touched by flame.

Their forms are more defined, less monumental.

Their eyes are steady.

Their movements precise.

They bow first to Sabry.

Then to Jinwing.

“Living root enters the house of ash.”

Sabry inclines her head.

“She comes willingly.”

The two Daughters turn their full attention to Jinwing.

## The Rite of Ash

One steps before her.

One behind.

Jinwing feels heat gather — controlled, disciplined.

Her hair is gently unbound.

Warm fingers pass through it — not burning, but drying away forest damp and the scent of loam and wind.

It is braided tight at the crown.

Not a warrior’s braid.

A supplicant’s.

A bronze bowl is brought forward.

Ash rests within it — darker than common soot.

It carries the faint scent of iron.

Resin.

Blood-memory.

Ash of the fallen Purifiers.

It is pressed to Jinwing’s brow.

Cool at first.

Then—

Heat that is not physical.

She feels it like a chorus of vows brushing her mind.

Fire held against rot.  
Bodies given so corruption would not spread.

It weighs her.

It does not reject her.

Ash is pressed to her throat.

She tastes smoke.

And sap split by flame.

To her palms—

And here the druid in her recoils for a breath.

Fire strips forest.

Fire consumes leaf and bark.

But deeper still—

Fire clears rot.  
Fire opens cones.  
Fire makes space for new growth.

The Daughter before her pauses.

As if sensing that realization.

Then a crystal vial is uncorked.

The Oil of Truth.

Its scent is sharp — myrrh and metal.

A single drop is placed at the hollow of her throat.

It burns.

Not outward.

Inward.

Something aligns inside her.

A crooked branch straightened.

Her pulse becomes loud in her ears.

And beneath it—

A second pulse.

From the magitech arm.

## The Arm

The arm is borne forward with ceremony.

Metal and rune.  
Cron’s theft.  
Sylvanranth’s legacy twisted.

It is placed upon a small brass tripod at the center of the chamber.

Jinwing feels it immediately.

Contained heat.

Not hearth heat.

Buried-coal heat.

Resentful.

Flame licks from its seams — orange and sharp.

For a moment she senses a second will press outward.

Hungry.

Impatient.

The two Daughters inside stiffen.

The central brazier answers.

White-gold flame surges upward and tightens.

Suppresses.

Contains.

The orange flicker snaps inward.

The rival efreeti remains bound.

But listening.

## The Invocation

The two inner Daughters take their positions to either side of the brazier.

Sabry steps into the center.

Her voice deepens.

Not louder.

Older.

She speaks first in elvish cadence:

“Nárë Aerlindor.”

The Flame That Remembers the First Song.

Jinwing feels the name in her bones — like roots spreading through stone.

Then Sabry names it in harsher tongue:

“Kha’zir al-Keth.”

Keeper of Bound Ash.

The brazier erupts.

Not violently.

Expansively.

White-gold flame spirals upward, and at its heart a thin seam of green-white lightning threads through the blaze.

The fire leans.

Toward Jinwing.

Heat gathers around her — not enough to burn, but enough to demand honesty.

The scent is wildfire and rain together.

The druid in her feels something impossible.

This flame is not enemy to forest.

It is enemy to rot.

It studies her.

Curious.

Hungry.

Its voice rolls through the chamber — wind through coals, distant thunder, ember-pages turning.

“Druid of living root…”

A flicker.

Recognition of her prior tread.

“You return the arm-prison.”

The green-white seam pulses — satisfaction at containment.

“And you stand anointed in ash.”

A breath of heat presses against her skin.

“What do you seek?”

A pause.

“And what do you offer?”

From the tripod—

Faint.

Resentful.

The rival heat stirs.

Listening.
