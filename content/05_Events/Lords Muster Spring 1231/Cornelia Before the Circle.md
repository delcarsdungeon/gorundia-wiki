# Before the Circle

The antechamber outside the council chamber is quiet.

Not empty — guards stand at the doors, courtiers move somewhere beyond the stone walls — but quiet in the way a place becomes quiet when everyone is waiting for something unpleasant to begin.

Cornelia Bordella sits alone.

Her hands rest in her lap.

Perfect posture.  
Perfect stillness.

Anyone passing the doorway would see nothing but a composed noblewoman awaiting the court.

---

The title still feels strange.

**Countess of Blackvale.**

Yesterday she had been Ponzio Bordella’s sister.

Today she holds his lands.

The court has not yet finished deciding what will happen to the man himself.

But Cornelia has lived at court long enough to understand how these stories usually end.

---

Footsteps echo faintly in the corridor outside.

Voices murmur.

Doors open and close somewhere deeper in the palace.

The council chamber waits beyond the inner doors.

And on its floor, the truth circle waits with it.

---

Cornelia inhales slowly.

Not nervously.

Simply measuring the moment.

Truth magic has a reputation for drama.

In reality, it is something simpler.

Uncomfortable.

Unforgiving.

And very difficult to argue with.

---

She considers, briefly, the people who will be in that room.

The king.

Lord Marshal [[Beringer]].

[[Sorek Redmarch]].

Several others who now have very good reasons to dislike the Bordella name.

And her husband.

Sir [[Bertram Moffet]].

That thought lingers a moment longer than the others.

Then she lets it pass.

Today will not be an easy day for either of them.

---

A palace officer enters quietly.

He bows.

“Countess.”

“It will begin shortly.”

Cornelia inclines her head.

“Thank you.”

The officer withdraws.

The door closes again.

---

For a moment longer the chamber is silent.

Then Cornelia rises.

She smooths one sleeve, a small unconscious gesture.

The mask settles into place as easily as it always has.

---

Beyond the door, the council waits.

And the circle.

Cornelia Bordella steps forward to meet them.

---

## The Corridor

The doors open.

Cornelia steps into the corridor leading toward the council chamber.

Someone else is approaching from the opposite direction.

Sir [[Bertram Moffet]].

They stop a few paces apart.

For a moment neither speaks.

There are guards nearby.  
Servants further down the hall.

Too many witnesses for anything resembling privacy.

Bertram studies her.

Cornelia meets his gaze.

No apology.

No explanation.

Just the quiet understanding that whatever happens next will not belong to the two of them alone.

After a moment Bertram nods once.

Cornelia returns the gesture.

Then the palace officer clears his throat gently.

“This way, my lady.”

The doors to the council chamber open.

The faint glow of the truth circle spills across the stone floor.

Cornelia walks forward.

Bertram follows.

Neither looks back.
