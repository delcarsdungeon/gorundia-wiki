# Deposition of Annelise
*Former Keeper, Grimwatch Spire*  
*Taken immediately after the incident*  
*By writ of the Crown*

---

I am not learned. I do not know the proper words. I will say what I can.

I was assigned to the lower holding cells. Women, mostly. Debtors. A few accused witches. I brought water. I sat with them when I could. That was my whole duty.

---

The night it happened, there was no alarm.

That’s important. Grimwatch always had alarms. Bells. Lights. The lights never went out before—not once. People said that meant the tower was watching even when we weren’t.

That night, the lights didn’t go out.

They stopped meaning anything.

---

I heard screaming above us.

Not the prisoners.

The guards.

It wasn’t shouting. It was… surprise. Like men realizing too late they were already dead.

---

I locked the cells the way I was trained. I told the women to stay back from the bars.

Some of them were praying.

The young girl—Marielle—was very calm.

She took my hand and said:

> “It’s all right. They’re here.”

I asked who.

> “Family.”

---

Something passed the stairwell.

Not footsteps.

Pressure.

Like when a storm is about to break and the air changes first.

The torches flickered but didn’t go out. Shadows moved the wrong way.

---

I saw blood come down the steps.

Not splashing—running.

As if poured.

I did not see faces.

I saw shapes, maybe. Large. Fast. One was lower to the ground than the other. Both moved like they knew the tower already.

Like it belonged to them.

---

No one spoke to me. No one threatened me.

At one point, something stopped outside the cell door.

I couldn’t see it clearly.

The light bent.

That’s the only way I can explain it.

Like heat over stone.

I remember thinking:

*this is what it feels like to be prey.*

Then it passed.

---

Afterward, the doors opened.

Marielle was released.

She hugged me. She smelled like rain and iron.

She told me to go home.

She said:

> “You were kind. That matters.”

I left.

---

When I came back in the morning with the reeve’s men, everyone who worked above was dead.

Some looked like they’d been torn.

Some looked untouched, but… wrong.

Like their insides had been rearranged.

No bodies were missing.

Nothing was burned.

The lights were still on.

---

I don’t know who did it.

But I know this:

Whoever came into Grimwatch Spire that night did not come to make a point.

They came to finish something.
