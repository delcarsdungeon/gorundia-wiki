# The Ash Years

> “The sky dimmed, the winds carried dust, and the world waited to see what would survive.”  
> — Fragment from the **Annals of Westfall Abbey**

The **Ash Years** refer to the turbulent decades following the destruction of the **[[Suloise Imperium]]** during the **[[Rain of Colorless Fire]]**.  

Though the catastrophic magic struck far to the east of the western lands, its consequences spread across the continent. The destruction of the Imperium and the simultaneous devastation of the **Baklunish Empire** shattered the political and ecological balance of the known world.

For those living beyond the **[[Baklhaut Mountains]]**, the disaster arrived not as flame or spellfire, but as **strange weather, dim skies, and the arrival of desperate refugees**.

These years marked the beginning of a long transformation that would eventually reshape the western kingdoms.

---

## The Darkened Sky

In the years immediately following the Rain of Colorless Fire, many regions west of the mountains recorded unusual atmospheric phenomena.

Accounts describe:

- Weeks of dim or gray sunlight  
- Blood-red sunsets and pale dawns  
- Thin layers of ash carried on distant winds  
- Unseasonable cold in otherwise temperate lands  

While the worst devastation remained confined to the lands of the Imperium itself, the sky above much of the western world appeared muted for years afterward.

In some chronicles this period is referred to as **“the Long Dimming.”**

---

## Refugees of the Imperium

Within months of the catastrophe, the first refugees began crossing the passes of the **[[Baklhaut Mountains]]**.

They came in scattered groups at first:

- wounded soldiers  
- wandering priests  
- displaced nobles with small retinues  

But as the years passed the migrations grew larger.

Entire communities fled the ruins of the Imperium, carrying with them fragments of their former civilization — language, customs, and fragments of arcane knowledge.

Many western tribes and settlements were unprepared for the sudden arrival of thousands of displaced people.

This created decades of tension, conflict, and uneasy cooperation between the newcomers and the existing inhabitants of the region.

---

## The Mountain Crossings

The Baklhaut Mountains became the great gateway between the ruined east and the uncertain west.

Ancient passes that had once been used for trade were suddenly filled with refugees.

The journeys were brutal.

Starvation, banditry, and exposure claimed many lives along the way. Some groups disappeared entirely within the mountain ranges.

Those who survived emerged hardened by the experience.

Many of the noble houses that would later shape western kingdoms trace their lineage to leaders who guided refugee columns through these perilous crossings.

---

## The Fracturing of the Suel

The collapse of imperial authority meant that the surviving Suel people were no longer united under a single banner.

Instead, the refugees divided into numerous competing factions.

Some sought to rebuild the Imperium in new lands.

Others rejected the imperial legacy entirely.

Still others blended with local cultures, creating entirely new identities over time.

This fracturing would eventually give rise to a wide range of successor cultures throughout the western regions.

---

## The Western Lands

For the peoples already living west of the mountains, the Ash Years were a time of uncertainty.

The newcomers brought:

- advanced metallurgy  
- unfamiliar magical traditions  
- new systems of nobility and warfare  

Some communities welcomed the refugees and prospered through trade and alliance.

Others resisted what they saw as an invasion.

Small wars, shifting alliances, and gradual cultural exchange defined the decades following the catastrophe.

Out of this chaos, entirely new political structures began to emerge.

---

## Seeds of Future Kingdoms

The Ash Years did not produce immediate states or empires.

Instead, they laid the groundwork for centuries of development.

Refugee leaders became warlords.

Warlords became landed nobles.

Nobles formed alliances that slowly evolved into organized realms.

Many of the kingdoms that would later dominate the western lands — including those that would eventually form **Gorundia** — trace their earliest roots to the migrations and conflicts of this period.

---

## Legacy

Historians often describe the Ash Years as a **bridge between two ages**.

The ancient world of the great empires had ended.

The fragmented patchwork of kingdoms that would shape the later history of the region had not yet fully formed.

The survivors of the Ash Years lived in the shadow of a catastrophe they barely understood, building new lives amid the ruins of the old world.

Their descendants would inherit both the knowledge and the fears born in those uncertain decades.
