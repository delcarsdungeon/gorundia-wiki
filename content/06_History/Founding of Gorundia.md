# 070 — Founding of Gorundia

> “A tribe rides with its chief.  
> A nation rides with its king.”  
> — early Gorundian war proverb

The **Founding of Gorundia** marks the moment when the loose network of Oeridian tribal settlements of the central plains unified under a single war leadership and began to form the first recognizable Gorundian state.

This transformation did not occur peacefully.

It was born from **raids, frontier warfare, and the constant pressure of neighboring powers**.

---

## The Frontier Problem

By the time permanent settlements spread across the plains, the tribes faced dangers on all sides.

**From the east** came orc warbands descending from the mountains.

**From the north and east** rode the Qazaq nomads, whose raiding culture clashed with the newly settled towns.

**From the south** the expanding **Dominion** began pushing influence northward through trade posts, tributary settlements, and military patrols.

The old system of independent clans could not respond effectively.

Several devastating raids destroyed villages and granaries across the plains.

The tribal chiefs realized that if they remained divided, their settlements would not survive.

---

## The War Confederation

In response, several of the largest Oeridian clans formed a **military confederation**.

The alliance was originally intended as a temporary war council meant to coordinate defense.

Major clans involved included early ancestors of houses remembered in Gorundian tradition:

- Clan Ryzan  
- Clan Belovar  
- Clan Torvach  
- Clan Vaskor  
- Clan Drovan  

Each clan contributed riders and infantry to a combined force.

Leadership rotated among the most successful war chiefs.

But eventually one leader proved far more capable than the others.

---

## The First War King

According to Gorundian chronicles, the confederation chose a single war leader to command the tribes during a period of particularly heavy raids.

Rather than stepping down after the campaign ended, this leader maintained the alliance and continued organizing the defense of the plains.

Over time the role changed from **temporary war chief** to **permanent high king**.

The tribes still maintained autonomy, but they recognized a central authority responsible for:

- coordinating armies  
- defending the frontier  
- settling disputes between clans  
- organizing major construction works such as fortifications and roads

This position became the **first Gorundian kingship**.

---

## The First Fortresses

Under unified leadership, the tribes began constructing permanent defensive works.

Stone and timber forts appeared along key river crossings and trade routes.

These early strongholds served several purposes:

- defending agricultural settlements
- controlling trade between regions
- coordinating military response to raids

Some of these early forts would later grow into the first major Gorundian cities.

---

## Relations With the Dominion

During the earliest years of the kingdom the Dominion remained a looming power to the east.

Rather than immediately confronting the new state, the Dominion initially attempted to influence the emerging Gorundian leadership through diplomacy and trade.

Dominion agents sought to draw the plains tribes into their political orbit through:

- military advisors  
- religious influence  
- economic agreements  

These early contacts laid the groundwork for the conflicts that would later erupt during the **[[War of Independence]]**.

---

## Cultural Identity

With the formation of the first Gorundian kingdom, a distinct cultural identity began to emerge.

The people of the plains were no longer simply individual clans.

They began to see themselves as part of a broader society defined by several shared traits:

- fierce independence
- mastery of horse warfare
- frontier resilience
- suspicion of outside empires

This identity remains central to Gorundian culture to this day.

A common saying from the period survives in later chronicles:

> “We were tribes once.  
> Now we are a people.”

---

## Legacy

The **Founding of Gorundia** created the political and military structures that allowed the kingdom to survive against larger powers.

It also set the stage for the defining conflict of early Gorundian history.

As the Dominion expanded, the young kingdom would soon face a struggle that would shape its future.

That conflict became known as the **[[War of Independence]]**.
