# The Founding of the Dominion

_The Crossing of the Hellfurnaces_

### The Last Days of the Imperium

When the **Rain of Colorless Fire** annihilated the Suel Imperium, the high houses died with their archmages.

Cities vanished.  
Wizard-kings burned.  
Entire bloodlines evaporated in seconds.

Most Suel houses simply ceased to exist.

But one house survived.

Because their wizard died **first**.

---

# House Rhola-Kesh

_(The Imperial House of the Dominion)_

Before the cataclysm, **House Rhola-Kesh** was a powerful but not supreme Suel noble house. Like every great house of the Imperium, their power depended on a court wizard—an archmage who commanded the house’s magical supremacy.

Then the disaster began.

Their archmage, **Ardrath Kesh**, attempted to counter the magical catastrophe sweeping the Imperium.

The spell failed.

He died instantly.

The entire magical hierarchy of the house collapsed.

And that accident saved them.

Without a wizard to anchor their defenses, the house abandoned its capital **days before the final destruction**.

Their rivals stayed.

And died.

---

# The Exodus

House Rhola-Kesh fled with:

- household knights
- engineers and artisans
- temple priests
- a few thousand retainers

They carried **no great arcane relics**.

No wizard towers.

No libraries.

Just steel, grain, and horses.

They fled south.

Into the mountains.

---

# The Hellfurnace Crossing

The refugees entered the **Hellfurnace Mountains**, a volcanic wasteland inhabited by:

- fire giants
- salamanders
- orc tribes
- hostile dwarven holds
   
The journey lasted **three years**.

Entire clans perished.

Giant raids nearly destroyed the column.

Many Suel aristocrats died.

Leadership collapsed.

It was during this catastrophe that the house changed forever.

---

# The First Tyrant

The surviving leader was **Lord Aedor Rhola-Kesh**, a war captain with little patience for the old Suel decadence.

Seeing the nobles paralyzed by fear and infighting, he took command.

His decree became legendary.

> “Magic abandoned us.  
> Steel will not.”

Aedor executed several rival nobles who attempted to split the refugee column.

He reorganized the survivors into **military cohorts**.

Priests of **Hextor**—once a minor cult within the Imperium—rose to prominence among the soldiers.

The refugees embraced the creed:

**Strength is truth. Order is survival.**

---

# The Burning Covenant

At the end of the crossing, the survivors gathered in a volcanic valley.

There the Hextorite priests declared a new oath.

The old Imperium had died.

The Suel must become something harder.

They swore the **Burning Covenant**.

Six laws were proclaimed:

1. Order above freedom
2. Strength above mercy
3. Loyalty above blood
4. Victory above honor
5. Tyranny above chaos
6. Hextor above all

The Dominion was born.

---

# The First City

The refugees descended from the Hellfurnaces into fertile lands south of the mountains.

There they built a fortress city.

**[[Kesharath]].**

Aedor Rhola-Kesh was crowned **Imperial Tyrant**.

The Dominion of Kesh began.

---

# The Cultural Transformation

The Dominion abandoned many Suel traditions.

Old Suel culture valued:

- wizard-lords
- arcane scholarship    
- decadent aristocracy    

The Dominion replaced it with:

- military aristocracy    
- priest-guided governance    
- disciplined bureaucracy    

Arcane magic never disappeared.

But it was no longer supreme.

Steel and faith ruled.

---

# Why Gorundia Is Their Enemy

When the Dominion later expanded north, they encountered Suel-descended frontier settlers who had adopted **Heironeous and Pelor** instead of Hextor.

Those settlers eventually formed **Gorundia**.

To the Dominion, Gorundia represents:

- betrayal of Suel destiny
    
- weakness masquerading as virtue
    
- rebels who abandoned the creed of survival
    

The Dominion never forgot.

---

# Heraldry of the Imperial House

**House Rhola-Kesh**

Blazon:

> Sable, a gauntlet gules grasping six lightning bolts argent.

Symbolism:

- the gauntlet — Hextor
    
- the lightning bolts — stolen from Heironeous
    
- six bolts — the Six Laws
    

It is meant to be provocative.

Deliberately.

---

# Dominion Imperial Titles

The ruler bears the title:

**Imperial Tyrant of Kesh**

Other titles:

- Lord of the Hellfurnace Passage
- Keeper of the Burning Covenant
- First Servant of Hextor

---

# The National Myth

Every Dominion child learns the same lesson.

Their ancestors survived because they embraced tyranny.

The weak died.

The strong endured.

The Dominion therefore believes its rule is not cruelty.

It is **natural law**.
