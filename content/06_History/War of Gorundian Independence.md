# War of Independence

> “They ruled valleys and temples.  
> We ruled the wind.”  
> — early Gorundian war saying

The **War of Independence** marks the struggle through which the plains tribes west of the mountains broke from the rule of the **Khorovišk Dominion** and formed the Kingdom of **Gorundia**.

The conflict was not a single campaign but a long period of rebellion, frontier war, and political upheaval that reshaped the northern world.

Gorundian historians often treat the period as the first of the **Three Breakings of the North**.

---

# Dominion Control of the Plains

By the time permanent settlements appeared in the western plains, the Dominion had already consolidated its power within the **Khorovišk Valley**.

Dominion governors believed the plains naturally fell under their authority.

Control was imposed gradually through:

- tribute demands
- temple courts of **Hextor**
- fortified mountain passes
- trade monopolies

At first many plains chiefs tolerated Dominion oversight, seeing it as distant politics.

But as Dominion officials attempted to impose taxation, religious authority, and military control, resentment grew rapidly among the warrior clans.

---

# The Plains Revolt

The Oeridian clans of the plains were fiercely independent and accustomed to ruling themselves.

Dominion attempts to enforce law and tribute were met with increasing resistance.

Raids against imperial caravans became common.

Several Dominion garrisons established along the mountain passes were destroyed by mounted warbands.

Eventually the tribes did something rare in their history.

They united.

Multiple clan leaders formed a **war alliance**, coordinating cavalry forces and defensive settlements across the plains.

This alliance laid the foundations for the future **Kingdom of Gorundia**.

---

# Birth of the Kingdom

The rebellion forced Dominion authorities into open war.

Despite the discipline of Dominion infantry and the leadership of Hextorite war priests, the empire struggled to control the vast plains.

Gorundian riders struck supply lines, destroyed outposts, and avoided decisive engagements.

After years of conflict the Dominion was forced to withdraw from large portions of the frontier.

The victorious plains alliance established a new kingdom.

**Gorundia**

Unlike the Dominion’s increasingly centralized Hextorite hierarchy, Gorundia embraced a balance between the **Triad**:

- **Heironeous** — valor and honor  
- **Pelor** — light and life  
- **Pholtus** — law and order  

This religious balance became a defining feature of the new state.

---

# Uneasy Peace

Though the Dominion never accepted Gorundian independence, both sides emerged from the war exhausted.

For nearly a century the frontier stabilized into an uneasy peace.

Dominion cities held the eastern valley.

Gorundian knights ruled the western plains.

Trade resumed cautiously across the mountains, but hostility remained constant.

---

# The Orenic Schism

Two centuries later this fragile peace collapsed.

The Dominion’s ruling clergy, the **Iron Synod of Hextor**, began a campaign of religious consolidation known as the **Purification Edicts**.

These decrees demanded:

- recognition of Hextor’s supremacy
- forced conversion of frontier temples
- suppression of rival faiths

The territories ruled by **House Orenic** refused to comply.

The Orenics had long-standing alliances with Gorundian nobles and maintained strong devotion to **Heironeous**.

When Dominion legates attempted to enforce the edicts, the region erupted into revolt.

This conflict became known as the **War of the Severed Crown**.

House Orenic successfully broke from Dominion rule and aligned itself with Gorundia.

The Dominion viewed this as a catastrophic betrayal.

---

# A Region Destabilized

The wars between Gorundia, the Dominion, and the Orenic rebels destabilized the entire region.

Armies stripped forests for siege engines.

Frontier towns burned.

Ancient magical sites were disturbed.

This chaos would soon trigger an even greater catastrophe:

the **[[Clay Uprising]]**.

---

# Legacy

The War of Independence established the political world that still exists today.

It created:

- the Kingdom of **Gorundia**
- the enduring rivalry with the **Dominion**
- the alliance between Gorundia and **House Orenic**

Even centuries later the cultural memory remains powerful.

Gorundians still boast of the lesson their ancestors taught the empire:

> “We are the only free men in the world.”
