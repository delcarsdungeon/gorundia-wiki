# The Caervane Reckoning

> “People say Theron wiped out a family over that woman.  
> That’s not quite right.  
>  
> He wiped out a *mistake*.”  
> — [[Sorek Redmarch]]

The **Caervane Reckoning** refers to the clandestine campaign of retaliation carried out by **[[Theron Sylvanranth]]** after the assassination of his wife, [[Rowena Caervane]], in **1138 OR**.

Among historians of Gorundia, the Reckoning is remembered as one of the most methodical acts of vengeance ever undertaken by a single noble household. What began as the investigation of a single murder evolved into a years-long dismantling of an entire intelligence network operating across the Gorundian–Dominion frontier.

The killing itself had not been intended for Rowena.

The assassins had come for Theron.

He was away on campaign when they struck.

Rowena died in his place.

Subsequent investigation revealed that the attack had been organized as part of a **Dominion intelligence operation**, executed through multiple layers of intermediaries operating within Gorundia.

Theron’s response was neither immediate nor theatrical.

Instead, he began tracing the structure of the conspiracy — identifying its agents, financiers, facilitators, and political sponsors.

For nearly a decade he pursued the network across **both sides of the Gorundian–Dominion border**, dismantling it piece by piece.

By the end of the campaign, **every identifiable individual connected to the plot had been eliminated**.

To outside observers, the campaign appeared less like vengeance and more like the ruthless correction of a failed conspiracy.

Theron did not lash out blindly or seek symbolic reprisals.  
He mapped the structure of the operation and removed every person who had made the attack possible.

Among political rivals and historians alike, the reckoning was often described simply as Theron dismantling a poorly executed Dominion operation.

Those closer to the Sylvanranth household understood it differently.

[[Rowena Caervane]] had been one of the very few people Theron loved openly and without calculation. Her death was not merely a political attack — it was the destruction of the one person who consistently stood beside him as an equal.

To them, the reckoning was not the correction of a mistake.

It was the work of a man who had decided that **every person responsible for that loss would eventually die**.

---

# The Conspiracy

The assassination network was deliberately constructed to obscure Dominion involvement.

It consisted of several operational layers: hired blades, smugglers, couriers, political facilitators, and noble sponsors.

The arrangement ensured that no single captured agent could expose the entire operation.

Theron dismantled every layer.

---

# Field Operatives

The killers themselves were the first to die.

- **Yarek Dolin** — assassin responsible for the fatal strike  
- **Sera Vanthel** — courier coordinating payment and movement  
- **Juro Talan** — weapons broker supplying the killing blade  
- **Kerrin Vale** — safehouse operator within the capital  
- **Marek Dolis** — secondary blade assigned as contingency

All five were dead within six months.

Their handlers soon followed.

---

# Gorundian Facilitators

Several individuals within Gorundia knowingly assisted the operation.

Motives ranged from profit to political rivalry.

Those later identified included:

- **Halveric Tann** — merchant factor who laundered Dominion funds through caravan trade  
- **Sir Odran Belric** — border knight who arranged safe passage through his lands  
- **Dellan Grist** — smuggler captain who ferried the assassins across the frontier  
- **Petron Malve** — clerk within the Royal Petty Council who provided travel seals and documentation  
- **Courtier Selvin Aroth** — intermediary who relayed messages between conspirators  
- **Lady Mirelle Dastain** — minor court figure who unknowingly passed coded correspondence

None survived the following three years.

Belric died in a riding accident that several witnesses later described as “remarkably precise.”

Petron Malve vanished during a routine journey between ministries.

Selvin Aroth was found drowned in the capital canals.

Lady Mirelle Dastain succumbed to what physicians described as a “rare and sudden poisoning.”

The merchant house of **Tann** collapsed soon afterward, its holdings quietly redistributed.

---

# The Capital Underworld

The investigation eventually uncovered that the conspirators had relied heavily on contacts within the **capital’s thieves’ guild**.

Theron’s retaliation against the guild became legendary.

Over the course of two winters:

- **Guildmaster Corval the Thin** disappeared  
- **Reth Durn**, chief fence for the western quarter, was executed  
- **The Black Ladder Crew**, a smuggling ring of eleven members, was eliminated  
- Three safehouses in the Dock Ward were burned

Nearly a **third of the guild’s leadership** died during the purge.

The organization survived, but it did so in a weakened and far more cautious form.

For years afterward the guild avoided any work remotely connected to Dominion intelligence.

---

# Dominion Sponsors

The conspiracy ultimately traced back to a Dominion intelligence cell.

Those identified included:

- **Lady Katrya Vosk** — Dominion noble suspected of financing the operation  
- **Magister Ruln Dareth** — intelligence officer coordinating agents in Gorundia  
- **Captain Ilvor Serev** — military liaison who authorized the operation  
- **The Vosk Trade Consortium**, which funneled payment through merchant intermediaries

Theron’s retaliation crossed the border repeatedly.

Within six years:

- Magister Dareth was found dead in his estate outside Arvenfall  
- Captain Serev died when his escort column was ambushed by unidentified riders  
- Lady Vosk disappeared while traveling under military guard  
- The Vosk consortium collapsed after the deaths of several senior partners

Dominion authorities never publicly acknowledged the connection.

Privately, however, the message was understood.

---

# The Reach of the Reckoning

Theron’s campaign became infamous for its **absolute thoroughness**.

Anyone who had knowingly assisted the plot was targeted.

Those who had unknowingly helped the conspirators were investigated.

Several were eliminated anyway.

Later accounts suggest Theron briefly considered executing **every courier who had delivered messages to the conspirators’ residences**, regardless of whether they understood the contents.

His advisers reportedly convinced him this would be excessive.

Even so, the purge came close.

The effort consumed enormous sums of gold and strained Gorundian diplomacy with the Dominion.

Theron never apologized.

---

# Sorek Redmarch

During the later years of the reckoning, **[[Sorek Redmarch]]** entered Theron’s service.

Accounts differ on exactly when.

Some claim Sorek joined after witnessing Theron dismantle an entire Dominion intelligence network.

Others say he simply recognized a commander worth following.

Whatever the cause, Sorek would remain at Theron’s side for decades.

---

# Legacy

The Caervane Reckoning fundamentally changed **Theron Sylvanranth**.

Those who knew him before the assassination remembered a man capable of warmth and restraint.

Afterward, he became colder, more calculating, and far less tolerant of perceived threats.

Many historians believe the reckoning shaped the ruthless pragmatism that later defined his career.

When Theron ordered the **Burning of Elderbrook** decades later in response to a half-orc uprising, critics argued that the seeds of that decision could be traced back to the long campaign of vengeance that followed Rowena’s death.

Theron had already tested mercy once.

It had cost him everything.

---

# Lingering Fallout

Though decades have passed, the consequences of the reckoning remain visible.

Several merchant families quietly resent the destruction of House Tann.

The capital’s thieves’ guild still avoids any dealings connected to Dominion intelligence.

Within the Dominion itself, the humiliation inflicted upon its agents was never forgotten.

Some historians believe the Dominion eventually answered Theron’s vengeance with a far more patient form of retaliation.

The betrayal that later emerged within the Sylvanranth bloodline may not have been coincidence.

It may have been **revenge**.
