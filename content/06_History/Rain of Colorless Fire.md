# The Rain of Colorless Fire

> “The sky did not burn.  
> It simply *unmade the world.*”  
> — fragment from a recovered Baklunish chronicle

The **Rain of Colorless Fire** is one of the most catastrophic magical events recorded in the history of Oerik. Occurring during the final days of the ancient wars between the **[[Suloise Imperium]]** and the **[[Baklunish Empire]]**, the event destroyed the heart of the Suel empire and reshaped the geography and history of the western world.

In a final act of vengeance, the Baklunish unleashed an arcane devastation upon the Imperium unlike anything previously seen — a weaponized magical cataclysm that annihilated cities, boiled seas, shattered mountains, and reduced the fertile lands of the Imperium to a lifeless desert now known as the **[[Sea of Dust]]**.

The event did not merely destroy a civilization. It shattered the balance of power across the entire western half of the continent.

---

## The Twin Catastrophes

The Rain of Colorless Fire occurred during the last phase of the **Baklunish–Suloise Wars**, a conflict between two magical superpowers whose mastery of arcane forces rivaled that of the greatest mythic civilizations.

In response to centuries of war and imperial aggression, the Baklunish invoked a devastating ritual. Their spell unleashed what later historians would call **the Rain of Colorless Fire**.

Moments after the spell struck the Imperium, the surviving Suel archmages retaliated with their own apocalyptic working — the **Invoked Devastation**, which obliterated much of the Baklunish heartland.

Together, the two spells destroyed both empires.

Where the Imperium once stood, only dust remained.

## Elder Race Accounts

### Elvish Chronicle

> “We saw the sky tear without flame.  
> The sun dimmed though no cloud crossed it.  
> For three days the eastern horizon glowed like pale glass, and the forests grew silent.  
> The younger races had broken the world again.”  
> — Fragment from the **Annals of Celene**

Elven records tend to describe the event with eerie precision rather than drama. Several surviving elven chronicles note the unnatural light on the horizon and the sudden disturbance of wildlife across vast distances.

Many elven historians refer to the Rain of Colorless Fire simply as **“The Second Folly of Man.”**

## Druidic Tradition

> “It was not fire that fell.  
> Fire belongs to the world.
> 
> What came from the sky that day was _absence._
> 
> The rivers forgot their paths.  
> The soil forgot how to grow.  
> Even the wind wandered like an animal that had lost its scent.
> 
> The archmages did not merely destroy a kingdom.
> 
> They wounded the land itself.”  
> — Oral teaching preserved by the **Circle of the Verdant Grave**

Among druids, the Rain of Colorless Fire is remembered not as a magical event but as **a violation of the natural order on a continental scale**.

Many druidic traditions teach that the catastrophe created **a scar in the living world**, a place where the balance between the elements was violently broken.

In druidic lore, the **[[Sea of Dust]]** is sometimes called **“the Empty Land”** — a region where the natural cycles of growth, decay, water, and renewal were stripped away by unnatural magic.

Several druid circles claim that even centuries later the land **may never heal**.

Animals avoid the region.

Storms behave strangely near its borders.

And certain ancient groves far to the west are said to have felt the wound **the moment the spell struck**, as if the roots of the world itself had been cut.

For this reason many druids teach a simple warning to younger initiates:

> “When wizards make war with the sky, it is the earth that bleeds.”

---
### Dwarven Record

> “We closed the deep gates and watched the stone tremble.  
> The sky burned without heat, and the eastern wind carried dust that was not dust.  
> The humans had turned wizardry into a hammer and struck the world with it.”  
> — Carving from the **Deep Ledger of Karak-Bakhar**

Dwarven accounts focus less on the sky and more on the physical effects felt underground. Records from several holds describe tremors in the deep stone and strange disturbances in underground water flows in the years following the catastrophe.

Among dwarves, the event is remembered as a lesson in the dangers of reckless sorcery.

---

### Gnomish Observations

> “The light came first. Not bright — wrong.  
> As though the sun had forgotten its color.  
> Three weeks later the refugees began arriving, and we understood what the sky had tried to warn us.”  
> — Field notes attributed to **Archivist Pellinwick of the Copper Library**

Gnomish records are among the most analytical surviving descriptions. Several early gnomish scholars attempted to catalog the atmospheric effects of the catastrophe, noting ashfall, unusual sunsets, and disturbances in migratory animal patterns.

Their notes also provide some of the earliest descriptions of the refugee migrations that followed.

## The Sea of Dust

The Rain of Colorless Fire transformed the heart of the Suloise Imperium into the **Sea of Dust**, a vast wasteland of ash, poisoned earth, and magical scars stretching across what had once been the wealthiest lands of the Imperium.

Entire cities vanished without leaving ruins.

Forests burned without flame.

Rivers evaporated.

Even centuries later, travelers report strange phenomena in the region — glass deserts, silent storms of ash, and lingering magical anomalies.

Few who enter the Sea of Dust return.

---

## Collapse of the Suloise Imperium

The destruction of the Imperium caused the collapse of its political order almost overnight.

The surviving Suel nobility, soldiers, and refugees fled in every direction:

- west across the **[[Baklhaut Mountains]]**
- south into new frontier lands
- east toward the Flanaess
- north toward the steppes

These migrations scattered the descendants of the Imperium across the continent, where they would eventually form new cultures, kingdoms, and empires.

Many later states trace their origins to these refugees.

Among them is the militaristic power that would eventually become the **[[Khoroûvsk Dominion]]**.

---

## The Exodus of the Suel

The survivors of the catastrophe fled the wasteland in great migrations.

Noble houses escaped with their retainers and armies.

Priests carried sacred relics.

Wizards fled with whatever lore they could preserve.

But the journey was brutal. Refugee columns crossed deserts, mountain passes, and hostile lands. Many died along the way.

Those who survived carried with them the trauma of witnessing the destruction of their civilization.

Centuries later, this memory still shapes the identity of many Suel-descended cultures.

---

## What the West Saw

For peoples living **west of the Imperium**, beyond the **[[Baklhaut Mountains]]**, the Rain of Colorless Fire was not seen directly — but its effects were unmistakable.

Accounts preserved in tribal songs and early chronicles describe strange phenomena in the years following the catastrophe:

- Ash falling from the sky despite clear weather  
- Weeks of dim, gray sunlight  
- Unnatural storms along the southern coasts  
- Blood-red sunsets lasting for months  
- Migrating columns of desperate refugees emerging from the eastern mountain passes

Some legends describe nights when the eastern horizon glowed faintly with a pale, colorless light — a lingering echo of the catastrophe.

Others speak of **“the silent thunder beyond the mountains.”**

For many communities west of the mountains, the disaster marked the beginning of what later historians would call **the Ash Years**, a generation of crop failures, strange weather, and unrest as refugees poured into the western lands.

## The Orbital Catastrophe Theory

Some historians and arcane theorists argue that the **Rain of Colorless Fire was not a single spell**, but rather the activation of a **pre-existing magical weapons system**.

The theory proposes that the Baklunish did not simply cast a catastrophic ritual. Instead, they **triggered or redirected ancient arcane engines in the upper heavens**.

In other words:

**The sky itself was weaponized.**

---

### Evidence Cited by Scholars

Several oddities in surviving accounts support the theory.

#### 1. The “Rain” Description

Nearly every surviving description says the devastation **fell from the sky**.

Not lightning.  
Not meteors.  
Not fire.

But something that descended silently.

Witnesses describe:

- pale streaks in the sky    
- colorless flashes    
- objects or energy “falling”    

This has led some scholars to speculate that **arcane constructs or spell engines were already positioned above the world**.

---

#### 2. The Absence of Magical Residue

Typical world-scale rituals leave **massive magical residue**.

But explorers in the **[[Sea of Dust]]** have long noted that the region is strangely _clean_ in arcane terms.

Instead of a lingering spell field, the destruction resembles **overwhelming instantaneous energy release**.

As if the damage came from **outside the normal magical environment**.

---

#### 3. The Precision of the Destruction

The Rain of Colorless Fire annihilated the **heartland of the Suloise Imperium**, yet regions far to the west and north survived with only atmospheric effects.

Such precision would be difficult for a ritual cast across thousands of miles.

But it would be consistent with **targeted strikes from high altitude spell platforms**.

---

#### 4. The “Colorless” Phenomenon

Several magical scholars have noted something very strange about the name.

Arcane energy normally manifests with **strong colors**:

- red for destructive evocation    
- violet for planar energies    
- blue-white for lightning    
- green for alchemical corruption    

But the Rain of Colorless Fire was repeatedly described as **without color**.

Some theorists interpret this as **energy stripped of elemental identity** — a raw magical force unknown in modern spellcraft.

---

## Possible Origins of the System

If the theory is true, the question becomes far more disturbing.

**Who built the weapon?**

Three major theories exist.

---

### Ancient Suloise War Magic

The Imperium itself may have created orbital arcane weapons as part of its imperial arsenal.

The Baklunish, unable to replicate them, might have instead discovered how to **trigger the system remotely**, turning the Imperium’s own weapons against it.

This would explain why the devastation fell primarily on Suloise lands.

---

### Baklunish Astral Engineering

Baklunish magical traditions were heavily influenced by **astronomy and planar mathematics**.

Some scholars believe their archmages could have constructed **spell engines anchored in the upper planes or the void above the world**.

These could then have been directed downward during the final stages of the war.

---

### Pre-Human Civilization

The most controversial theory suggests that the system predates both empires.

Some ruins and arcane anomalies found in the Sea of Dust hint at **magical infrastructure far older than either civilization**.

If true, the Baklunish may have merely **activated something ancient** rather than creating it.

Many historians dismiss this theory as speculation.

Others find it deeply unsettling.

---

## Why the Theory Persists

The orbital catastrophe theory is controversial because it implies something terrifying:

The Rain of Colorless Fire might not represent the **limit** of magical destruction.

It might represent **someone discovering how to turn the key**.

If such a system once existed, some scholars fear that remnants of it might still exist — buried in forgotten archives, hidden in ancient ruins, or locked away in magical vaults whose builders never imagined anyone would find them again.

---

## Quiet Warnings Among Wizards

Among experienced archmages, there is a common saying when discussing ancient magical catastrophes.

> “The Rain of Colorless Fire was not the worst spell ever cast.  
> It was the worst spell **successfully used.**”

---

## Legacy

The Rain of Colorless Fire reshaped the entire history of Oerik.

Its consequences include:

- the destruction of the **[[Suloise Imperium]]**
- the creation of the **[[Sea of Dust]]**
- the migrations that reshaped western civilization
- the rise of successor states such as the **[[Khoroûvsk Dominion]]**
- the spread of Suel bloodlines and magical traditions across the continent

Even centuries later, the event remains a defining moment in history — a reminder of the catastrophic power wielded by the ancient empires.

In many traditions, the Rain of Colorless Fire is remembered not only as a disaster, but as a warning.
