# The Crossing of the Hellfurnaces

> “The archmages burned with their towers.  
> The soldiers marched.”  
> — Dwarven Chronicle of Khaz-Borun

When the [[Rain of Colorless Fire]] annihilated the Suloise Imperium, most of the great houses of that empire died alongside their archmages.

Cities vanished.  
Wizard-kings burned.  
Entire bloodlines evaporated in moments.

Most Suel houses simply ceased to exist.

A handful survived.

Among them was **House Rhola-Kesh**, later remembered as the house that founded the Dominion.

---

## The Last Days of the Imperium

Before the catastrophe, House Rhola-Kesh was a powerful but not supreme noble house of the Imperium.

Like most Suel aristocracy, its power depended heavily upon a **court archmage** who anchored the magical supremacy of the house.

That wizard was **Ardrath Kesh**.

When the magical catastrophe began sweeping the Imperium, Ardrath attempted to counter it with a desperate spell.

The spell failed.

He died instantly.

The magical hierarchy of the house collapsed.

Ironically, that failure saved them.

Without their wizard to anchor their defenses or demand they hold their capital, the house abandoned its holdings days before the final devastation.

Their rivals stayed.

And died.

---

## The Exodus

House Rhola-Kesh fled with what it could gather.

The refugee column included:

- household knights  
- engineers and artisans  
- temple priests  
- several thousand retainers and dependents  

They carried few arcane relics.

No wizard towers.  
No great libraries.

Only steel, grain, horses, and discipline.

The refugees marched south toward the mountains.

---

## The Hellfurnace Crossing

The refugees entered the **Hellfurnace Mountains**, a volcanic wasteland inhabited by:

- fire giants  
- salamanders  
- orc tribes  
- hostile dwarven holds  

The crossing lasted nearly three years.

Entire clans perished.

Giant raids nearly destroyed the refugee column more than once.

Many Suel aristocrats died during the march, leaving the survivors leaderless and desperate.

It was during this catastrophe that the house changed forever.

---

## The First Tyrant

The surviving leader was **Lord Aedor Rhola-Kesh**, a war captain with little patience for the decadent traditions of the old Imperium.

Seeing the surviving nobles paralyzed by fear and infighting, he seized control of the refugee host.

His decree became legend among Dominion chroniclers.

> “Magic abandoned us.  
> Steel will not.”

Aedor executed several rival nobles who attempted to divide the column.

He reorganized the survivors into disciplined military cohorts.

Priests of **Hextor**, once a minor cult within the Imperium, rose rapidly in influence among the soldiers.

Among the refugees a new creed took hold.

Strength was truth.

Order was survival.

---

## The Burning Covenant

At the end of the Hellfurnace crossing the survivors gathered in a volcanic valley beyond the mountains.

There the Hextorite priests proclaimed a new oath.

The old Imperium was dead.

The Suel would become something harder.

They swore the **Burning Covenant**.

Six laws were proclaimed:

1. Order above freedom  
2. Strength above mercy  
3. Loyalty above blood  
4. Victory above honor  
5. Tyranny above chaos  
6. Hextor above all  

This moment is traditionally regarded as the **birth of the Dominion**.

---

## The First City

Descending from the Hellfurnaces, the refugees discovered fertile lands beyond the mountains.

There they founded a fortress city.

**Kesharath.**

Aedor Rhola-Kesh was crowned **Imperial Tyrant of Kesh**, ruling not as a king but as the supreme warlord of the surviving houses.

Over time the ruling title evolved into **Dominar**, the military ruler of the Dominion.

Several noble families that had marched with Rhola-Kesh became the **servitor houses** of the new state.

Their names changed over centuries, but Dominion tradition still speaks of the **Six Houses of the Covenant**.

---

## The Plains of the Oeridians

The refugees soon discovered that the lands beyond the Hellfurnaces were not empty.

The plains were inhabited by scattered **Oeridian tribes and settlements**.

These people were numerous and deeply rooted in the land.

The Dominion refugees were too few to conquer them outright.

For several generations the region existed as a **dual society**.

The Suel refugees dominated the fortified cities and engineering works they built.

The Oeridian clans held the plains, supplying cavalry, grain, and trade.

Suel metallurgy and administration mixed uneasily with Oeridian horse culture and warrior traditions.

The relationship was tense but largely peaceful.

---

## Seeds of the Future Conflict

The cultural divide between the two peoples was reinforced by religion.

The Dominion increasingly followed **Hextor**, whose creed justified their harsh new order.

The Oeridian clans favored **Heironeous**, whose ideals of honor and courage matched their warrior culture.

For generations the two faiths coexisted.

But the philosophical divide would eventually prove impossible to maintain.

Centuries later the Oeridian territories would revolt, forming the independent kingdom of **Gorundia**.

Dominion historians still regard that event as a betrayal.

Gorundian historians remember it as liberation.

---

## Heraldry of the Imperial House

**House Rhola-Kesh**

Blazon:

*Sable, a gauntlet gules grasping six lightning bolts argent.*

Symbolism:

- the gauntlet — Hextor  
- the lightning bolts — taken from Heironeous  
- the six bolts — the Six Laws of the Covenant  

The design was intentionally provocative.
