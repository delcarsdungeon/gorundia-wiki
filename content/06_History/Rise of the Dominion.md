# 065 — Rise of the Dominion

> “Where the archmages failed, the generals prevailed.”  
> — Dominion military chronicle

While the plains tribes slowly transformed into the settlements of early Gorundia, a far more organized power was rising beyond the eastern mountains.

This state would become known as **the Dominion of Khorovišk**.

---

## The Valley Empire

The Dominion was born in the **vast Khorovišk Valley**, a fertile basin surrounded by mountains and natural defenses.

Unlike the scattered tribal societies of the plains, the valley possessed several advantages:

- rich farmland
- navigable rivers
- defensible mountain passes
- remnants of Suel arcane infrastructure

Early migrants from the **Suloise Exodus** settled the valley and gradually unified its settlements under powerful noble houses.

These houses originally relied heavily on arcane advisors.

But the magical traditions of the old Suel empire had been devastated during the cataclysm.

Over time, **the Dominion shifted away from archmages toward military authority**.

---

## The Military Ascendancy

As the old magical elites declined, power passed to **military aristocracies devoted to conquest and order**.

These houses embraced the harsh philosophy of **Hextor**, god of tyranny and martial discipline.

The Dominion developed a rigid social hierarchy centered on:

- noble warlords
- professional soldiers
- priestly administrators
- vast populations of laborers and slaves

Their society valued **strength, obedience, and expansion** above all else.

---

## Expansion Into the Plains

Once the valley was secure, Dominion rulers turned their attention west.

The open plains beyond the mountains appeared disorganized and ripe for influence.

Rather than immediate conquest, the Dominion pursued a gradual strategy:

- establishing **trade routes**
- sending **envoys to tribal chiefs**
- introducing **Hextorite priests**
- building **fortified outposts near mountain passes**

Through these methods the Dominion began extending its influence into the western frontier.

Many tribal leaders accepted Dominion gifts and recognition.

Others resisted.

---

## The Frontier Balance

At this time the plains were still dominated by **Oeridian tribal societies**, only beginning the slow transition toward permanent settlements.

The Dominion viewed these tribes as:

- potential allies
- future subjects
- or obstacles to be removed

Meanwhile the tribes regarded the Dominion with suspicion.

Their society valued independence and clan loyalty above imperial authority.

This uneasy relationship would shape centuries of conflict between the two civilizations.

---

## Contact With the West

As Gorundian settlements expanded, another development began to reshape the region.

Permanent towns and roads gradually pushed westward toward the ancient forests.

For the first time in generations, the plains peoples established **formal contact with the twin elven kingdoms** that bordered the western woodlands.

These interactions were cautious but largely peaceful.

Elven rulers recognized that the rising Gorundian settlements could serve as a buffer against the expanding Dominion.

Trade, diplomacy, and occasional alliances soon followed.

---

## The Coming Conflict

By the time Gorundia began forming into a unified kingdom, the political map of the region had changed dramatically.

Three powers now faced one another across the frontier:

- the **Dominion** to the east
- the **Gorundian tribes and settlements** in the plains
- the **elven kingdoms** guarding the western forests

Tensions between these civilizations would eventually ignite the defining struggle of early Gorundian history.

The **[[War of Independence]]**.
