# **Ashmaw of the Red Hills**

_The Fortress-Breaker_

> _“Half the stories about Cwenheld are nonsense. The other half aren’t big enough…_  
> _I’ve seen the woman. The dragon didn’t lose to a knight._  
> _It lost to a wall that decided it wasn’t moving.”_  
> — **Sorek Redmarch**

---
## **Overview**

Ashmaw of the Red Hills was a red dragon that attempted to establish dominion over the upland ruins west of Marsten approximately three decades ago.

Unlike the more complex draconic events recorded elsewhere in the region, Ashmaw represents a **straightforward case of territorial dominance and destruction**.

It is also one of the few recorded instances where:

> **A dragon was confronted directly—and killed—without broader systemic consequences.**

---
## **The Battle of Ashfall Ruins (Summary)**

The dragon descended upon an abandoned fortress in the Red Hills, burning outlying settlements and claiming the ruins as a prospective lair.

A small company answered:

- [[Cwenheld]] — Knight of Heironeous    
- [[Avelyne Marsten]] — Noble warrior    
- [[Galmyr Vogel]] — Rising knight of House Vogel    
- [[Theravain the Ashkeeper]] — Elven wizard    

The engagement was immediate and catastrophic.

- The dragon’s first assault **collapsed portions of the fortress**    
- **Theravain countered dragonfire directly**, halting a full breath—at the cost of his life    
- [[Cwenheld]] **held the line under direct breath**, suffering permanent burns and blindness    
- [[Galmyr Vogel]] and Avelyne exploited the dragon’s grounding and inflicted fatal wounds    

The dragon was slain.

The company did not survive intact.

## **Inferred (Norwin’s Analysis)**

- Ashmaw operated as a **pure dominator-type dragon**:
    
    - No evidence of manipulation, corruption, or long-term planning beyond territorial control
        
- The engagement confirms:
    

> Dragons can be killed through force—  
> but only when the problem is simple.

- The fortress itself was likely incidental:   

> Either a strategic asset… or simply something in its way.
