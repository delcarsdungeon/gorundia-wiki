# Wild-Eye Wilcox

_House Mage of the Lee Business Syndicate_

---
## Overview

Few people in Bard’s Gate are entirely certain who **Wild-Eye Wilcox** really is. Even fewer are certain what he is.

Wilcox is a gnome mage employed by the **Lee Business Syndicate**, serving as their resident magical specialist. His primary duties involve **masking magical signatures**, frustrating divinations, and ensuring that curious wizards, priests, or inquisitors have a very difficult time finding anything the Syndicate would rather keep hidden.

He is widely regarded as **eccentric, unsettling, and chemically unstable**, but also **surprisingly effective**.

Most members of the Syndicate would describe him the same way:

> “Not sane. Not clean. Not sober. But when the spellwork matters, the little bastard delivers.”

---
## Appearance

Wilcox looks like someone who has been living inside an alchemist’s workshop for the last decade.

• Age: Appears middle-aged for a gnome  
• Hair: Dirty blond, usually wild and uncombed  
• Beard: Patchy and uneven  
• Eyes: One normal blue eye, the other a strange yellow-gold that reflects light like a cat’s

His clothing is a jumble of:

• stained robes  
• leather tool belts  
• potion vials  
• chalk sticks  
• loose spell components

Small bottles hang from nearly every strap or pocket. Some are empty. Some are not.

He constantly smells of **burnt sugar, lightning, and regret**.

---
## Reputation

Wilcox’s magical talents are undeniable.

Despite his erratic demeanor, he has repeatedly proven capable of:

• obscuring magical traces  
• interfering with divination magic  
• hiding enchanted objects  
• scrambling magical detection

Several rival spellcasters have attempted to track Syndicate activities through magical means.

Few have succeeded.

Most eventually conclude that **someone very competent is deliberately interfering with them**.

---
## Chemical Habits

Wilcox is rarely sober.

He is known to frequently use 
- **Phoenix Drops**, a volatile magical stimulant that allows a caster to push themselves beyond normal limits.
- **Tinker’s Joy**  (formally marketed as _High Velocity Thinking Powder_)

In addition to these substances, Wilcox has a well-known habit of experimenting with **potions not intended for recreational use**.

No one in the Syndicate has successfully convinced him to stop.

---
## The Doll

Wilcox is rarely seen without a small **porcelain doll**.

The doll resembles a gnome child with pale porcelain skin, bright blue eyes, and faint freckles painted across its cheeks.

Wilcox carries it constantly.

He sometimes **talks to it**.

He occasionally **asks it questions**.

No one has ever heard the doll reply.

---
## The Rumor

There is a story that circulates among Syndicate members.

No one admits to starting it.

Several swear it is true.

The story goes that if Wilcox sets the doll down somewhere…

…and everyone looks away…

…the doll will not always be where it was left.

Some claim its head turns.

Others say its eyes follow them.

A few swear the doll sometimes ends up in **entirely different rooms**.

No one has ever proven this.

No one has ever been eager to try.

---
## Reliability

Despite everything—his habits, his oddities, his conversations with porcelain children—Wilcox remains valuable to the Syndicate.

His spells work.

His wards hold.

His concealments are difficult to unravel.

And when magical trouble starts brewing, someone in the Lee Business Syndicate inevitably says the same thing:

> “Get Wilcox.”

---
## Rumors in Bard’s Gate

Various things are said about Wild-Eye Wilcox.

Most are probably lies.

• “He was a court wizard once.”  
• “He got thrown out of one of those elf academies.”  
• “He died once. Not sure when he came back.”  
• “That yellow eye isn’t his.”  
• “The doll watches people.”
