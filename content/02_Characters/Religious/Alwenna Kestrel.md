Appearance
![[Alwenna Kestrel.png|220]]

Alwenna Kestrel is the sort of woman people underestimate instantly.

She is small — barely **5'1"**, slight, and immaculately composed.
Her pale blonde hair is worn in a tight braided scholar’s knot.
Round spectacles rest on the bridge of her nose and slide down when she reads.

Her robes are **temple white trimmed with narrow gold lines**, the vestments of a junior Truthseeker of Pholtus.

At her belt hangs:

* a **small mace** (perfectly maintained, rarely used)
* three **ink pens**
* a **wax seal kit**
* a **leather-bound codex of royal law**

A satchel filled with scroll tubes, legal notes, and reference tablets never leaves her side.

She writes **everything down**.

Everything.

---

# Personality

Alwenna is:

* precise
* methodical
* patient
* relentless

She is not timid.

She simply prefers **facts to noise**.

When arguments erupt around her, she waits.

Then eventually says something like:

“According to Royal Charter Seven, Subsection Twelve… that is incorrect.”

She never raises her voice.

She does not need to.

---

# Her Faith

Pholtus teaches that:

* Law reveals truth
* Truth reveals order
* Order reveals righteousness

Alwenna believes this completely.

She does not care about politics.

She cares about **correctness**.

If the Warden acts lawfully, she will defend him against anyone.

Duke.

Priest.

General.

Doesn’t matter.

If he does not act lawfully?

She will tell him so.

Immediately.

---

# Why the King Sent Her

Officially she is assigned to assist **Thalrik, Warden of the King’s Law.**

Unofficially, the king knows exactly what he’s doing.

Thalrik is:

* brilliant
* decisive
* extremely effective
* occasionally inclined to solve problems with artillery

The king therefore provided him with:

1. A walking legal authority
2. A temple witness of Pholtus
3. Someone who can say *“Yes, this is lawful”* before the cannon fires

Or occasionally…

“No, Warden. That is technically treason.”

---

# The King’s Joke

At the ceremony the king said:

“Every Warden requires a clerk.”

Alwenna corrected him immediately.

“I am a Truthseeker, Your Majesty.”

The king smiled.

“Of course you are.”

Thalrik will later realize the king has handed him **a walking statute book with a holy symbol.**

---

# Her Legal Superpower

### The Lantern Mind

Alwenna remembers **structure**, not just facts.

While others argue she is silently building:

* timelines
* chains of authority
* contradictions between witnesses
* jurisdictional limits

She does not interrogate emotionally.

She constructs **legal traps for lies.**

Example moment:

A suspect contradicts himself several times.

The crew ignores it.

Alwenna closes her notebook.

“You previously stated you were at the harbor.”

A pause.

“The dockmaster testified you left the district two hours earlier.”

Another pause.

“You cannot have been in both places.”

She adjusts her glasses.

“Which statement would you like to amend?”

The crew realizes:

**She does not hunt criminals.
She traps lies.**

---

# Her Relationship with Thalrik

She addresses him always as:

“Warden.”

Not Admiral.

Not Captain.

Not Sir.

Because in her mind the title granted by the king carries **legal authority over all others.**

She studies him with academic fascination.

Thalrik represents **the living instrument of royal justice**, which is exactly the sort of thing Truthseekers write treatises about.

Sometimes he will notice her taking notes while he argues with someone.

If asked why:

“I am documenting precedent.”

---

# How the Crew First Sees Her

Initial impression:

A fragile temple scholar.

Tiny. Polite. Bookish.

Second impression:

Oh no.

She remembers everything.

If someone contradicts themselves days later she will calmly say:

“You previously stated the opposite on the twelfth.”

---

# When the Crew Starts Respecting Her

### Incident One

A minor noble attempts to bully her.

“You will address me properly, girl.”

She adjusts her glasses and recites the royal decree empowering the Warden.

Word for word.

Then calmly concludes:

“Your refusal to comply constitutes obstruction of royal authority.”

A pause.

“That offense carries imprisonment.”

The noble backs down.

The crew notices.

---

### Incident Two

During an arrest someone threatens her physically.

The crew reacts instantly.

Not because she needs protection.

But because harming her would mean **attacking the authority of the Crown itself.**

She becomes something like:

* the crew’s legal shield
* their walking arrest warrant
* their tiny terrifying mascot

---

# The Moment She Proves Her Worth

A powerful noble refuses arrest and claims Thalrik lacks jurisdiction.

Political standoff.

Everyone watching.

Thalrik is about two seconds from solving the problem with dwarven directness.

Then Alwenna quietly steps forward.

She opens her satchel.

Three documents appear.

One by one.

“Royal Charter establishing the office of Warden of the King’s Law.”

Second.

“Delegation of investigative authority.”

Third.

“A sealed writ signed by the king.”

She turns the document toward the noble.

“Your arrest is lawful.”

Silence.

She then looks at Thalrik.

“Warden.”

A pause.

“You may proceed.”

That is the moment Thalrik realizes something.

The king did not give him a clerk.

He gave him **legal artillery.**

---

# Her Hidden Arc

Alwenna slowly begins to see Thalrik as more than a commander.

To her he becomes something dangerous:

**The embodiment of lawful justice in the realm.**

If he proves worthy of that trust, she becomes fiercely loyal.

If he betrays that ideal?

She will oppose him.

Without hesitation.

Because to Alwenna:

**The Law outranks everyone.**

Even the Warden.

---

# The King’s Real Move

By assigning her, the king gave Thalrik three things:

1. A **legal shield**
2. A **legitimacy engine**
3. A **moral compass**

But also something else.

A Truthseeker of Pholtus.

Which means every action the Warden takes now has a **witness of lawful truth.**

Not a spy.

A witness.

---

# Her First Words to Thalrik

She boards his ship, bows politely, and says:

“Warden.”

A pause.

“I have been assigned to assist you in the enforcement of the King’s Law.”

She opens her notebook.

“What are we arresting first?”

---

# Her Running Joke with the Crew

Someone eventually asks:

“How many laws do you actually know?”

She considers the question carefully.

Then answers:

“All of them.”

She is not joking.

---

# Her Most Dangerous Line

When the crew debates doing something questionable she will look up calmly and say:

“Warden.”

A pause.

“That is technically illegal.”

Another pause.

“But it can be made legal.”
