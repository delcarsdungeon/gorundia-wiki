# Sir Mathren Solgard

> [!quote] *“You ever notice how the Church sends two kinds of men?*  
> *Ones that make you feel better…*  
> *and ones that make you tell the truth.”*  
> 
> *“…Solgard’s the second kind.”* - Sorek Redmarch
> 

---

## Of the Man

Sir Mathren Solgard is a knight of the Dawnfather not made for ceremony, but for judgment.

He bears the titles:

- **Dawn Marshal of the Third Radiant Inquiry**  
- **Warden of False Flame**  
- **Keeper of the Black Threshold**

These are not honors given for valor alone. They are burdens carried by those who are sent when faith has begun to rot at the edges.

He is known in several dioceses as a man who arrives after whispers have already become problems—and leaves with fewer whispers.

---

## His Charge

Solgard’s duty is not to preach.

It is to **verify**.

He is sent to determine:

- whether a miracle is true or false  
- whether a preacher serves the light or themselves  
- whether a priest has bent doctrine—or broken it  
- whether something wearing holiness is, in fact, a lie  

He does not concern himself with comfort.

Only with truth.

---

## His Bearing

Those who have stood before him remark on the same thing:

He does not loom.

He **settles**.

Like stone.

His armor is not bright. It is worn, repaired, traveled. The sunburst upon it is worked into the steel, not displayed upon it.

He does not glitter.

He endures.

His face bears scars not as trophies, but as facts. His eyes are steady, measuring, and without hurry.

He wastes nothing—not words, not motion, not attention.

---

## His Temperament

Solgard is not cruel.

Nor is he kind.

He is **precise**.

He does not punish weakness.  
He does not excuse deceit.

He will listen longer than most expect.

He will judge faster than most prefer.

He does not raise his voice to command a room. He simply speaks—and the room arranges itself around that.

He does not drink while on duty.

He is almost always on duty.

---

## The Inquiry at the Blood and Sand

Sir Mathren came to [[Bard’s Gate]] on quiet authority, bearing concerns regarding [[Lyosha Ohotnik]] and reports of an unordained preacher working among [[The Dregs]].

He found the latter in the common room of the [[Blood and Sand]].

The room had prepared for violence.

He did not.

He entered, observed, and began.

The woman—[[Druuna Sunseeker]]—was questioned on doctrine, authority, and intent.

She failed, by formal measure, in several respects:

- she could not recite doctrine cleanly  
- she held no formal ordination  
- she lacked proper instruction  

Yet she did not claim what was not hers.

She did not seek coin.

She did not bind others to her.

She fed the hungry, tended wounds, discouraged vengeance, and spoke Pelor’s name without twisting it.

Solgard listened.

Considered.

And rendered judgment:

> *“You are imperfect… but you are not false.”*

He then declared before witnesses:

> **“Druuna Sunseeker is no heretic.”**

He ordered instruction.

He offered protection.

And he made it quietly understood that harm done to her under Pelor’s light would not go unanswered.

---

## On Lyosha Ohotnik

Though Solgard did not confront [[Lyosha Ohotnik]] directly, he examined the man through records, testimony, and temple accounts.

His conclusion was recorded simply:

Lyosha Ohotnik stands within doctrine.

No hidden corruption.  
No deviation requiring censure.

Severity, yes.

Corruption, no.

For a name such as Ohotnik, that judgment carries weight.

---

## On Porthos Ryomar

It is recorded that this same knight was later called upon to confirm the matter of [[Porthos Ryomar]].

That alone speaks to his standing.

When crown and church both require certainty—**not opinion, not politics, but certainty**—they send Solgard.

And they accept what he says.

Whether they like it or not.

---

## Sayings Attributed to Him

> “I hunt vampires. I burn false prophets. I do not tolerate charlatans.”

> “Truth does not require polish.”

> “The Dawnfather’s light is not diminished by imperfect vessels.”

---

## Final Measure

There are brighter paladins.

There are kinder priests.

There are louder men.

Sir Mathren Solgard is none of these.

He is what remains when faith is stripped of comfort, pride, and ornament—and tested against what is real.

And in such matters, he is very difficult to argue with.

---

> *If he approves you… you are now everyone else’s problem.”*
