## Appearance

Dame Cwenheld is a towering knight whose body bears the scars of terrible fire.

Her skin is burned across much of her frame, and her eyes—damaged in battle—remain watery and half-blind. Her grey hair is usually hidden beneath a knightly helm fitted with a wig, worn not from shame but to avoid frightening children.

Despite her injuries she still carries herself like the war-leader she once was.

---

## Faith

Cwenheld is a deeply devoted servant of **Heironeous**.

Unlike many priests she expresses her faith through action rather than ceremony. Her belief is simple:

> *Stand between the innocent and the blade.*

---

## The Company of the Red Hills

In her youth Cwenheld traveled as part of a small adventuring company with:

- **[[Avelyne Marsten]]
- **[[Galmyr Vogel]]**
- **[[Theravain the Ashkeeper]]**, an elven wizard and mentor to the group

Their greatest battle came **31 years ago** against the red dragon **Ashmaw of the Red Hills**.

The fight took place among the ruins of an ancient fortress where the dragon intended to make its lair.

During the battle:

- Theravain sacrificed himself in a catastrophic duel of elemental fire.
- Cwenheld was nearly killed holding the dragon’s breath long enough for her companions to escape.
- The dragon was slain at great cost.

The victory ended the adventuring company forever.

---

## Legacy

Cwenheld is remembered as one of the great knights of her generation.

Among soldiers and temple knights her name is spoken with deep respect.

Her own words about the battle are still quoted:

> **“Theravain was a brother to us all. I still hear his voice in the wind, whispering warnings and old jokes.”**

---

## Personality

Cwenheld is direct, stubborn, and impossible to intimidate.

She has little patience for noble politics but immense respect for courage and honor.

Those who know her well say that despite her stern demeanor she carries a deep compassion for the weak and the young.

---

## Reputation

Among knights of Gorundia it is commonly said: 

*“If Cwenheld is standing beside you, the line will not break.”*

## History

# The Venerated Shield of Heironeous

Among the militant orders of Heironeous there exists an honor rarely bestowed upon a living knight.

**The Venerated Shield.**

It is not a rank.  
It is not a command.

It is a **symbol**.

A knight who bears that title represents the idea that the faithful must stand between danger and the innocent—no matter the cost.

Dame Cwenheld of Marsten carries that honor.

---

# Legendary Deeds of Dame Cwenheld

# The Battle of Ashfall Ruins

_31 years ago – Year 1199 OR_

For several seasons the hill country west of Marsten had been plagued by a red dragon seeking a permanent lair.

The creature came to be known as **Ashmaw of the Red Hills**.

She was first sighted circling the broken towers of an ancient ruin in the uplands—an old fortress from a forgotten war whose shattered halls and volcanic stone would have made a perfect lair.

Farmsteads burned.  
Caravans vanished.  
Three villages were abandoned within a month.

It was then that a small band of adventurers answered the call.

They were:

**Dame Cwenheld Thralen** – knight of Heironeous  
**Avelyne Marsten** – noble adventurer and warrior  
**Galmyr Vogel** – rising knight of House Vogel  
**Theravain the Ashkeeper** – elven wizard and mentor to the company

Together they climbed the Red Hills and confronted the dragon in the ruins of the old fortress.

---

## The Fight

The battle began before the party even reached the courtyard.

Ashmaw descended from the clouds like a falling furnace.

The first breath of flame shattered the western tower and turned half the ruin into a river of molten stone.

Cwenheld charged through the fire to meet the dragon head-on, shield raised and blade drawn.

Avelyne fought beside her, driving the beast away from the crumbling stair where villagers had taken refuge.

Galmyr struck from the flanks, cutting at the dragon’s wings and forcing it to remain grounded among the ruins rather than taking to the air again.

But it was **Theravain** who changed the battle.

---

# Theravain’s Last Stand

Theravain the Ashkeeper had spent centuries mastering the forces of elemental fire.

Where most wizards feared flame, he **understood it**.

When Ashmaw unleashed her second breath the elf stepped forward and raised his staff of volcanic glass.

Witnesses later said the air itself **split open with heat**.

Dragonfire and wizardfire collided in the courtyard like two storms meeting.

For a moment the dragon’s inferno halted in midair, held in place by Theravain’s spell.

Then the wizard answered with fire of his own.

A column of blazing elemental force erupted from his staff and struck Ashmaw full in the chest.

The explosion shattered the courtyard.

Stone melted.  
Columns collapsed.

The dragon screamed as its wing membrane tore and its scales blackened.

But no mortal wizard could stand against the breath of an adult red forever.

The backlash of the spell burned Theravain alive where he stood.

When the flames cleared only scorched stone and the shattered staff remained.

---

# The Wounding of Cwenheld

The dragon, wounded but furious, lashed out in rage.

Galmyr Vogel was caught beneath falling stone when the courtyard wall collapsed.

Ashmaw turned to finish him.

Cwenheld threw herself between them.

The dragon’s breath engulfed the knight at point-blank range.

Her armor glowed white.

Her shield melted.

Even as the flames consumed her she refused to yield ground.

That moment gave Avelyne time to drag Galmyr clear of the rubble.

When the fire finally died Cwenheld still stood.

Burned.  
Half blind.  
But alive.

Galmyr and Avelyne rallied and drove their blades into the dragon’s already shattered wing.

Unable to fly, gravely wounded, Ashmaw fell.

---

# The End of the Company

The victory came at terrible cost.

Theravain the Ashkeeper was dead.

Cwenheld was permanently scarred and partially blinded.

The old adventuring company never rode together again.

In the days that followed the survivors buried their friend among the shattered stones of the ruin.

It is said the wind through the broken towers still sounds like distant laughter—  
and some swear it carries the voice of the old elven wizard.

---

# What Came After

The battle changed them all.

Cwenheld devoted herself entirely to the service of Heironeous.

Galmyr Vogel returned to his family lands and began the political ascent that would eventually reshape House Vogel.

And in the long shadow of that ruin…

**Avelyne Marsten and Galmyr Vogel became lovers.**

A year later their son was born.

His name was **Beringer**.

## The Bridge of Wycliffe Ford

Twenty-five years ago a bandit host attempted to seize the stone bridge at Wycliffe Ford during a spring caravan crossing.

Merchants and their families were trapped halfway across the bridge when the bandits charged.

Cwenheld rode alone into the mouth of the bridge and dismounted.

She planted her shield and held the crossing long enough for the caravan to retreat behind the town militia.

Witnesses claim she fought **six men at once** and refused to retreat even after taking two crossbow bolts.

When asked afterward why she did not withdraw she reportedly answered:

> “There were children behind me.”

---

## The Chapel of Broken Spears

During a border skirmish with raiders from the western hills, a small shrine of Heironeous was overrun.

The temple novices barricaded themselves inside.

Cwenheld and only three temple knights reached the chapel in time.

The defenders expected a siege.

Instead Cwenheld kicked open the doors and **charged directly into the raiders**, forcing them to fight her inside the narrow entry hall.

The raiders fled after losing their leader.

The chapel survived.

The novices later claimed they saw a faint blue light around her shield during the fight.

---

## The Night of Falling Ash

A caravan train carrying refugees was caught in a volcanic ash storm while passing through the Red Hills.

Visibility dropped to nothing.

Bandits attempted to attack the column under cover of darkness and choking ash.

Cwenheld reportedly rode the entire length of the caravan shouting directions and rallying the guards while guiding refugees to shelter.

Many survivors later said they followed **the sound of her voice** because they could no longer see the road.

---

## The Last Charge of Marrow Field

A marauding ogre pack broke through a militia line and began cutting down villagers fleeing toward a walled farmstead.

Cwenheld ordered the militia to hold position while she rode alone toward the ogres.

When asked later why she attacked alone she replied simply:

> “They were chasing the children.”

The charge delayed the monsters long enough for the villagers to reach the gates.

---

# The Sad Truth

Age and the dragonfire of Ashmaw left their mark.

Without the blessings of Heironeous flowing through her armor and blade:

- Her legs are slow and stiff.    
- Her sight is permanently damaged.    
- Pain is a constant companion.    

In quiet halls some cynical aspirants whisper:

> “The Venerated Shield smashes down walls because she cannot see the door.”

Such words are rarely spoken where senior knights can hear them.

---

# Why She Still Matters

To the Church, Cwenheld represents something important.

Not perfection.

**Endurance.**

She is living proof that faith is not about glory or victory.

It is about standing between danger and the helpless **even when you are broken**.

That is why the Church keeps her visible.

Why squires still train beneath her banner.

Why knights salute when she enters a hall.

And why the title **Venerated Shield of Heironeous** still carries weight.

---

# The Truth Few Speak

When Cwenheld dons her armor and the divine strength of Heironeous flows through her…

The years fall away.

The limp fades.

The old fire returns.

And for a few moments the knight who faced **Ashmaw of the Red Hills** still lives.
