# Rowena Caervane

Born: 1096 OR  
Died: 1138 OR

Rowena Caervane was the **first wife of [[Theron Sylvanranth]]** and the mother of **[[Dana Freeblade]]**.

She was a noblewoman of House Caervane, remembered in surviving accounts as intelligent, politically astute, and far more comfortable navigating court society than her husband.

[[Theron Sylvanranth]] was known instead for relentless focus and cold strategic thinking — a work-obsessed administrator who ruled by sheer force of will and rarely concerned himself with reputation or popularity.

Rowena understood the man behind that discipline better than most.

Where Theron often ruled by force of will and reputation, Rowena preferred subtlety and patience. Many contemporaries believed she tempered the worst of his impulses.

For a time, the marriage was considered one of the more stable political unions among the nobility.

That stability ended abruptly in **1138 OR**.

---

# The Assassination

Rowena Caervane was murdered in **1138 OR** in an attack widely believed to have targeted **Theron Sylvanranth**.

The killers struck while Theron was absent from the estate.

Rowena died in his place.

Evidence later suggested the conspiracy involved multiple layers:

- hired mercenaries    
- paid couriers    
- discreet financiers    
- and at least one noble patron    

The attack was intended to eliminate Theron and fracture the influence he was beginning to build among the frontier houses.

Instead, it ignited one of the most ruthless revenge campaigns in regional memory.

# Dana’s Memory

[[Dana Freeblade]] was still a child when her mother died.

Her memories of Rowena herself are fragmented — scattered impressions of warmth, intelligence, and a presence that once balanced her father’s stormy nature.

What Dana remembers clearly is the silence that followed.

Not grief.

Not rage.

But the cold, systematic vengeance that unfolded afterward.

For Dana, the Reckoning was the first time she saw what her father truly was capable of becoming.

---

# Legacy

Rowena Caervane’s death altered the course of several lives.

It hardened **Theron Sylvanranth**, pushing him toward the ruthless pragmatism that would later define his reputation.

It shaped the upbringing of **Dana Freeblade**, whose own disciplined brutality often echoes the lessons of that time.

And it left behind a cautionary tale that still circulates in noble courts and criminal circles alike:

> Killing a Sylvanranth might be possible.
> 
> Missing… is far worse.
