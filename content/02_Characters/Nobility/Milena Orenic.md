# Milena Orenic

_Storm-lynx of House Orenic. Builder of frameworks. Frequently mistaken for a siege engine._

---
## Overview

**Milena Orenic** is a niece of **[[Duke Vladek Orenic]]** and a member of the extended Orenic household. Known for her formidable physical presence and unexpectedly gentle manner, she occupies a curious place within Gorundian society: both a respected noblewoman and a figure many courtiers approach with cautious curiosity.

Among those familiar with the Orenic domains, she is often described simply as **“infrastructure.”**

The remark originates with her uncle, who delivered the assessment approvingly during a breakfast meeting with **[[Beringer]] Amadis**, while explaining why he had briefly considered marrying her to **[[Bertram Moffet]]**.

---

## Appearance

![[Milena with Uncle Vladek.png|340]]![[Milena Orenic.png|340]]![[Milena and Cornelia.png|680]]

Milena stands **approximately 6’4”**, with the powerful frame common to the Orenic line. Broad shoulders, strong arms, and an easy physical confidence give the impression of someone built more for campaigning than for court.

Despite this, she dresses and carries herself with careful grace. Those encountering her for the first time often experience a moment of mild confusion: a woman built like a cavalry captain moving with the quiet manners of a court lady.

Observers frequently note that she is **fully aware of the effect she has on people**, and deliberately chooses when to lean into it.

In practical settings she has been known to:

- Carry sacks of grain on one shoulder    
- Move furniture without assistance    
- Lift animals that most people would rather not lift    

Her uncle summarizes the matter succinctly.

> [!quote] **[[Duke Vladek Orenic]]**
> 
> “Strong back. Good legs.  
> If winter bad? She wrestles it.”

---
## Personality

Milena is generally described as:

- **Patient**    
- **Controlled**    
- **Observant**    
- **Dryly humorous**    

Unlike some members of House Orenic, she is not especially loud or confrontational. Instead she tends to speak calmly and directly, often in short, thoughtful statements.

Those who mistake this for passivity usually correct the misunderstanding quickly.

Milena possesses a quiet strategic mind and reads extensively, including technical works and obscure literature. She has demonstrated particular interest in dense gnomish texts that blend legal frameworks, inheritance disputes, and romance narratives.

In conversation she has occasionally summarized her outlook simply:

> [!quote] Milena Orenic
> 
> “Arranged marriages are frameworks.  
> Inside framework, one may still build.”

---
## Family Background

Milena belongs to the extended network of **House Orenic**, a lineage known for blunt politics and resilient frontier culture.

Within Orenic lands, competence is valued above delicacy, and physical strength is not considered an unusual virtue in a noblewoman.

Her uncle frequently speaks about her with a mixture of pride and practical approval.

> [!quote] **[[Duke Vladek Orenic]]**
> 
> “This one?”
> 
> He thumps the table once.
> 
> “Infrastructure.”
> 
> “Strong back. Good legs. Can lift sack of grain and husband at same time.”
> 
> A satisfied shrug.
> 
> “Marriage is infrastructure.”

---
## The Proposed Marriage to Bertram Moffet

At one point **[[Duke Vladek Orenic]]** attempted to arrange a marriage between Milena and **[[Bertram Moffet]]**, the knight known for publicly humiliating **Ponzio Bordella** and later defying **Wilhelm Vogel**.

The duke’s reasoning was characteristically practical:

- Bertram possessed **reputation and courage**    
- Milena possessed **strength and stability**    
- The resulting grandchildren would, in his words, **“break furniture”**    

Milena herself treated the proposal with calm courtesy. When speaking with **[[Sir Beringer Amadis]]**, she simply asked when she should expect Bertram to call upon her.

Events ultimately took a different course.

---
## Engagement to Alistair the Younger

Instead of marrying Bertram, Milena formed a close attachment to **[[Alistair the Younger]]**, son of **[[Sir Beringer Amadis]]**.

The relationship appears to have developed quickly during a series of gatherings hosted by the Orenic household.

Observers generally agree that the pairing works surprisingly well.

Alistair, who frequently describes Milena as **“very delicate,”** seems entirely unaware that she could likely lift him with one arm.

Milena appears quietly amused by this.

---
## The Concordance of Embers

Milena’s most visible appearance in court society occurred during the **Concordance of Embers**, a gathering where young nobles publicly declared romantic or political intentions.

During the event she engaged in a brief but memorable exchange with **[[Cornelia Bordella]]**, newly married to **[[Bertram Moffet]]**.

Milena opened with a dry observation about Cornelia’s footwear.

> [!quote] Milena Orenic
> 
> “Those are impressive.  
> I considered something similar.”
> 
> A thoughtful pause.
> 
> “But I decided against deploying siege engines indoors.”

Cornelia replied smoothly:

> [!quote] **[[Cornelia Bordella]]**
> 
> “I like to see my husband clearly.  
> It helps when giving instructions.”

Milena considered this for a moment before nodding once.

> [!quote] Milena Orenic
> 
> “Clarity is important in any campaign.”

Observers later described the interaction not as hostile, but as **two intelligent people realizing the other was not foolish**.

## Common Misconceptions

### “She must be intimidating.”

Milena is generally polite, soft-spoken, and careful in conversation.

This sometimes produces the opposite of the intended effect.

Several courtiers have reported feeling **more** intimidated after realizing that her calm demeanor is entirely genuine.

---

### “She is probably simple.”

Her physical presence leads some observers to assume she is unsophisticated.

In reality she reads widely, including complex legal texts and obscure gnomish literature.

One court observer remarked privately:

> “She reads the sort of books that argue with themselves.”

---
### “She is a political pawn of House Orenic.”

Milena is fully aware that marriage alliances in noble houses often serve political purposes.

Rather than resisting this, she treats such arrangements as **structures within which personal agency still operates**.

---
### “She must resent Cornelia Bordella.”

Some courtiers expected a rivalry following the Concordance of Embers.

Instead the interaction between Milena and **[[Cornelia Bordella]]** suggested mutual recognition rather than hostility.

One knight described the moment as:

> “Two generals quietly checking each other’s maps.”

---
### “She is delicate.”

This misconception originates almost entirely from **[[Alistair the Younger]]**.

Evidence remains inconclusive.

---
## Things Milena Has Accidentally Broken

Despite her careful manners, Milena occasionally forgets that most furniture and equipment are not designed with the Orenic physique in mind.

These incidents are rarely dramatic, but they have become a quiet running joke among those who know her.

---
### A Practice Spear

During a training exercise with household guards, Milena reportedly snapped a practice spear while attempting what witnesses described as _“a completely reasonable parry.”_

The guard involved later explained:

> [!quote] Household Guard, Orenic Retinue  
> “Spear did not fail because of bad technique.”
> 
> “Spear failed because it met Milena.”

Milena apologized and paid for a replacement.

Two replacements, in fact.

---
### A Ballroom Chair

At a winter gathering in the Orenic court, a chair beneath Milena reportedly gave way during a long evening of conversation.

Milena stood up calmly, examined the broken chair for a moment, and then moved to another seat.

Witnesses recall that she later sent the host a note of apology along with a gift of **three much sturdier chairs**.

---
### A Training Dummy

One of the castle training dummies reportedly split open during a particularly enthusiastic practice session.

Milena insisted the equipment must have already been damaged.

The weapons master reportedly disagreed.

> [!quote] Castle Weapons Master  
> “Dummy lasted twelve years.”
> 
> “Then Milena arrived.”

---
### A Door

During a visit to a rural manor house, Milena attempted to assist with moving furniture and accidentally pulled a door from its hinges.

She reportedly paused for a moment, holding the door in both hands, before asking politely where the hinges were kept.

> [!quote] Milena Orenic  
> “I may have misunderstood the strength required.”

---
### One Young Nobleman’s Confidence

A young courtier once attempted to boast about his strength in Milena’s presence.

Milena listened politely, then lifted a heavy chest he had been struggling with and carried it across the room without comment.

The nobleman has reportedly been **much quieter** in subsequent conversations.

---
## What Soldiers Say About Her

Among soldiers and household guards, Milena’s reputation is notably different from the one circulating in court.

Where courtiers see a curiosity, soldiers tend to see something simpler: **a person who understands practical work and does not pretend otherwise.**

Many report that she listens carefully when speaking with guards, stablehands, and laborers, and rarely treats such conversations as beneath her.

One veteran sergeant summarized the matter in blunt terms.

> [!quote] Orenic Household Sergeant  
> “Lady Milena?”
> 
> “Good sort.”
> 
> “Carries her own gear. Doesn’t complain.”
> 
> A small shrug.
> 
> “Built like a gatehouse, too.”

---

Some guards claim she occasionally joins training exercises simply to stay active.

When asked why a noblewoman would bother doing so, she reportedly answered:

> [!quote] Milena Orenic  
> “If people must stand between me and danger, I should understand what they are asked to do.”

---

A younger guard once attempted to apologize after losing a sparring match to her.

Milena reportedly waved the concern away.

> [!quote] Milena Orenic  
> “You did not lose.”
> 
> “You discovered a limit.”
> 
> A thoughtful pause.
> 
> “Limits are useful things to know.”

---

Perhaps the most common remark among soldiers, however, is much simpler.

> [!quote] Castle Guard, overheard in the barracks  
> “If she ever decides to join the shield wall…”
> 
> “…I’m standing next to her.”

---
