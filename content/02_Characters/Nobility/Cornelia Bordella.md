# Cornelia Bordella
*"A pawn who learned the board." — Sorek Redmarch*

---
## Overview

Cornelia Bordella is the younger sister of [[Ponzio Bordella]] and daughter of the powerful Bordella family of Blackvale. Once known primarily as a scandalous noblewoman caught in a doomed romance, she has emerged as a far more complicated figure — observant, patient, and increasingly influential in the quiet politics of Bard’s Gate.

Cornelia’s life has been defined by betrayal, exile, and survival among powerful men who believed they controlled her fate. What they did not anticipate was that the years spent in cloister and disgrace would forge a woman capable of navigating intrigue with calm precision.

To some she remains a romantic fool.  
To others she is something much more dangerous.

---
## Appearance

![[Cornelia.png]]

Cornelia is striking rather than delicate.

Cornelia stands approximately **5'9"**, tall enough to command attention in most courts — though not quite tall enough to match the imposing presence of **[[Milena Orenic]]**, a fact the court noticed immediately at the Embermeet ball.

Long red hair, intelligent eyes, and a composed bearing give her the presence of someone who listens more than she speaks. Her beauty is well known in courtly circles, though it carries an air of restraint rather than flamboyance.

She dresses simply compared to many noblewomen, favoring tasteful gowns over extravagant displays.

Those who underestimate her based on this restraint tend to regret it.

---

## Early Life

Cornelia grew up within the wealth and ambition of House Bordella, raised among the expectations of nobility and the ruthless politics of Gorundian high society.

From an early age she formed a deep bond with [[Bertram]]. Their affection grew quietly over years of shared youth and secret hopes of marriage.

The match was politically inconvenient.

Her supposed 'father' Lavinio, brother [[Ponzio Bordella]] and other powerful figures within the duchy arranged instead for Cornelia to be promised to [[Wilhelm Vogel]], a union meant to strengthen alliances and elevate Bordella prestige.

The decision shattered both Cornelia and Bertram.

---

## The Night of Defiance

On the eve of her arranged future, Cornelia chose defiance.

She spent one night with Bertram — an act of genuine love but also a scandal powerful enough to disrupt the carefully arranged political marriage.

The consequences were immediate and brutal.

Bertram was disgraced and nearly destroyed socially.  
Cornelia was removed from court and sent away in shame.

The marriage to Vogel collapsed.

The scandal became one of the quiet tragedies whispered about among the nobility.

---
## Exile to the Abbey

Cornelia was sent to a cloistered religious house — the [[Abbey of the Celestial Veil]] — where troublesome daughters of noble houses were expected to disappear quietly from the political stage.

The abbey lay far from court, surrounded by broad fields, orchards, and carefully tended gardens dedicated to the **Velaeri** — the seasonal gods Atroa, Sotillion, Wenta, and Telchur — whose clergy oversaw the sacred rhythm of the agricultural year. Life there followed that same rhythm: planting, tending, harvest, prayer.

Silence was common. Reflection unavoidable.

For many young noblewomen, the abbey was meant to be a lesson in humility.

For Cornelia, it became something else.

Within its quiet walls she encountered scholars, visiting clergy, and travelers who passed through the abbey’s guest halls. Their conversations ranged far beyond court gossip. She read widely — theology, philosophy, history, and political treatises — and began to understand how power truly moves beneath the surface of noble society.

The sisters of the abbey hoped to teach discipline.

They succeeded.

More importantly, Cornelia learned patience.

The woman who eventually left the abbey was not the same naive girl who had entered it.

She returned to the world quieter, sharper, and far more observant.

But she still hated gardening.

Digging in the dirt, in her experience, was often the fastest way to discover what people had tried to bury.

---

## Return to Society

Cornelia eventually returned to society under more controlled circumstances, still marked by scandal but no longer politically useful as a marriage pawn.

This change ironically granted her a degree of freedom rare for a noblewoman.

She began to move through social circles with quiet intelligence, listening more than speaking, observing alliances, rivalries, and ambitions.

Many dismissed her.

Some did not.

---

## Relationship with Bertram

Despite everything, the bond between Cornelia and [[Bertram Moffet|Bertram]] never truly vanished.

Years of distance, scandal, and political maneuvering had pushed them onto separate paths, yet whenever their lives intersected again the old connection remained impossible to ignore.

In the end it was Cornelia who forced the matter.

During the chaotic evening when **Duke Wilhelm Vogel was seized at the royal ball**, when the entire court seemed to teeter between revelation and panic, Cornelia simply decided she had waited long enough.

Before anyone had time to reconsider — least of all Bertram — she insisted they marry immediately.

[[Lyosha Ohotnik]] of Pelor performed the ceremony on the spot.

The king himself, already presiding over the unfolding crisis, gave his blessing to the union before the assembled court.

The moment felt less like a wedding and more like a dam breaking.

Years of uncertainty, hesitation, and unspoken fears simply washed away.

Cornelia had long suspected that if she waited for Bertram to decide the matter, he might never take the leap.

So she made it for both of them.

Their marriage now exists in the strange intersection of affection, politics, and survival. Both understand the world around them has grown far more dangerous than the romantic dreams of their youth.

Yet whatever storms may come, the bond between them is no longer uncertain.

It has already been tested in front of the entire court.

And like many things born in moments of upheaval, it now carries truths neither of them has yet fully spoken aloud.

## The Saber “Lenore”

Bertram once presented Cornelia with a saber named **Lenore**, a symbolic gift representing both affection and trust.

The weapon is elegant and well-balanced, more ceremonial than practical at first glance.

To outsiders it appears merely a sentimental relic.

The weapon was Bertram’s personal blade for years, and when he gave it to Cornelia he did so for a simple reason: he intended it to keep her alive.

When Bertram placed the saber in her hands, he shrugged as if the decision were obvious.

>[!quote] “If anyone’s going to stab my wife, they’ll have to get through my sword first.” - Knight-Marshal Bertram Moffet

## Political Awareness

Cornelia is far more perceptive than most nobles realize.

Years spent in quiet exile taught her how fragile reputations, alliances, and power itself can be. Long before she possessed any formal authority, she developed a keen understanding of how influence actually moves through noble society — often far from the seats where decisions are supposedly made.

Those who pay attention have noticed that Cornelia rarely pushes openly. Instead she observes, waits, and nudges conversations at precisely the moment when people believe the conclusion was their own idea.

Now, as **Countess of Blackvale**, she finally possesses the authority that once eluded her.

But the instincts that guide her were sharpened long before the title ever rested on her shoulders.

Some at court have begun to suspect that the crown did not simply elevate a convenient heir.

It may have elevated someone who already understood the game better than most of its players.

## Possible Religious Sympathies

Some whispers suggest Cornelia has attracted followers of **Johydee**, the trickster goddess associated with deception, masks, and subversion of authority.

It is unclear whether Cornelia actively encourages this connection or whether her circumstances merely resonate with the trickster faith’s philosophy.

The ambiguity itself may be intentional.

[Archivist Note: Followers of Johydee are drawn to figures who upset established power structures through cleverness rather than force.]

## Relationship with Ponzio Bordella

Cornelia’s relationship with her brother [[Ponzio Bordella]] is complicated.

Publicly she remains loyal to the family.

Privately their relationship carries deep scars from the events surrounding her forced betrothal and exile.

Whether Cornelia intends reconciliation, revenge, or something subtler remains unclear.

---
## Current Position

Cornelia now holds the title **Countess of Blackvale**, though her time is divided between several centers of influence.

She maintains quarters in **[[Karelthun]]**, granted as part of her elevation to the peerage, allowing her regular presence at the royal court. She is also under the personal protection of **[[Braga Kharne]]**, Warden of the West — a promise made in the informal but ironbound fashion of frontier nobles during a late and very drunken night at **[[The Griffon’s Roost]]**, Beringer’s bar in the lower bailey of Stormarrow.

Braga swore the oath publicly before half the tavern, sword in hand.  
**[[Orin Kharne]]** was present when the promise was made and did not dispute it — which, to anyone who knows the Wardens of the West, was the moment the oath truly became binding.

At the same time, Cornelia retains a quiet network of acquaintances and sympathizers within **[[Bard’s Gate]]**, where her family name once carried considerable influence.

As a result, her movements now form a quiet triangle of influence between **Blackvale**, **Karelthun**, and **Bard’s Gate**.

Her presence at court gatherings, masquerades, and diplomatic functions has become increasingly notable.

Many nobles still see her as a curiosity — a scandal that somehow survived.

Others have begun to suspect that **Countess Cornelia Bordella** is quietly weaving herself into the wider web of Gorundian politics.

And that she may have begun doing so long before anyone thought to watch her.

## Personality

Cornelia is thoughtful, observant, and rarely speaks without purpose.

She values loyalty deeply but no longer mistakes sentiment for strength.

Her greatest skill is patience.

Those who rush to act often find she has already considered the outcome.

---
## Temperament

Cornelia presents a calm and polished exterior, but those who know her well recognize a sharper edge beneath the refinement.

She is patient, observant, and unusually skilled at reading social dynamics — a habit formed during her years of enforced exile.

Yet she is not immune to vanity, nor entirely free of jealousy. Cornelia values loyalty intensely and reacts poorly to surprises, particularly where matters of affection or trust are concerned.

She prefers to understand the board before the pieces move.

Being caught unaware is the one position she refuses to occupy twice.

## Narrative Role

Cornelia stands at a crossroads of multiple political currents within Gorundia.

Depending on circumstances she may become:

- A stabilizing influence among Bard’s Gate nobles  
- A political ally to emerging factions  
- A symbol for movements challenging the traditional order  
- A catalyst for further intrigue within the duchy

Her story is not yet finished.

## Vignette — _The Matter of Milena_

During the **[[Embermeet Concordance]]**, one of the more discussed interactions of the evening occurred quietly near the central braziers.

Earlier in the season, **[[Vladek Orenic]]** had briefly attempted to arrange a match between **[[Bertram Moffet]]** and his formidable niece **[[Milena Orenic]]**, a woman whose physical presence alone had already become the subject of half the court’s speculation. The arrangement dissolved quickly once Milena and **[[Amadis the Younger]]** developed a clear mutual interest.

Even so, the situation left a certain tension in the air.

Cornelia, newly married to Bertram and not entirely immune to jealousy, spent part of the evening quietly determining whether the earlier proposal had involved any… indiscretions.

Observers noted that the conversation between the two women was polite, composed, and almost entirely inscrutable.

Even with the added height of her formal heels, **Cornelia still had to tilt her head to meet Milena’s eyes.**, whose presence was difficult to ignore even in a hall full of armored nobility.

Milena’s gaze briefly dropped to Cornelia’s footwear.

> “Those are impressive,” Milena remarked calmly.  
> “I had considered something similar.  
> But I decided against deploying siege engines indoors.”

Cornelia did not hesitate.

She glanced toward Bertram across the hall, then back.

> “I like to see my husband clearly,” she replied.  
> “It helps when giving instructions.”

Milena paused.

Then nodded once.

> “Clarity is important in any campaign.”

Those nearby later described the exchange not as hostile, but as the sort of careful social maneuvering that occurs when two intelligent people quickly realize the other is not foolish.

Cornelia left the conversation satisfied that Milena posed no romantic threat to her marriage.

Milena, for her part, appeared quietly amused.

Meanwhile across the room, **[[Alistair the Younger]]** was enthusiastically explaining to **[[Beringer]]** and Bertram that Milena was “very delicate,” a statement which none of the witnesses considered especially convincing.
