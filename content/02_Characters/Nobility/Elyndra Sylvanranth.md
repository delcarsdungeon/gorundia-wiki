# Elyndra Sylvanranth

**Elyndra Sylvanranth** was the daughter of [[Theron Sylvanranth]] and **Cassara Ravenshade**, and the full sister of [[Naira Sylvanranth]].

She was widely regarded as the gentlest soul of House Sylvanranth—kind, patient, and possessed of a quiet grace that seemed almost miraculous given the ruthless world surrounding her father.

Where the politics of [[Bard's Gate]] produced ambition, intrigue, and cruelty, Elyndra was remembered for generosity, empathy, and restraint.

To many who knew the family, she was **the only light in Theron's otherwise grim life**.

---
## Appearance

Elyndra inherited much of her mother Cassara Ravenshade's beauty: soft dark hair, clear blue eyes, and delicate features that gave her an almost luminous presence.

She favored simple but elegant clothing, often in pale fabrics that reflected her quiet tastes rather than the ostentation common among the nobility of Bard's Gate.

Those who met her often remarked that she possessed an unusual warmth for someone raised within such a powerful house.

# Family

Father: [[Theron Sylvanranth]]  
Mother: [[Cassara Ravenshade]]  
Sibling: [[Naira Sylvanranth]]  

Cassara Ravenshade died in **1210**, at the age of fifty.  

Though never a figure of great political power, the Ravenshades were an old minor house with a reputation for scholarship and magical aptitude. Some accounts suggest Cassara herself possessed modest magical training, though she never pursued a formal career in the arcane arts.

Both Elyndra and Naira inherited the striking beauty often attributed to the Ravenshade line.

---

# The Better of the Two

If Naira embodied the reckless charm of their father's bloodline, Elyndra carried the steadier virtues of her mother's.

Servants often remarked that she possessed a rare gift: **the ability to calm Theron Sylvanranth's temper**.

While Naira pursued pleasure and reputation in the courts and taverns of the realm, Elyndra quietly observed the political world around her and seemed to understand the burden her father carried better than anyone.

Several of Theron's advisers later claimed that Elyndra was the **only person capable of speaking to him plainly without provoking his wrath**.

---

# A Dangerous Hope

Shortly before her death, Theron made a bold political gamble.

He brought Elyndra to **[[Bard's Gate]]** intending to present her as a possible match for the **Prince [[Porthos Ryomar]], soon to visit the city**.

The move was risky but brilliant if successful. Such a marriage would have elevated House Sylvanranth into the center of Bard's Gate's political hierarchy.

Those who knew Theron well suspected that the plan carried deeper motives.

For a man who trusted very few people, securing Elyndra's future may have been his final attempt to give his daughter **a life untouched by the darker compromises of his own power**.

---

# The Murder

Elyndra had only recently arrived at Theron's manor in Bard's Gate when tragedy struck.

Unknown to the household, several servants had already been replaced by **serpent cultists tied to a Yuan-Ti conspiracy operating within the city**.

With their assistance, the cult's champion—known only as **[[The Archon of Erythnul]]**—gained entry to the manor.

Disguised as [[Orin Kharne]], the Archon murdered Elyndra inside the house.

The killing was not merely an act of cruelty. It was intended to spark political chaos and ignite conflict among the city's factions.

The crime later became known as **[[The Bloody-Handed Deception]]**.

---

# Aftermath

Elyndra's death broke something in Theron Sylvanranth.

Though he continued to wield influence within Bard's Gate, those close to him noticed a permanent change.

He never again set foot inside the **Lord Mayor's Manor**, where the murder had taken place.

Instead, he withdrew to **the Conclave**, the former Wizard's Guild hall that served as headquarters for his private Dragonguard.

From that day forward the Conclave became both his fortress and his refuge.

Many who served him later said that whatever warmth remained in Theron Sylvanranth **died alongside his daughter**.

---

# Legacy

Though Elyndra lived only briefly within the political world of Bard's Gate, her death had lasting consequences.

- It triggered the crisis known as [[The Bloody-Handed Deception]].
- It deepened Theron Sylvanranth's ruthless reputation.
- It exposed the presence of **Yuan-Ti cult activity** within the city.

Among those who remembered her personally, Elyndra was mourned not as a political figure, but simply as **a good woman taken too soon**.
