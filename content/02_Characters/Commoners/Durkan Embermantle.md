

# Durkan Embermantle

### Master Armorer Durkan Embermantle
 
> “You’d think a dwarf that gruff would be hard to read. Not Durkan. He’s an open book — it’s just written in curses and filed under ‘Disappointment.’” - Sorek Redmarch

 **Born:** 1162 OR  
**Race:** Dwarf (Hill Dwarf)  
**Title:** Master of the King’s Steel, Royal Armorer of Gorundia  
**Affiliation:** Crown of Gorundia, High Forge of Karethlun  
**Residence:** The Royal Forges, Lower Citadel of Karethlun  
**Notable For:** Outfitting generations of royal knights; refusing to flatter anyone taller than him (which is everyone) 
#### Overview

 Durkan Embermantle is the irritable, immovable backbone of the Royal Forges. For over three decades he has crafted, repaired, and cursed at the armor of Gorundia’s finest. The king trusts him, nobles fear him, and squires scatter when they hear his boots in the hall. Appointed Master of the King’s Steel after the Battle of Southwatch, Durkan’s creations are recognizable by their flawless balance, subtle geometric scrollwork, and quiet defiance of fashion trends. If a knight wants unnecessary ornamentation, he tells them to “go buy it from a silversmith who hates their parents.” Durkan’s grumpiness is legendary, but beneath the beard-bark and profanity lies a craftsman of staggering precision and unspoken loyalty to the Crown. 

#### Personality

 Durkan operates under three principles: Steel doesn’t care about your feelings. Neither does Durkan. If he’s shouting, it means he likes you enough to care whether you die. His bedside manner is comparable to a wagon hitting a pothole full of bees, but every knight who’s worn his harnesses survives longer because of him. He hates compliments, despises bowing, and has never once said “good job” without immediately following it with “don’t get cocky.” 

#### Appearance

 Durkan is squat even by dwarven standards, built like a keg of black powder. His beard is braided into geometric plates bound with iron rings. His right eye is clouded from a forge blast decades ago; the left is sharp enough to see a loose rivet from across the courtyard. He wears a leather apron that has outlasted two kings and a reputation that has outlasted three. 

#### Skills & Craft

 Durkan is responsible for: • Commissioning and fitting ceremonial and battle armor for royal knights  
• Ensuring heraldic accuracy on shields and tabards  
• Overseeing the tempering of weapons for the king’s household guard  
• Training the next generation of royal smiths (whom he calls “my disappointments”)  
• Approving gifts of arms and armor for newly knighted heroes Each piece that leaves his forge bears the discreet Embermantle rune—a stylized mountain flanked by twin hammers. 

#### History

 Durkan was born to the Embermantle Clan of the Stonevault Mountains, a family famous for crafting practical, no-nonsense armaments. He apprenticed under his aunt, the volcanic-tempered Borga Embermantle, who once broke a sword over his head because he “quenched it like an elf learning to swim.” He came to Karethlun as a journeyman but became indispensable during a siege when he devised a way to rapidly re-plate damaged armor for the king’s cavalry. After Southwatch, the king personally elevated him to royal service. He accepted by grumbling that it would “ruin him for good honest work,” then secretly framed the appointment notice. 

#### Politics

 Durkan Embermantle has the political engagement of a brick. He doesn’t vote, he doesn’t care, and if asked his opinion of the Bannerhall, he grunts something that may be a curse or a digestive noise. His only “policies” are: • Goblins: “Shoot first.” • Full-blooded orcs: “Shoot harder.” • Half-bloods: He treats them exactly as he treats everyone else—only slightly more gruff, mostly because he thinks they “need thicker hides if they’re going to survive this kingdom anyway.” He will work for anyone the king approves of, and if nobles try to sway him, he pretends he can’t hear them over the forge. 

#### Quotes

**Durkan Embermantle**: “Hold still. I’m trying to make sure your ribs stay inside you this season.” **Durkan Embermantle**: “If you want pretty armor, marry rich. If you want to stay alive, stand still and shut up.” **Durkan Embermantle**: “Stop thanking me. The king pays me for this. Badly.” 

#### Companions & Assistants

 • Talla Quickstripe – a halfling apprentice who Durkan claims is “the only one with fingers nimble enough to not ruin everything.” • Morgran Ironvault – a retired tunnel-fighter who serves as Durkan’s runner, shield-bearer, and interruption-preventer. • The Ember Forges – Durkan’s personal workshop, a triple-wide stone chamber blessed fifty years ago by a cleric of Moradin who was too tired to explain the details. 

#### Role in the Campaign

 Durkan is responsible for outfitting: • Newly knighted PCs (Rykeena, Guy, Thalrik, etc.) • Anyone the king wants to publicly endorse • Visitors of rank who require ceremonial arms • Knights of the Bannerhall receiving battlefield promotions He provides gear equal parts impressive and functional, often muttering that if the king wants to hand out shiny toys, he should also hand out “a pamphlet on how not to get stabbed.” Durkan may also become a source of rumors—he hears gossip from every knight, squire, guard, and cook who walks through his forge.
