# The Kingdom of Celene

Celene is the ancient high-elven kingdom of the Lortmil Mountains, ruled by **Queen Yolande**, one of the oldest living monarchs in the Flanaess.

The realm is famed for its beauty and feared for its patience.

To mortal kingdoms, Celene often appears aloof or indifferent to the wars of the world. In truth, the elves of Celene simply measure events across **centuries rather than years**.

The kingdom’s capital, **Enstad**, is considered one of the most magnificent cities ever built by elven hands.

White towers rise like living trees above terraces of silver gardens, and bridges of crystal span courtyards that bloom in every season. The city appears less constructed than grown, every stone shaped to endure millennia.

# The Lost Twin Kingdom

Two hundred years ago, one of the **High-Elven Twin Kingdoms of the West** collapsed during a cascade of wars and invasions.

The final queen of that kingdom was a **blood relative of Queen Yolande of Celene**.

Her death marked the end of the western elven crown.

The territory was consumed by humanoid invasions and fractured human states. For centuries the land remained a lawless frontier where the remnants of elven civilization slowly faded.

Celene did not intervene.

The Queen maintained the doctrine that **the survival of Celene must come before the salvation of the world**.

Yet the loss of the twin kingdom was never forgotten.

---

# The Three-Century Strategy

Long before the final collapse, Celene began quietly preparing the frontier for eventual restoration.

Instead of sending armies, the court placed **long-term agents** among the rising powers of the west.

Two of the most important were:

### Theron Sylvanranth

A young noble of Celene, brilliant but notoriously unruly.

His assignment was to shape the emerging **human kingdoms** into a force capable of reclaiming the lost elven lands.

In essence:

Humans would perform the war of reconquest.

The elves would restore the ancient crown afterward.

Theron accepted Celene’s coin and instructions for many decades.

But over time he severed those ties.

His final message to Celene reportedly read:

> “I am Gorundian now. Your coin is no longer required.”

He continued influencing the region, but no longer as Celene’s servant.

---

### Faelar Stormarrow

At the same time, Celene dispatched an emissary to the **Grugach**, the wild elves of the deep forests.

This elf was **Faelar Stormarrow**, father of the future war-chief Thranduil.

His task mirrored Theron’s:

• unite the grugach tribes  
• prepare them as a military arm of the eventual reclamation  
• maintain Celene’s influence over the forest peoples

Instead, Faelar did what Celene’s court least expected.

He **went native**.

He married a grugach woman and abandoned the politics of the court entirely.

He died many years ago among the forest clans he had come to call home.

# The Advance Company

Before the Celene embassy formally arrived in Gorundia, a small **advance force** crossed the frontier.

This force was led by **Thranduil’s uncle**, accompanied by:

• one hundred heavily armored elven spearmen  
• a powerful mage known only as **Lómelindë**

Their mission was reconnaissance.

Lómelindë traveled disguised among human soldiers, concealing her identity while observing the frontier conflict firsthand.

The reports she delivered to Celene were stark.

The war was escalating.

The frontier kingdoms were stronger than expected.

And the Stormarrow war-chief had become a central figure in the conflict.

These reports are widely believed to be one of the factors that finally persuaded Celene’s court to end its centuries of silence.

---

# The Recognition of Thranduil

During the recent embassy, Queen Yolande formally acknowledged Thranduil Stormarrow as a captain of the elven host.

Her words to him were both praise and warning:

> “The line of Stormarrow has never been tame, yet it has ever been true.”
> 
> queen of celene

She granted him several honors:

• the restored armor of his father  
• the war-mage **Maerithil Embercrown**  
• a company of elite rangers known as the **Veiled Bough**

This gesture represented Celene’s first overt military commitment to the reclamation effort in centuries.

---

# The Political Reality

Celene now faces an uncomfortable truth.

The agents it once planted across the frontier have grown into powers of their own.

Theron Sylvanranth commands the loyalty of human nobles.

Thranduil Stormarrow commands the loyalty of the grugach.

Neither answers to Celene.

Yet both are essential to the restoration of the lost elven kingdom.

The court of Celene must now decide whether to treat them as servants…

or allies.

# Dana Sylvanranth in Enstad

Dana Sylvanranth accompanied the diplomatic mission to Celene in place of her father.

For the first time she saw **Enstad**, the ancient capital.

The experience left a deeper impression than she admitted.

The city seemed impossibly old, every tower and garden shaped with a patience mortals rarely comprehend.

Dana maintained her composure.

But the elves were watching closely.

---

# The Prince’s Private Conversation

On the final evening of the embassy, **Prince Vorondil Calandor** requested a private audience with Dana.

They met in a quiet gallery overlooking the silver terraces of Enstad.

The prince studied her for a long moment before speaking.

“Your father was sent west as a gardener,” he said calmly.

“To cultivate the strength that would one day restore our fallen crown.”

Dana did not respond.

The prince continued.

“He stopped writing to us a century ago.”

His eyes softened slightly.

“But we never stopped watching.”

He stepped closer to the balcony.

“The forest has many storms, Lady Sylvanranth. Your father is one of them.”

Dana’s voice was quiet.

“Do you consider him a traitor?”

The prince considered the question carefully.

Then he shook his head.

“No.”

A pause.

“Merely… wild.”

He turned back toward her.

“And sometimes it is the wild things that survive when the garden fails.”

For a moment the prince seemed almost amused.

“Celene is patient, Lady Sylvanranth.”

He gestured toward the distant mountains.

“We planted seeds three hundred years ago.”

Another pause.

“It seems they have grown.”
