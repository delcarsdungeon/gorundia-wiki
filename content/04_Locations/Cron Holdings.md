# Additional Holdings of Baron Lynwenden Cron

*Compiled from ducal charters, revenue rolls, and land grants recorded under the authority of House Vogel.*

While **Baron [[Lynwenden Cron]]** derives his primary income from the **Barony of [[Thunderhold]]**, several additional properties and charters appear under his name through direct ducal writ. These holdings lie outside the traditional Thunderhold charter and depend entirely on ducal favor.

All revenue estimates use the kingdom’s copper standard.

*1 gold = 400 copper*  
*1 silver = 20 copper*

---

## I. Eastbank Quarry Lease

**Type:** Ducal Extraction Charter  
**Term:** Renewable (3-year intervals)  
**Status:** Active

Estimated gross revenue: **5,000–6,000 copper / year**  
Estimated net to Cron: **~2,000 copper / year (≈5 gold)**

**Notes**

- Quarry sits on **ducal land**, not Thunderhold territory.
- Lease was countersigned directly at **Direhold**.
- Stone shipments frequently move toward **Wyrmlea markets**.

Revocation of ducal favor would immediately terminate this charter.

---

## II. Redwater Crossing Toll

**Type:** Minor River Toll Charter  
**Status:** Granted for “maintenance and security”

Toll rate: **1 copper per axle, ½ copper per mounted rider**

Estimated annual intake: **1,200–1,800 copper**  
Estimated net to Cron: **~1,000 copper / year (≈2½ gold)**

**Notes**

- Crossing lies outside Thunderhold’s original baronial boundary.
- Often used by grain and charcoal wagons avoiding larger trade routes.
- Charter authority derives directly from ducal writ.

---

## III. Black Hollow Farmsteads

**Type:** Forfeited Rebel Lands  
**Status:** Held “in stewardship”

Number of properties: **Three former farmsteads**

Combined agricultural yield: **~1,400 copper / year**  
Estimated net to Cron: **~1,000 copper (≈2½ gold)**

**Notes**

- Lands seized after border unrest.
- Never formally integrated into the Thunderhold charter.
- Revenue flows directly to Cron’s household accounts.

---

## IV. North Greymark Forest Charter

**Type:** Ducal Hunting and Timber License  
**Status:** Exclusive license

Rights granted:

- Timber harvest  
- Charcoal production  
- Noble hunting privileges

Estimated revenue: **3,000–4,000 copper / year**  
Estimated net to Cron: **~1,500 copper (≈3¾ gold)**

**Strategic Value**

- Independent lumber supply
- Legal pretext for retainers operating within the forest
- Private hunting grounds for noble guests

---

## V. Custodianship of Ashmere Manor

**Type:** Temporary Noble Custody  
**Status:** “Pending succession clarification”

Estimated estate yield: **~3,500 copper / year**  
Estimated net to Cron: **~2,000 copper (≈5 gold)**

**Notes**

- Estate previously held by a disgraced knight.
- Title never formally transferred.
- Managed by Cron’s reeves under temporary ducal authority.

## Context

For comparison:

A typical **Status-2 noble household in Bard’s Gate** grosses roughly **25,000 copper per year**, with approximately **5,000–6,000 copper taxable** after expenses.

Cron’s **Thunderhold revenues already sustain his barony**. These additional charters therefore represent the equivalent income of **another small barony**, dependent entirely on the favor of **[[Wilhelm Vogel]]**.

Should Cron be stripped of rank or attainted, **all such ducal charters revert immediately to the ducal ledger**.

---

## Observational Note

Nearly all of these holdings:

- originate from **direct ducal authority**,  
- lie **outside Thunderhold’s historic charter**, and  
- depend on **renewable or revocable grants**.

This pattern suggests Baron Cron was not merely compensated for service, but **financially reinforced through discretionary ducal patronage**.
