The **Duchy of Gravendar** dominates the central and western heart of the kingdom. Ancient, wealthy, and militarily powerful, it is one of the crown’s most strategically vital domains.

Its strength comes not from a single resource but from the diversity of its lands:

- the grain wealth of **Blackvale** 
- the quarries and dwarven hills of **Wyrmlea**
- the industrial forges of **Sunhollow**
- the halfling breadlands of **Southmere**
- the disciplined infantry traditions of **Ironmere**
- the storm valleys and militant monasteries of **Thunderhold**

At the center of this network stands **[[Castle Direhold]]**, the ancient fortress of House Vogel, rising from Lake Vaelrath and long believed impossible to take by conventional siege.

Unlike many noble domains, the **Vogel ducal demesne is austere**. The lands are organized into tightly controlled estates administered by military governors, and serfdom is rigidly enforced. Wealth from these lands flows directly into Vogel’s military power rather than aristocratic luxury.

# Counties of Gravendar

## [[County Blackvale]]

**Ruling House:** [[House Bordella]]

Blackvale is the **grain belt of Gravendar** and among the richest agricultural regions in the kingdom.

Deep soils, broad plains, and extensive irrigation systems allow enormous harvests. The wealth generated by these lands has made **House Bordella famously opulent**, and their estates are among the most extravagant in the duchy.

Blackvale’s output feeds large portions of the kingdom and provides the economic backbone of Gravendar’s power.

### Vassal Baronies

#### [[Barony Ironmere]]

**Ruling House:** [[House Moffet]]

Ironmere is a broad, relatively flat region that is **dead-average economically**. Its land is good horse country and productive but not exceptionally rich.

What Ironmere lacks in wealth it makes up for in military tradition.

The barony is famous for its **tenacious heavy infantry**, whose reputation was cemented during the **Battle of Red Gorge**, where Moffet troops held the line at tremendous cost.

Their sacrifice earned House Moffet the rare honor of **heraldic supporters** on their coat of arms.

---

#### [[Barony Thunderhold]]

**Ruling House:** [[House Cron]]

Thunderhold stretches across a series of **long, storm-swept valleys**, whose violent seasonal weather gives the region its name.

The terrain favors cavalry and produces excellent horse stock.

The barony is also home to several powerful **temple-monasteries of Heironeous**, militant religious institutions that maintain significant independence from secular authority.

While economically modest, Thunderhold contributes heavily to Gravendar’s **cavalry forces and militant clergy** in times of war.

---

## [[County Wyrmlea]]

**Ruling House:** [[House Vallytrex]]

Wyrmlea is a rugged and hilly county famous for the **finest quarries in the kingdom**.

Stone from Wyrmlea is used in fortifications, castles, roads, and temples throughout the realm.

Large populations of **hill dwarves** live and work in the region, many belonging to long-established quarry clans or subterranean holds in the hills.

Agriculture is limited due to the terrain, but the county’s mineral and stone wealth make it one of Gravendar’s most strategically valuable territories.

### Vassal Baronies

#### [[Barony Sunhollow]]

**Ruling House:** [[House Terevon]]

Sunhollow lies in a long river valley crowded with **mines, mills, and forge settlements**.

Iron drawn from the surrounding hills feeds a network of smithies and workshops that produce arms, armor, and tools in enormous quantity. The valley is rarely quiet, and the glow of furnaces can be seen along the river even at night.

Large numbers of **hill dwarves** live and work here alongside human smiths and craftsmen.

A great share of the kingdom’s **weapons and armor originate in Sunhollow**, making it one of Gravendar’s most strategically important industrial regions.

---

#### [[Barony Southmere]]

**Ruling House:** [[House Kendal]]

Southmere consists of **low rolling hills and farmland** that lack the rich soil and irrigation systems of Blackvale.

Despite this, the region is highly productive due to its large **halfling agricultural communities**.

Halfling farmers in Southmere are famous for their meticulous cultivation techniques, often **outproducing neighboring human farms**.

Southmere is **mineral poor and industrially weak**, but it ranks **second only to Blackvale in agricultural output**.

# Demographics

Gravendar’s population is predominantly **human**, though several regions contain notable demi-human communities.

### Hill Dwarves

Major populations reside in:

- [[County Wyrmlea]]
- [[Barony Sunhollow]]
   
Dwarves dominate quarry labor, mining, and metalworking industries.

### Halflings

Concentrated in:

- [[Barony Southmere]]

Halflings form dense rural farming communities that significantly increase agricultural efficiency.

### Ducal Demesne

The lands directly controlled by **House Vogel** contain comparatively few non-human residents due to strict feudal control and military administration.

---

# Economic Overview

Gravendar’s economy rests on three primary pillars:

### Agriculture

Dominated by:

- [[County Blackvale]]
- [[Barony Southmere]]

Together these lands produce enormous quantities of grain and food exports.

---

### Industry and Mining

Centered in:

- [[County Wyrmlea]] — quarries and stone    
- [[Barony Sunhollow]] — iron mines and forge towns

These regions provide the materials and arms that equip much of Gravendar’s military forces.

---

### Military Manpower

Several regions contribute disproportionately to Gravendar’s wartime forces:

- [[Barony Ironmere]] — disciplined heavy infantry
- [[Barony Thunderhold]] — cavalry and militant clergy
   
Combined with Sunhollow’s arms production, Gravendar fields one of the most formidable military forces in the kingdom.
