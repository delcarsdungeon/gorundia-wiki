# Sunseeker Shrine

A roadside shrine to [[Pelor]] established in the **Dregs Quarter of [[Bard's Gate]]** under the guidance of [[Druuna Sunseeker]].

Though modest in scale, the shrine was built properly using **good stone, fresh timber, and honest labor**, funded largely by a **10-gold donation from [[Thalrik]]** and constructed entirely by volunteers of the Dregs.

The shrine serves as a place for:

- prayer
- food distribution
- quiet refuge
- Druuna’s sermons

Within weeks of its construction it became **a protected corner of the Dregs**, watched over by locals who have little patience for anyone foolish enough to vandalize it.

---

## Construction

Unlike many charitable works in the district, the shrine’s construction involved **no skimming or corruption**.

Funds were used for:

- **cut marble facing for the alcove**
- **fresh structural beams**
- **stone foundation blocks**
- **a carved sunburst lintel**
- **a brass Pelor emblem set into the shrine wall**

Volunteer labor from dockworkers, laborers, and residents of the Dregs provided the rest.

The work was done carefully and openly, creating a structure that appears **simple but unmistakably solid**.

---

## Location

The shrine sits at the junction of two narrow alleys in the Dregs, creating a natural corner space where small gatherings can form without blocking traffic.

The placement was chosen deliberately.

Several nearby residents — including some of the harder half-blood dockworkers — have made it quietly clear that **anyone damaging the shrine will regret it immediately**.

---

## Description

The shrine consists of:

- a **marble-lined alcove**
- a **carved wooden beam bearing Pelor’s sunburst**
- a **small shelf for candles and offerings**
- a **copper basin**
- a **brass sun emblem set into the stone**

Though not ornate, the materials and workmanship make it stand out against the surrounding slum architecture.

Candles are almost always burning.

---

## Cultural Role

In the Dregs the shrine functions less as a formal temple and more as a **community anchor**.

People come to:

- leave food for others
- pray briefly
- rest
- listen to Druuna preach
- seek small blessings

Even residents who have no interest in religion tend to treat the place with respect.

---
## Founding Moment

The shrine’s early construction drew quiet attention from some of the regulars of the [[Blood and Sand]].

Late one afternoon, as volunteers worked the stone and set the beam over the alcove, **[[Thalrik]]** stood watching the progress with a deep scowl carved into his beard.

Across the street, **[[Sorek Redmarch]]** leaned against a post with a mug in hand.

Thalrik jerked his chin toward the shrine.

“Thirty gold.”

Sorek grunted.

Thalrik kept staring at the work.

“Thirty.”

A laborer hauled another block into place while [[Druuna Sunseeker]] carefully set a small brass Pelor sun into the stone.

She thanked him with a tired smile.

Sorek took a slow drink.

“Looks like ten of it’s right there,” he said.

Thalrik scowled.

“And the rest?”

Sorek nodded down the street.

A thin woman walked past carrying a loaf wrapped in cloth.

A boy ran by chewing something that had definitely not been in his hands yesterday.

Across the alley, two half-orc dockworkers leaned against the wall watching the shrine with the quiet attention of men who would very much enjoy breaking the bones of anyone foolish enough to deface it.

Sorek tipped his mug toward them.

“That corner,” he said. “Anyone dumb enough to mess with that shrine is going to wake up with fewer teeth.”

Thalrik’s beard twitched.

Sorek finished his drink.

“Relax, Thalrik.”

He nodded toward Druuna lighting the first candle in the alcove.

“If the girl ever learns how to keep money…”

Sorek shrugged.

“…we’ll know [[Pelor]] has abandoned her.”
