From the Elvish Codex Nael'thyr e'Lirael (“The Twilight Mirror”) 
Translated from Silvaeren by Lorekeeper Ilion Myreth, Fourth Age 

“To match a fae in wrath is to dance barefoot on a frozen blade— sharp, cold, and always beneath you. 

But those who bow not in fear, but in riddles— those who bleed wit before blood— may find the storm parts for them, and silver fruit hangs lower on thorned boughs.* 
For the Courts do not love fairness. They adore spectacle, surprise, and story. 

Be clever, and be remembered. Be bold, and be offered wine.* 

*The Unseelie do not play fair... but they do play favorites.”
