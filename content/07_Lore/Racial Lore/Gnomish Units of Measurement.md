Gnomish Units of Measurement

Standardized by the Guild of Precision Chaos, Teenkdiwar Chapter

---

Length,
Snicket – The basic gnomish unit of length= exactly 3¼ inches (because “that’s how long my uncle’s thumb was,” according to Founding Engineer Glimrick the Slightly Toasted)10 snickets = 1 twizzle,

Twizzle = 32½ inches(1 twizzle = 10 snickets = “about nose to tail on a well-fed ferret”),

Cogsnap = exactly 1 gnomish pace, roughly 2 feet(used when installing trapdoors or determining "fall-safe" distances),

Kablunk = 100 twizzles("The exact length at which you start forgetting what you were measuring"),

---

Weight,
Tinker = about 1 pound, or "the weight of a solid bronze teacup"8 tinkers = 1 bramble,

Bramble = 8 pounds("Named after the inventor who famously misjudged the weight of his prototype flying kettle"),

Whump = variable—defined as “enough weight to make the floorboards go whump”(Roughly 40–60 pounds, depending on wood quality and whether the gnome is sneaking),

---

Volume,
Gurgle = 1 cup("The amount of tea before someone starts complaining it’s not enough"),

Sprocket = 1 pint, or “half a gurgle more than you thought”,

Boom = 1 gallon, usually referring to how much alchemical fluid should not be in one container,

---

Time,
Zing = approximately 7 seconds("The time it takes a gnome to regret pressing the button"),

Whizzle = a quarter-hour("How long Glimrick claimed he’d been gone before the fire started"),

Tocksproing = 3 hours, or one full alchemy cycle,

---

 Fun Canon Note,
The gnomes of Teenkdiwar still use this system even when negotiating with elves and dwarves. There are documented treaties that include phrases like:

"...no fewer than four and twenty kablunks of reinforced trenchwork, each no shallower than two and a half cogsnaps, plus or minus a wiggle."
