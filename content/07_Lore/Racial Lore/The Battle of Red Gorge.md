# The Battle of Red Gorge

The **Battle of Red Gorge** in **1190 OR** marked the decisive engagement of the long war between the [[Dominion]] and the Kingdom of **Gorundia**.

For decades the Dominion had pressed westward, its armies driven by the iron doctrine of [[Hextor]] and commanded by a rigid hierarchy of generals, priest-officers, and political commissars. Gorundia, newly unified and desperate to survive, answered with a coalition of noble houses, knightly orders, and allied elven forces.

At Red Gorge, strategy and sacrifice finally broke the Dominion’s advance.

---

# The Armies

## The Dominion Host

The Dominion army embodied the harsh military doctrine of the eastern empire.

Its formations were composed of:

- disciplined heavy infantry fighting in shielded ranks  
- cavalry armed with curved sabers and lamellar armor  
- elite Tyrant Knights devoted to Hextor  
- large numbers of coerced soldiers and slave levies

Among the most feared figures on the battlefield were the **Scourge Commissars**, priest-officers of the [[Iron Hierarchy of Hextor]] who marched with Dominion banners.

Their presence ensured discipline through terror.  
Deserters were executed immediately, and officers who faltered could expect the same fate.

Dominion banners bearing the **red gauntlet of Hextor** advanced with grim determination through the gorge.

---

## The Gorundian Host

Opposing them was the army of Gorundia, a coalition of noble houses united under the cause of independence.

Its strength lay in:

- heavily armored knights  
- disciplined men-at-arms  
- elven longbowmen and skirmishers  
- battlefield priests of **Heironeous**

Where the Dominion fought through fear and domination, Gorundia relied on **coordination, terrain, and courage**.

---

# The Architect of Victory

## Count Amadis

At the center of Gorundia’s strategy stood **[[Alastair Amadis]]**, Count of Blackbriar and one of the kingdom’s most formidable tacticians.

Amadis rejected the romantic notion of heroic charges deciding the battle. Instead he treated Red Gorge as a **weapon**.

The gorge itself was narrow, wooded, and broken by steep cliffs—terrain poorly suited for the Dominion’s heavy formations.

Amadis used this to his advantage.

---

### The Plan

His strategy unfolded in three coordinated phases:

**Terrain Control**

Elven skirmishers were positioned along the cliffs and wooded ridges overlooking the gorge. From these heights they harassed Dominion formations and disrupted their advance.

**The Feigned Retreat**

House Moffet was ordered to deliberately withdraw on the Gorundian right flank.  They took terrible losses.

Dominion commanders, believing the enemy was collapsing, pushed their heavy infantry and cavalry forward into a narrow ravine.

**The Hammer Blow**

When the Dominion forces entered the trap, hidden knights struck from both sides while reserves slammed into the exposed center.

What had appeared to be a retreat became a devastating encirclement.

---

# The Vogels: Blood in the Center

At the heart of the battle rode the banner of **[[House Vogel]]**.

Duke **Maletryx Vogel**, despite being nearly eighty years old, rode into battle beside his son **Maxen Vogel** and his grandson **Galmyr Vogel**, then only eighteen.

Their manticore banner became a rallying point for Gorundian troops as they charged directly into the Dominion center.

The assault shattered the enemy’s heavy infantry and scattered formations of slave soldiers forced into the front lines.

But the price was terrible.

A towering half-orc warlord leading Dominion shock troops counterattacked with brutal ferocity.

Both **Maletryx** and **Maxen** were slain in the fighting.

Young **Galmyr Vogel**, wounded and surrounded, seized his father’s sword and rallied the remaining knights.

Witnesses later claimed that lightning split the sky as Galmyr invoked **Heironeous**, and the Dominion lines faltered.

Whether divine or coincidence, the moment broke the enemy center.

---

# Lady Allarson's Stand

On the Gorundian left flank, **[[Nienor Allarson]]** commanded the elven forces and her household knights.

Her troops held dense woodland overlooking the battlefield, where archers poured arrows into advancing Dominion units.

When Dominion cavalry finally turned toward her position, the fighting became desperate.

Allarson and her guard held the line long enough for reinforcements to arrive.

When the battle ended, she was found wounded but standing among the fallen—her sword still drawn.

---

# The Moffet Gambit

On the right flank, **[[House Moffet]]** executed Amadis’s most dangerous maneuver.

Feigning panic, they retreated through a narrow ravine.

Dominion infantry pursued aggressively, urged forward by their commissars.

Once the trap was sprung, Gorundian knights struck from concealed positions.

The Dominion right flank collapsed under the sudden assault.

---

# Rumors of the Gods

Both sides later claimed divine intervention at Red Gorge.

Gorundian soldiers swore they saw **Heironeous** riding with the knights, lightning flashing from his blade.

Dominion survivors told darker stories—of a six-armed avatar of **Hextor** stalking the battlefield and slaughtering men without mercy.

Whether truth or battlefield myth, these tales cemented Red Gorge as a **holy battle** in the memory of both nations. 

---

# Aftermath

By nightfall the Dominion army had broken.

Without their center and right flank, surviving commanders ordered a retreat eastward.

The battle ended the Dominion’s westward expansion and secured **Gorundian independence**.

The cost, however, was immense.

- Duke **[[Maletryx Vogel]]** fell in battle.  
- **[[Maxen Vogel]]** died beside him.  
- **[[Galmyr Vogel]]** emerged from the carnage as the surviving heir and a newly knighted war hero.

The battle elevated **Amadis Blackbriar** to legendary status and cemented the reputations of Houses Vogel, Allarson, and Moffet.

Even decades later, the fields of Red Gorge are remembered as the place where **Gorundia was truly forged in blood**.
