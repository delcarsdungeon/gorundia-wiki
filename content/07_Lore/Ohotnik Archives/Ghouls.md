# From the Ohotnik Archives: Ghouls

> *Ohotnik Codex — Field Observations on the Ghoul Folk*

---

## Overview

Since the dawn of recorded history, **Ghouls** have been hunted relentlessly by humans and other civilized races. Many times humanity has believed the species completely exterminated. Yet the Ghouls have always endured.

They live quietly beneath the feet of the living, often inhabiting forgotten tunnels, crypts, and the shadowed earth beneath graveyards — using mankind’s burial grounds as their larder.

Ghouls are masters of **stealth, camouflage, and disguise**, both magical and mundane.  
A human — or even an elf — might pass within inches of an entire ghoul family and never suspect their presence.

---

## Nature of the Ghoul Folk

Despite their fearsome reputation, Ghouls are **not inherently evil**.

They are:

- Suspicious of outsiders  
- Protective of their communities  
- Occasionally ruthless when threatened  

Centuries of persecution have sometimes twisted individual Ghouls or even entire communities toward cruelty or vengeance. However, most ghoul societies seek only **to survive without conflict with the living**.

---

## Ghoul Society

One trait consistently noted by Ohotnik observers is the **remarkable loyalty Ghouls show to their own kind**.

Within ghoul society:

- No Ghoul will knowingly harm another
- Theft between Ghouls is almost unheard of
- Betrayal of one’s own kind is considered unthinkable

In this regard, their internal code of conduct is, ironically, **often stricter than that of humanity**.

---

## Habits and Living Conditions

Ghouls are not a pleasant species by human standards.

Their appearance alone is unsettling:

- Pale flesh
- Long claws
- A disturbingly wide, tooth-filled grin

Their customs are likewise alien.

Ghouls are completely **immune to disease**, and as a result their living environments tend to be extremely unsanitary by human standards. Rotting flesh, grave soil, and damp tunnels are common features of their settlements.

Many practices they consider mundane are viewed by humans as **deeply taboo**.
