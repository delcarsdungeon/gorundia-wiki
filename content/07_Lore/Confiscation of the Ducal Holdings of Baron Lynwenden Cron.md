# Confiscation of the Ducal Holdings of Baron Lynwenden Cron

Following the formal judgment of the Crown against **[[Lynwenden Cron]]**, Baron of [[Thunderhold]], the court issued an immediate review of all properties and charters held by him **outside the hereditary bounds of his barony**.

These lands were determined to be **ducal concessions granted at the pleasure of House Vogel**, rather than hereditary rights.

Under royal authority, and at the recommendation of **[[Beringer]], Lord Marshal of Gorundia**, the Crown has ordered that these holdings **revert to the ducal ledger pending reassignment or royal disposition**.

---

# Holdings Seized by Writ of the Crown

## Eastbank Quarry Lease

**Type:** Ducal Extraction Charter  
**Status:** Terminated by writ

The quarry lease was granted directly through **ducal authority rather than baronial inheritance**.

The Crown therefore ruled the charter **void upon Cron’s conviction**, returning operational control to the ducal steward.

Current status:

• Quarry workers remain employed under temporary crown supervision.  
• Stone shipments continue uninterrupted.  
• Revenue now flows to the **Grevendar ducal treasury**.

Estimated annual value:  
**≈2,000 copper net**

---

## Redwater Crossing Toll

**Type:** Minor toll charter

The toll charter existed solely through **renewable ducal permission**.

The Crown ruled the crossing **public infrastructure rather than noble property**, and thus the charter was dissolved.

New disposition:

• Toll rights transferred to the **Royal Road Wardens**.  
• Collection administered through crown reeves.

Estimated annual value:  
**≈1,000 copper**

---

## Black Hollow Farmsteads

**Type:** Confiscated rebel lands held in stewardship

The court determined these lands had **never been legally incorporated into the Thunderhold charter**.

Disposition:

• Farms placed under **direct crown administration**.  
• Tenants retain their holdings to prevent disruption of food supply.

Estimated annual value:  
**≈1,000 copper**

---

## North Greymark Forest Charter

**Type:** Ducal hunting and timber license

This charter allowed **exclusive timber rights and hunting privileges**.

The Crown has suspended the license pending ducal reassignment.

Disposition:

• Timber rights returned to the **Grevendar forestry office**.  
• Noble hunting privileges revoked.

Estimated annual value:  
**≈1,500 copper**

---

## Custodianship of Ashmere Manor

**Type:** Noble estate held in temporary custody

The estate was held by Cron **without full transfer of title**, awaiting succession determination.

Disposition:

• Manor placed under **royal guardianship** until lawful inheritance is determined.  
• Household staff retained.

Estimated annual value:  
**≈2,000 copper**

# Political Context

The Crown’s ruling carefully distinguished between:

• **Hereditary baronial lands of [[Thunderhold]]**, which remain attached to the title  
• **Ducal concessions granted by House Vogel**, which were revocable privileges

By reclaiming these properties, the Crown ensured that **Cron’s punishment did not destabilize the barony itself**, while still stripping him of the additional wealth that had elevated his influence among the nobility.

The judgment was widely interpreted by observers as a **direct repudiation of Vogel’s patronage network**.

---

# Court Commentary

Several nobles present at the hearing remarked that the decision was **as political as it was legal**.

> “You do not destroy a barony for one fool’s vanity.  
> But you take away the toys that let him pretend to be a duke.”  
> — [[Sorek Redmarch]]
