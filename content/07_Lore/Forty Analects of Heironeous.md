# The Forty Analects of Heironeous

*Tags: #religion #heironeous #chivalry #doctrine #gorundia*

> “Scripture’s a funny thing. Knights make it sound like war-cries, priests chant it like sermons, and the smallfolk twist it into proverbs. But the bones of it are the same: do not shame yourself before the Archpaladin.”
> — [[Sorek Redmarch]] 

---

## Overview

The **Forty Analects of Heironeous** form the core ethical doctrine of the faith of [[Heironeous]], patron of valor, justice, and righteous war within [[Gorundia]]. 

They are not merely scripture, but a living code—interpreted differently by clergy, knightly orders, and common folk alike.

---

## The Forty Analects

1. Shield the helpless, for in their survival lies your honor.

2. Do not barter with evil, for every coin paid feeds its hunger.

3. Stand firm when others falter; courage is most needed in the hour of fear.

4. Strike swiftly, but only in justice; wrath without cause is the work of Hextor.

5. Suffer no evil, no matter its guise or its whisper.

6. Remember the weak—your strength was given to bear their burden.

7. Honor your oaths, even when they bind like iron.

8. Betrayal stains deeper than blood.

9. Let mercy temper your hand; a tyrant knows only cruelty.

10. Speak truth, even when silence would profit you.

11. The sword is sharpened by the whetstone; the soul is sharpened by trial.

12. Fear no wound that is taken for the sake of the innocent.

13. Justice delayed is justice denied.

14. Glory is nothing if bought with dishonor.

15. Do not envy the wicked their victories—they have already lost.

16. Let your life be a lantern to guide those behind you.

17. Walk the path of light, that others may follow; oppose darkness wherever it breeds.

18. A knight’s word is his fortress; never let yours crumble.

19. Trust is a blade that cuts both ways—wield it carefully, yet offer it freely to the worthy.

20. Remember always: cowardice kills more surely than any spear.

21. To protect your people is a greater prize than crowns or gold.

22. Hold your tongue in anger, but let your sword speak plainly in justice.

23. Beware pride: it whispers with Hextor’s tongue.

24. Do not waste your strength in boasting—let your deeds speak.

25. A true victory is one that lifts others with you.

26. Aid the faithful, for even the smallest prayer strengthens the host of heaven.

27. Embolden the way for others, that no man march alone.

28. Resist corruption in all its forms; it begins as a drop and becomes a flood.

29. Remember that justice is not vengeance, though both burn hot.

30. To show kindness to the defeated is to deny evil its last triumph.

31. Do not let despair take root—hope is the enemy of shadows.

32. To kneel only in prayer is to bow to none but heaven.

33. Purity of heart is stronger than purity of blood.

34. Never die easy; strive always for a valorous end.

35. He who stands alone may yet hold the line.

36. A shield raised for another is the truest sign of faith.

37. Give no quarter to cruelty; it is the seed of tyranny.

38. Be vigilant in peace, for that is when the enemy plots.

39. Let each dawn find you ready to defend what is just.

40. The worthy will know you by your deeds, not your words.

## Interpretations by Tradition

### The Church of Heironeous

*See also: [[Triad of Law and Light]], [[Inquisition of the Light]]*

* **Analects 1 & 6** — Strength exists to carry the weak; charity is sacred duty.
* **Analects 2 & 5** — Absolute prohibition on dealing with devils, cults, or Hextorites.
* **Analect 23** — Pride is the quiet corruption of clergy.
* **Analect 34** — Funeral rite for fallen knights: *“He died valorous, not easy.”*

---

### Knightly Orders

*See also: [[Bannerhall]], [[Ordained Knights of Heironeous]]*

* **3, 12, 20** — Quoted before battle to harden resolve.
* **7 & 18** — Central to oath-taking; memorized before dubbing.
* **14 & 24** — Used to condemn dishonorable victories and boasting.
* **27 & 36** — Define mentorship and brotherhood among knights.

---

### The Common Folk

* **Analect 1** — “May a knight with a dented shield come down our road.”
* **Analect 9** — “Show mercy, or you’ll be a tyrant.”
* **Analect 14** — Mockery of pompous nobles: *“Hear that, Sir Peacock?”*
* **Analect 20** — “A man who runs from a wolf leaves his flock to die.”
* **Analect 32** — “We kneel only in prayer.” (used against corrupt authority)
* **Analect 39** — “Sharpen your scythe before the sun.”

## Selected Analects with Commentary

### Analect 1

**Shield the helpless, for in their survival lies your honor.**

* **Priests:** Protect the vulnerable—widows, orphans, the poor.
* **Knights:** Guarding a peasant’s home may be holier than war.
* **Common:** “Better a dented shield than a polished helm.”

---

### Analect 9

**Let mercy temper your hand; a tyrant knows only cruelty.**

* **Priests:** Offer repentance even to the guilty.
* **Knights:** Spare honorable foes.
* **Common:** “A man who spares none will find none to spare him.”

---

### Analect 14

**Glory is nothing if bought with dishonor.**

* **Priests:** Empty victories are worthless.
* **Knights:** Condemn rigged tourneys and false valor.
* **Common:** Tavern mockery standard issue.

---

### Analect 20

**Cowardice kills more surely than any spear.**

* **Priests:** Fear is evil’s weapon.
* **Knights:** Pre-charge mantra.
* **Common:** “Run from the wolf, lose the flock.”

---

### Analect 23

**Beware pride: it whispers with Hextor’s tongue.**

* **Priests:** Warning against spiritual arrogance.
* **Knights:** Overconfidence invites defeat.
* **Common:** “Loud cocks end up in the pot.”

---

### Analect 27

**Embolden the way for others, that no man march alone.**

* **Priests:** Stand with the faithful in hardship.
* **Knights:** Veterans must train the next generation.
* **Common:** “Don’t leave a neighbor to rebuild alone.”

---

### Analect 29

**Justice is not vengeance, though both burn hot.**

* **Priests:** Courts must be measured.
* **Knights:** Duels may be honor—vendettas are not.
* **Common:** Quiet warning in feuds.

---

### Analect 32

**To kneel only in prayer is to bow to none but heaven.**

* **Priests:** Defiance of tyranny.
* **Knights:** Justifies refusal of unjust commands.
* **Common:** Anti-corruption rally cry.

---

### Analect 34

**Never die easy; strive always for a valorous end.**

* **Priests:** Martyrdom in defense of the innocent.
* **Knights:** Battle-cry and epitaph alike.
* **Common:** “Heironean mules don’t die easy.”

---

### Analect 36

**A shield raised for another is the truest sign of faith.**

* **Priests:** Faith requires action.
* **Knights:** Core of chivalry.
* **Common:** Highest praise for a neighbor.

---

### Analect 39

**Let each dawn find you ready to defend what is just.**

* **Priests:** Morning liturgy.
* **Knights:** Muster call.
* **Common:** “Sharpen your scythe before the sun.”
