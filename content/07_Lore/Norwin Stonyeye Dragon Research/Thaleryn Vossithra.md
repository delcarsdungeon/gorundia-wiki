# **The Root Dirge of the Third Wyrm**

_Recovered fragment — Wood Elf funerary tradition_

> _“Sing it low. She still hears the roots.”_  
> — margin note, unknown hand

---

## **Translated Text (Trade Common)**

_(Original in Low Sylvan; structure preserved where possible)_

---

We do not burn this one.  
We do not leave her for the crows.

We press her down.  
We ask the earth to remember her weight.

---

**Thaleryn Vossithra.**

Not the name she took in the high places.  
Not the name she wore in the wind.

The name given  
when the first leaves knew her shadow.

---

She drank the deep green.  
She learned the slow hunger.

Not fang.  
Not flame.

Root.

---

Where she passed:

Water turned inward.  
Beasts forgot their young.  
The trees leaned away from themselves.

---

We cut her paths.  
We burned her breath.

It was not enough.

---

She fell from the high places.

Not driven.  
Not hunted.

**Broken.**

---

Her wings did not carry her again.

The sky refused her.

---

She went to ground.

Not to hide.  
To **remain**.

---

She learned the dark roots.

She learned the slow ways.

She moved where the earth is thickest.

---

Not above us.

**Through us.**

---

We drove her where the roots run oldest.

Where the first tree drinks the dark.

---

She fought there.

Not to escape—

To **keep her place**.

---

We could not follow.

Not far.  
Not deep.

---

So we did what we could.

We closed the ways.

We watched the ground.

We marked the trees that should not move.

---

We do not say she is dead.

We say:

> _She listens where the roots are thickest._

---

Do not dig.

Do not follow broken earth.

Do not trust still water.

---

If the ground shifts without wind—

Leave.

---

## **Norwin’s Marginal Notes (Revised)**

> _“…there it is.”_

---

### **Name Extracted**

- **Thaleryn Vossithra**  
    → Likely pre-corruption or early designation  
    → Not necessarily current identity
    

---

### **Critical Distinction from Vharak-Thul**

- This dragon:
    
    - **Does not dominate from above**        
    - **Does not rely on aerial control**        

Instead:

> It operates **subterranean-first**

---

### **Key Discovery**

> _“Her wings did not carry her again.”_

Interpretation:

- **Permanent wing damage**

- Loss of:
    
    - aerial dominance
    - traditional draconic mobility

---
### **Behavioral Adaptation**

Instead of retreating:

> She changed domains.

- From:
    
    - sky / canopy predator
      
- To:
    
    - **subsurface infiltrator**


---

### **New Threat Model (Partial)**

- Movement:
    
    - Underground
    - Root networks        
    - Soil displacement
    
- Presence indicators:
    
    - Ground shift without cause
    - Root movement        
    - Disturbed water tables        

---

### **Most Important Line**

> _“Not above us. Through us.”_

---

### **Containment (Revised Understanding)**

No evidence of:

- Ritual binding    
- Druidic suppression (in this context)    

Instead:

> **Geographic restriction**

- Driven into:
    
    - deep-root zones        
    - ancient tree systems        
- Followed by:
    
    - monitoring        
    - avoidance        
    - territorial marking        

---

### **Conclusion**

> This dragon was not defeated.

> It was **forced into a domain where pursuit becomes impractical**.

---

### **Secondary Implication**

If she remains:

- She is:
    
    - alive        
    - adapted        
    - operating on a **different axis than expected**        

---

### **Final Note**

> _“We are very used to looking up for dragons.”_
> 
> _“This one ensures that is a mistake.”_

---

## **Use in Reconstruction**

This changes the Sister from:

- “contained entity”


to:

> **territorial subterranean apex predator with known injury adaptation**
