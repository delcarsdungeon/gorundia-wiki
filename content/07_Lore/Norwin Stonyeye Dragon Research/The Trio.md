# **The Green Triad**

*Zara’s Counterparts*

---

> *“This is where people start trying to make it cleaner than it was.”*
> *“It wasn’t clean. It barely makes sense now.”*
> — Norwin Stonyeye

---

## **Overview**

Multiple independent sources confirm a **three-dragon conflict** involving:

* [[Zarathryss Vaelorath]] (bronze)
* [[Vharak-Thul]] (green, slain)
* [[Thaleryn Vossithra]] (green, extant)

The event resulted in:

* catastrophic loss of life (elves, knights, grugach)
* permanent injury to [[Theron Sylvanranth]]
* long-term destabilization of the region

---

## **Established Outcomes**

Each dragon reached a **different end state**:

| Dragon                   | Type   | Outcome                     |
| ------------------------ | ------ | --------------------------- |
| [[Vharak-Thul]]          | Green  | Slain                       |
| [[Thaleryn Vossithra]]   | Green  | Driven below, persists      |
| [[Zarathryss Vaelorath]] | Bronze | Survived, later compromised |

---

## **Interaction Model (Minimal Interpretation)**

The three dragons were:

> **Co-present in the same conflict**
> **Acting within the same operational space**

Beyond that, evidence becomes inconsistent.

---

### **Observed Functional Differences**

From compiled records:

* [[Vharak-Thul]]
  → environmental corruption and expansion

* [[Thaleryn Vossithra]]
  → persistence within territory (post-injury adaptation)

* [[Zarathryss Vaelorath]]
  → mobility, decision-making, and later political activity

---

> These differences are **observed**, not assigned roles.

---

## **On the Term “Sister”**

The term appears in:

* the dying words of [[Vharak-Thul]]
* later associative references tied to [[Thaleryn Vossithra]]

---

### **Problem**

* Vharak-Thul is identified as **male**
* Zara is a **bronze dragon**, not green
* Thaleryn is the only confirmed **green counterpart remaining**

---

### **Norwin’s Position**

> The term *“sister”* should **not** be taken as literal familial designation.

Most likely explanations:

* **Draconic linguistic convention**

  * indicating:

    * same kind
    * same grouping
    * same origin cluster

* **Translation artifact**

  * Low Grugach / Elven → Trade Common drift

---

### **Rejected Interpretations**

* Direct biological sibling relationship
* Strict brood-based lineage inference

---

> *“If dragons used kinship terms the way we do, we would have better records.”*

---

## **Relationship Between the Three**

Evidence supports:

* **Proximity** (same region, same event)
* **Temporal overlap** (same engagement window)
* **Mutual awareness** (confirmed via dialogue reference)

---

Evidence does **not** support:

* Unified command structure
* Stable alliance
* Shared objective beyond presence

---

### **Norwin’s Working Position**

> They were not random.

> That is as far as the evidence allows.

---

## **On the Absence of Record**

The conflict is:

* referenced
* fragmented
* partially preserved

But never fully described in a single coherent source.

---

### **Observation**

> The larger the event, the worse the documentation.

---

### **Norwin’s Interpretation (Cautious)**

Either:

1. Records were:

   * lost
   * fragmented
   * culturally reshaped

or

2. Records were:

> **deliberately simplified to avoid describing the full event**

---

## **Relevance**

The Triad represents:

* The only confirmed case of:

  * **multiple high-tier dragons operating in the same theater**

* A precedent for:

  * **non-uniform outcomes**
  * **partial resolution rather than victory**

---

## **Operational Implication**

When dealing with dragons:

> There is no guarantee of a consistent result.
