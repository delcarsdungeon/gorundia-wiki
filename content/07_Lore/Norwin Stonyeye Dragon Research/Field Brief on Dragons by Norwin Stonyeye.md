# **Norwin’s Field Brief — Dragons of Gorundia**

> *“If you only remember one thing—*
> *stop thinking of dragons as problems you solve.”*
>
> *“They’re problems you survive… or inherit.”*

---

## **What We Know (No Debate)**

* There have been **multiple active dragons** in this region within living or near-living memory

* A confirmed **multi-dragon conflict** occurred involving:

  * elves
  * knights
  * grugach

* That conflict resulted in:

  * One dragon **slain** → [[Vharak-Thul]]
  * One dragon **driven underground** → [[Thaleryn Vossithra]]
  * One dragon **escaping and later changing** → [[Zarathryss Vaelorath]]

---

* A separate red dragon:

  * [[Ashmaw of the Red Hills]]
  * was **killed by direct force**
  * at catastrophic but contained cost

---

* A silver dragon:

  * [[Elarithanys, the Moonborne Sentinel]]
  * remains **stable and integrated**
  * does not behave like any other known dragon

---

## **What We Are Actually Looking At**

Dragons in this region fall into four functional categories:

| Type           | Behavior                             |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| **Dominator**  | Burns and claims (Ashmaw)            |
| **Corruptor**  | Poisons and collapses (Vharak-Thul)  |
| **Entrenched** | Adapts and persists (Thaleryn)       |
| **Integrated** | Becomes part of system (Elarithanys) |

Zara is the fifth case:

> **Compromised. Controlled. Thinking anyway.**

---

## **The Important Part (Read This Twice)**

The Triad did not end.

It changed.

* One is dead
* One is under the ground
* One is walking around pretending to be a person

---

### **Current Situation**

* [[Thaleryn Vossithra]]:

  * Beneath a sacred tree
  * Semi-dormant
  * Being **used** in a process tied to cloned goblins

* If she wakes:

> That problem becomes immediate.

---

* [[Zarathryss Vaelorath]]:

  * Bound to external forces
  * Still capable of reasoning
  * Possibly unaware of what is being done to her “sister”

* If she finds out:

> That problem becomes catastrophic.

---

## **What This Means For You**

* You are not dealing with:

  * “a dragon”

You are dealing with:

> **A broken system of dragons**

---

* Killing one does not fix it
* Ignoring one does not remove it
* Using one **creates new problems**

---

## **Operational Reality**

If things go wrong:

* You will not have time to debate
* You will not have complete information
* You will be making decisions with consequences you do not understand

---

## **Clarification (Because Apparently This Needs Saying)**

[[Theron Sylvanranth]] was:

* A dragon-slayer
* A terrifying man
* A problem in his own right

He was **not a dragon**. 

> *“Future archivists are advised not to reinterpret every competent or frightening individual as a disguised wyrm.”*
> — Norwin
