# **Account of the Green Breath**

_Recovered fragment — attributed to a surviving druid of the outer circles_

---

## **Transcribed Text**

---

The first assumption was incorrect.

It was not a gas.

---

It behaved as one.

It settled in low places.  
It followed still air.  
It lingered.

---

But it did not fade.

It took hold.

---

The second assumption was also incorrect.

It was not poison.

---

Poison ends a thing.

This did not.

---

It changed it.

---

Water held it.  
Soil held it.  
Living bodies accepted it.

---

We observed:

- breath failing without wound    
- memory loosening before death    
- motion continuing after understanding had gone    

---

This was not killing.

---

It was **conversion**.

---

## **Response**

We did not attempt to cleanse it again.

Fire spreads such things.  
Wind teaches them to travel.  
Water gives them roots.

---

So we changed our place in the cycle.

---

## **On the Fighters**

Those who stood against the creature could not endure the breath.

So we walked with them.

---

We set our hands upon them.  
Upon their lungs.  
Upon the rhythm of their breath.

---

We did not remove the corruption.

---

We **answered it**.

---

Where it sought to take hold—

we unmade that moment.

---

Not forever.

Only long enough.

---

They breathed.

They fought.

They died as themselves.

---

This was sufficient.

---

## **On the Ground**

The greater work was not with the warriors.

It was with the land.

---

We entered the places already taken.

We did not resist the corruption.

---

We **received it**.

---

Hands to soil.  
Hands to root.  
Breath shared with the earth.

---

We did not push it out.

We held it in place.

---

Where we stood:

- it did not spread    
- it did not deepen    
- it did not become more    

---

It remained.

---

This is the only victory such things allow.

---

## **On Cost**

The young believed this could be endured.

They were wrong.

---

The body yields first.

Then the self loosens.

Then the will becomes quiet.

---

There is no pain in the end.

Only distance.

---

Several spoke before they left.

---

> “It is quieter here.”

---

This is consistent with dissolution into the greater cycle.

---

We did not call them back.

---

## **On Purpose**

We are not set apart from the world.

We are not above it.

---

We are what stands **between**.

---

Between growth and rot.  
Between life and unmaking.  
Between what is… and what should not be.

---

The creature did not seek to destroy the forest.

---

It sought to **change the rule by which the forest exists**.

---

That cannot be allowed.

---

## **Outcome**

The lines held.

The spread slowed.

The hunters were given time.

---

The creature was slain.

---

The change was not completed.

---

This is sufficient.

---

## **Closing**

We did not cleanse it.

We did not defeat it.

---

We ensured:

> it did not become everything.

---

## **Norwin’s Marginal Notes**

> _“…they call that sufficient.”_

---

### **Key Additions (Compared to Other Accounts)**

- Confirms dual role of druids:
    
    1. **Field counteraction**        
        - actively resisting the breath within fighters            
        - preserving function, identity, and agency            
    2. **Environmental anchoring**       
        - halting spread at the source            
        - accepting contamination to prevent escalation            

---

### **Important Distinction**

- They did not:
    
    - cure
    - cleanse        
    - reverse        

They:

> **bought time at the cost of themselves**

---

### **Most Important Line**

> _“We changed our place in the cycle.”_

---

### **Implication**

Druids do not see this as sacrifice.

They see it as:

> **correct positioning**

---

### **Final Note**

> _The Grugach say they “held the rot.”_
> 
> The druids say they “answered it.”
> 
> Both are correct.
> 
> Only one of those groups expected to survive.
