## The Knife Table

*Wine half-drunk. Papers spread across a scarred oak table. Lamplight curls through smoke.*

*Four figures sit together, all smiles edged with knives.*

---

**[Kursk]:**  
“Your knights think they’ve bloodied me. They’ve only stirred the forest. The girl still carries my shadow in her blood. When she breaks, half their hearts will break with her. I’ll be there to hear it snap.”

---

**[Marco Galibano]:**  
*(smiles, swirling his cup)*  
“Hearts don’t pay tariffs, Kursk. Coin does. Bard’s Gate bleeds coin now that your dwarf riled the mob. We withdraw, yes — but to return stronger. Fear is a market, and business thrives on demand.”

*His eyes linger on Vogel a moment: the “duke” plays at kingship, but Marco knows coins crown kings faster than swords.*

---

**[Zara Vallytrex]:**  
*(leaning forward, voice silk and venom)*  
“Malik is dead, and I know who felled him. **Thranduil**. That grugach cur loosed the arrow and laughed while my world burned. Until he dies, nothing is balanced. Do not prattle to me of tariffs or shadows when blood is owed.”

---

**[Wilhelm Vogel]:**  
*(sits taller, voice level, full of self-importance)*  
“Balance? Stories for peasants. The lords crave certainty, and they will not abide Beringer for long. He is a child in borrowed armor. The crown bends only one way — to strength. To me. The rest is noise.”

*His smile flickers — he doesn’t even consider that Zara or Marco might think themselves his equal.*

---

**[Kursk]:**  
“Beringer is prey. Strong prey, yes, but prey. Thranduil is wolf. I would hunt him myself.”

*He spits the word “wolf” with admiration, knowing Vogel fears what cannot be bought.*

---

**[Marco Galibano]:**  
“Hunt him if you must. Wolves fighting wolves is good business — provided cages are waiting for the survivors. But blood in the streets ruins ledgers, and my family keeps its books clean.”

*He smooths his sleeve, thinking: Vogel drips arrogance, Zara drips poison, Kursk drips blood — and only Marco talks sense.*

---

**[Zara Vallytrex]:**  
*(eyes narrow, emerald fire burning)*  
“No, Kursk. Thranduil is not wolf — he is quarry. My quarry. The dwarf, the baroness, the rest were accomplices. But it was his arrow. And I will answer it. Arrow for arrow.”

*She smiles faintly, watching Vogel’s jaw tighten — she knows he thinks her “secondary.” Let him. He never sees the knife until it’s in his back.*

---

**[Wilhelm Vogel]:**  
*(waves a dismissive hand)*  
“Petty vendettas. Thranduil, Orin, Braga — all will break when pressed. Obstacles. Nothing more. The lords will come crawling to me because they must. There is no other end to this story.”

*The hubris is naked. Even Marco raises a brow at it. Zara hides a smirk.*

---

**[Kursk]:**  
“And the others? The gnome, the tinkerer, the halfling capo? Chaff. Burn them, and their friends howl. Howling makes them reckless.”

---

**[Marco Galibano]:**  
*(smooth as polished marble)*  
“Howling men pay dearly. The capo can be bought. Better bought than broken — honor matters in business. A contract is a contract. Even the mob understands that.”

*A pointed glance at Vogel: his pacts are blood and ash — Marco prefers clean ink and coin.*

---

**[Zara Vallytrex]:**  
“They are gnats circling a torch. The torch is Vogel’s throne, or so he insists. My vengeance burns brighter. I am patient. My fire waits.”

*She watches Vogel preen, thinking: he sees only the crown — never the knife. That is why he will always be predictable.*

---

**[Wilhelm Vogel]:**  
*(leaning forward, voice iron)*  
“No. Every gnat carries plague. Thalrik already cost you coin, Marco. His mob still echoes. I will not underestimate him — nor should you.”

*He thinks he sounds measured, but his pride shows — Vogel cannot abide that a dwarf conman has bested him in anything.*

---

**[Kursk]:**  
“Then let me take the girl. [[Blue Sylvanranth|Blue]]. She is soft. Break her heart, and they all crumble.”

---

**[Marco Galibano]:**  
*(arches a brow)*  
“Rash. Riots cut profits unless carefully pruned. A riot at the right time may be… useful. But the city must not think it was my hand.”

*His honor is in business: blood is always someone else’s mess.*

---

**[Zara Vallytrex]:**  
*(smiles thinly, venom in her tone)*  
“Forget the girl. Forget the city. The grugach dies first. His death is my ledger. After that, you may play with hearts and coins as you wish.”

*Her gaze lingers on [[Wilhelm Vogel]], daring him to tell her “no.”*

---

**[Wilhelm Vogel]:**  
*(final, dismissive)*  
“The crown bends to strength. [[Beringer]]’s luster will fade. The lords will turn. And when they do, they will find no one left but me. Until then, silence your vendettas. They serve only when I say so.”

*Inwardly, he already imagines the throne beneath him. To Vogel, the others are pawns — Zara a useful mask, Marco a purse, Kursk a beast on a leash. Hubris blinds him to the knives at his side.*

---

### Private Thoughts

*Unspoken reflections linger beneath the conversation.*

**Zara:**  
*He thinks himself inevitable. That arrogance will be his undoing. He is the blunt blade — I am the edge that cuts.*

**Marco:**  
*Arrogance, vengeance, savagery. I alone speak for honor and coin. When their games collapse, the family will remain. It always does.*

**Kursk:**  
*They circle thrones and ledgers. I smell only blood. And I will have it, crown or no crown.*

**Vogel:**  
*They scheme and whisper. But when the lords kneel, they will kneel to me. All of them. They just don’t know it yet.*
