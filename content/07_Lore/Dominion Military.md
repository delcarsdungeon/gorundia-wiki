# Dominion Military

The **Dominion Military** is the centralized war host of the [[Dominion]], combining feudal levies, professional soldiers, religious enforcers, and large formations of coerced troops.

While noble houses still provide commanders and cavalry, the army itself is heavily centralized and ideologically controlled by the Church of [[Hextor]]. Military authority and religious authority operate side by side, with priest-officers embedded throughout the command structure.

To neighboring realms, the Dominion’s armies are infamous for their rigid discipline, harsh punishments, and relentless battlefield tactics.

---

# Cultural Character

Dominion military culture has evolved into a harsh northern imperial tradition shaped by endless frontier wars and Hextor’s doctrine of domination.

The army emphasizes:

- rigid hierarchy  
- harsh discipline  
- ritualized military ceremony  
- ideological loyalty enforced by clergy  
- overwhelming force against enemies

Dominion officers speak in clipped, direct commands, and their culture prizes strength, obedience, and endurance above all else.

---

# Command Structure

## High Marshal of the Dominion

The supreme military commander appointed by the Dominion’s ruler.

The High Marshal directs strategy for all field armies and commands the Dominion’s wars of conquest.

---

## Boyar-General

Senior noble commanders responsible for major fronts or entire regions of warfare.

Boyar-Generals frequently control multiple armies and act as the Dominion’s military governors during occupations.

---

## Voivode

Field commanders responsible for leading individual Dominion armies.

A Voivode typically commands between **5,000 and 20,000 soldiers**, depending on the campaign.

---

## Banner Captain

Commander of a military **banner**, the fundamental organizational unit of the Dominion army.

A banner typically contains several hundred soldiers organized into smaller companies.

---

## Centurion

Officer responsible for leading roughly one hundred soldiers.

Centurions handle battlefield discipline and tactical execution.

---

# Political Oversight

## Scourge Commissar

Clerical officers assigned by the Church of [[Hextor]] to Dominion military formations.

Their duties include:

- enforcing ideological loyalty  
- punishing cowardice or dissent  
- executing deserters  
- reporting disloyal officers

Scourge Commissars possess authority to override military officers when matters of doctrine are involved.

Most are drawn from the ranks of **War Canons** or **Iron Prelates** within the [[Iron Hierarchy of Hextor]].

Their presence ensures the Dominion army serves both the state and the Iron Tyrant.

---

# Elite Forces

## Iron Guard

Heavy infantry sworn directly to the Dominion’s ruler.

These soldiers receive the best equipment available and are deployed as decisive assault troops in critical battles.

---

## Tyrant Knights

Fanatical paladins and mounted warriors devoted to [[Hextor]].

They serve as shock cavalry and religious enforcers within the army.

---

# Standard Forces

## Dominion Legionaries

Professional soldiers trained in disciplined formation warfare.

They fight primarily as heavy infantry supported by crossbows and cavalry.

---

## Frontier Lancers

Light cavalry recruited from the Dominion’s borderlands.

They specialize in scouting, raiding, and rapid pursuit.

---

# Slave Legions

Slavery is a central pillar of Dominion society and warfare. The state and the Church of [[Hextor]] maintain vast slave populations drawn from conquered peoples, criminals, debtors, and political prisoners.

Among these slaves, **half-orcs are particularly valued** by Dominion warlords.

Dominion doctrine holds that half-orcs possess the strength and endurance necessary for brutal military service while remaining easy to control through fear and deprivation.

As a result, many half-orcs are captured, traded, or bred specifically for military use.

Half-orc slaves commonly serve as:

- **Chainbound Soldiers** in penal formations  
- shock troops for dangerous assaults  
- forced labor in siege operations  
- expendable garrison troops in hostile territories

Dominion officers often refer to these formations as **“war cattle.”**

Because of this practice, a large portion of the Dominion’s expendable infantry is drawn from enslaved half-orc populations driven into battle under the watch of **Scourge Commissars**.

---

# Conscripts and Penal Units

## Chainbound Soldiers

Indentured soldiers forced into service through debt, punishment, or enslavement.

Many Chainbound Soldiers are drawn from the Dominion’s slave populations, particularly half-orcs.

They are frequently deployed in the most dangerous portions of a battle.

---

## Shackled Cohorts

Penal units composed of criminals, political prisoners, and rebellious slaves.

These formations are used for:

- siege assaults  
- suicidal attacks  
- dangerous labor operations

Survival rates are extremely low.

---

# Relationship with the Church

The Dominion army and the Church of [[Hextor]] function as parallel hierarchies.

Military commanders control battlefield operations, while Hextorite clergy enforce ideological obedience and loyalty to the Iron Tyrant.

This dual command structure creates constant tension between generals and priest-officers, though open defiance of the church is rare.

---

# Reputation

Across neighboring kingdoms, Dominion armies are known for:

- relentless battlefield discipline  
- brutal treatment of prisoners  
- heavy use of slave soldiers  
- ideological enforcement by Hextorite clergy

Enemies often describe Dominion troops as grim, disciplined, and utterly merciless once battle begins.
